## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *teudrą

### Etymology

Uncertain. Probably from Proto-Indo-European *dewk- (“to pull”) or *dewh₂- (“to tie to, secure”) suffixed with *-tróm or *-dʰrom, but Kroonen prefers an alternative relationship to *deh₁-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dewk-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈteu̯.ðrɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ that which is tugged or pulled on, tether
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ a drawbar; shaft
3. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ a cord; rope; line

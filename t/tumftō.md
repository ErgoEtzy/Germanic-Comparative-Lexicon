## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *tumftō

### Etymology

From a compound of Proto-Indo-European *dm̥- (“to build”) and *ped- (“to walk, to step”), assuming a relationship with at least Ancient Greek δάπεδον (dápedon, “floor”). However, this word may simply be equivalent to *tumþiz ~ *tumþō; see there.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dem-, *dm̥-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈtum.ɸtɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, North, feminine, reconstruction)_ ground around a building

# -tēhundą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/-t%C4%93hund%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -tēhundą

### Etymology

Of disputed origin:
* Traditionally seen as a vrddhi derivative of *tehun (“ten”) or a related form, but this view is now commonly rejected as extra-Germanic evidence for such a formation is lacking.
* Following Szemerényi, now most commonly seen as extended from *sebuntēhundą, viewed as a remodelling of earlier *seftunēhundą under the influence of *sebunt (“seven”), itself derived from earlier *seftunhundą remodeled after *fimfēhundą (“50”) and *sehsēhundą (“60”). *sehsēhundą (“60”) is in turn posited to be remodelled after the former from *sehskundą, with unusual *-kundą for *-hundą. The ultimate source of these forms is Proto-Indo-European *pénkʷedḱomt (“50”), *s(w)éḱsdḱomt (“60”), *septḿ̥dḱomt (“70”), with final *-gan~*-han replaced with *-hundą on the model of *tehun, lost *wīhundi (“20”), and *hundą (“100”).
:The variant *-tōhundą (posited as the source of Proto-West Germanic *-tō) was instead extended from *ahtōhundą (“80”) to the adjacent *seftunhundą (“70”) and *newunhundą (“90”), after which *ahtōhundą was remodelled to the more transparent *ahtōutōhundą.
* Another view posits a combination of a putative suffix *-tê/*-tô and *hundą (“100”); Proto-West Germanic *-tō would therefore represent the unextended suffix. The posited derivation and function of this suffix varies, though it is often suggested to be some sort of genitive plural: Shields and Voyles posit a former instrumental suffix from the pleonastic combination of *-t and *-ê/*-ô, while relation to the ordinal ending *-dô or *tō (“to”) has also been suggested.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *pénkʷedḱomt

1. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Forms the higher decads: *sebuntēhundą (“70”), *ahtōutēhundą (“80”), *newuntēhundą (“90”), and *tehuntēhundą (“100”)

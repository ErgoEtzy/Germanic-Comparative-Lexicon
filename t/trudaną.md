[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/trudan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *trudaną

### Etymology

Probably originally a root aorist, present singular *tred-, non-singular *trud-, from a possible Pre-Germanic *dret-, which could theoretically be an extension of Proto-Indo-European *dr- (“to run”), compare *drem- (“to run, sleep”) and *dreh₂- (“to run”) (the latter whence Sanskrit द्रा (drā, “to run, make haste”)). Akin to Proto-Slavic *dьrtěti (“to degrade, to age”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dr-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈtru.ðɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to tread, to step on

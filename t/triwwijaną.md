# *triwwijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/triwwijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *triwwijaną

### Etymology

From *triwwiz (“true, faithful”) + *-janą and *trewwō (“fidelity, pledge”) + *-janą.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-ye-ti, *-yeti, *-yé-ti, *-yéti, *-éh₁-ti, *-éh₁ti, *-éye-ti, *-éyeti, *drew-, *drewH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈtriw.wi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction, transitive)_ to make faithful
2. _(reconstruction, transitive)_ to ally, form a relation with
3. _(reconstruction, transitive)_ to trust, have faith in

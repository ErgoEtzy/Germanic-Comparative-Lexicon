## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *tīwaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *deywós (“god”). Possibly attested as 𐌕𐌄𐌉𐌅𐌀 (teiva) on the Negau helmet.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *deywós, *dyew-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈtiː.wɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ deity, god
2. _(masculine, reconstruction, as a proper noun)_ Tyr, the Germanic god of war. Identified in later times with the Roman god Mars.
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ name of the rune ᛏ (t)

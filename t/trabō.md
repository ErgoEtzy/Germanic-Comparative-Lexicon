## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *trabō

### Etymology

Possibly related to Proto-Slavic *drapati, Sanskrit द्रापि (drāpi, “garment, mantle”), and Ancient Greek δρέπω (drépō, “to pluck, tear off”). Also compare Old Norse trefja (“to buzz”) and trefill (“rag, tatter”). Note that a relation with Proto-Slavic *drabъ (“clothes”) is phonetically impossible by regular application of sound laws.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ fringe (decorative border)

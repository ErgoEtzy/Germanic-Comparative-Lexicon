# *tawjaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/tawjan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *tawjaną

### Etymology

Factitive derived from an adjective *tawaz, from Pre-Germanic *dowh₂os, from Proto-Indo-European *dewh₂- (“to fit”). Possibly attested as *gatawaz, borrowed into Proto-Slavic *gotovъ, though this is disputed.
Cognate with Tocharian B tsuwa and Ancient Greek δύναμαι (dúnamai).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dewh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈtɑu̯.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to make, complete

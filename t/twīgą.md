# *twīgą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/tw%C4%ABg%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *twīgą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *dwigʰom (“split in two, branch”), which Orel derives from *dwóh₁ (“two”), presumably based on the two-forked shape of twigs, though Kroonen adduces no cognates outside of Germanic and leaves the origin open. Compare Old Church Slavonic двигъ (dvigŭ, “branch”), Albanian degë (“branch”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dwigʰom

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈtwiː.ɣɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ branch
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ twig

# *teguz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/teguz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *teguz

### Etymology

Reanalyzed u-stem from consonant stem *tegun(dz), from Proto-Indo-European *déḱm̥-t(-s) ~ *dḱm̥-t-és (“group of ten, decad”). Alternatively, inherited from Proto-Indo-European *déḱ-ú-s ~ *dḱ-éw-s, related to Latin decuria (“group of ten men”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *déḱ-ú-s ~ *dḱ-éw-s, *déḱm̥-t(-s) ~ *dḱm̥-t-és

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈte.ɣuz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ group of ten, decad
2. _(in-plural, masculine, reconstruction)_ -ty

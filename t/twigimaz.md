# *twigimaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/twigimaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *twigimaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *dwiǵʰimos (“two winters (old)”), from *dwi- (“two”) + a form of *ǵʰéyōm (“winter”). Cognate with Latin bīmus (“two years old”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dwiǵʰimos

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈtwi.ˌɣi.mɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, West, reconstruction)_ two years old

# *lingwą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/lingw%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lingwą

### Etymology

Perhaps from Pre-Proto-Germanic *lengʷʰom, from Proto-Indo-European *lengʷʰ-. Compare Proto-Slavic *lǫgъ (“meadow”) from Proto-Balto-Slavic *lanˀgas. The original meaning may have been *“unused land”; Kroonen compares the semantic development of *haiþī (“heath”). The same semantic pairing exists in modern Irish fraoch.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *lengʷʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈliŋʷ.ɡʷɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, North, neuter, reconstruction)_ heather, ling

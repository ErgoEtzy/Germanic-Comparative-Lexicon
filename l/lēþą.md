# *lēþą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/l%C4%93%C3%BE%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lēþą

### Etymology

Disputed. Suggested as cognate with Ancient Greek λάτρον (látron, “payment”) and Latin latrō (“mercenary”), from Proto-Indo-European *leh₂t- (“to grant; to possess”), but deriving the Germanic *ē from this root requires a lengthened grade with application of Eichner's law, both of which are nowadays controversial. Perhaps alternatively from *leh₁-t-, extended from *leh₁- (“to let”) (whence also Proto-Germanic *lētaną, from a different extension *leh₁-d-).
Beekes rejects an Indo-European etymology entirely and considers the purported Greek cognate to be from Pre-Greek, whereas the Latin is borrowed from Greek.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *leh₂t-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈlɛː.θɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ possession, ownership

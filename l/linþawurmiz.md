# *linþawurmiz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/lin%C3%BEawurmiz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *linþawurmiz

### Etymology

From *linþaz (“flexible, flimsy”) + *wurmiz (“worm, serpent”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *lent-, *lént-o-s, *wr̥mis

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈlin.θɑˌwur.miz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ serpent, snake
2. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ dragon (mythical large serpentine creature)

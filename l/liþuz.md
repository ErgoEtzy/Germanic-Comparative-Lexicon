# *liþuz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/li%C3%BEuz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *liþuz

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin; Kroonen considers the word as cognate with *limuz (“limb”). Other theories include derivation from Proto-Indo-European *h₂el-, *leyH- (“to bend”). A relationship with Latin lituus is sometimes suggested, but is doubtful as it is likely a loanword from Etruscan. Given the variety of forms, which are difficult to reconcile as inherited, origin in a substrate language is possible.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂el-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈli.θuz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a bend
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a joint
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a body part, limb, member

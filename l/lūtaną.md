# *lūtaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/l%C5%ABtan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lūtaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *lewd- (“to duck, crouch, feign, cringe, become small”), a root whose purported descendants are uncertain, but which may include Lithuanian liū̃dnas (“sad”), liū̃sti (“to become sad”), and Welsh lludded (“tiredness”). By synchronic analysis, Kroonen takes the word as a back-formation from the iterative *luttōną (“to stoop, droop”) (apparently from the same root), and tentatively compares Old Irish lott (“damage, destruction”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *lewd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈluː.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to bow down, lout

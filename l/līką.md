## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *līką

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *leyg- (“image, likeness; similar, like”), with semantic shift “similar” > “having a similar shape” > “likeness” > “body”. Cognate with Lithuanian lýgus (“equal, level, flat, even, like”). Possibly cognate with Persian لاش (“corpse”) (whence via Turkish Bulgarian леш (leš), Serbo-Croatian леш), which would show a parallel semantic development.

Probably Proto-Indo-European *leyǵ- (“to bind”), and cognate with Latin ligō (“to bind”). Kroonen, in addition to the above theory, offers an alternative suggestion that the word is a semantic extension of the "body" sense above, with semantic association "to connect" > "to compare" > "to be alike".

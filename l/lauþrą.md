## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lauþrą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *lówh₃trom (“that which is used for washing”), from Proto-Indo-European *lewh₃- (“to wash, bathe”) + *-trom (equivalent to *-þrą). Cognate with Ancient Greek λουτρόν (loutrón, “a bath, wash-room”), Old Irish lóathar (“wash-basin”). Kroonen attributes the Germanic o-grade to a different reconstruction of the root as *leh₃-, extended as *lh₃-éw-, based on Hittite 𒆷𒀀𒄷𒄿 (la-a-ḫu-i, “to pour”), though this is unlikely and there are other possible explanations.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *lewh₃-, *lówh₃trom

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈlɑu̯.θrɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ nitre used as soap, soap
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ foam, lather

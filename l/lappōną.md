## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lappōną

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *lap-néh₂- (“to lick”). The root was probably onomatopoeic and might have come about after the PIE period proper. Related to Latin lambō (“to lick”), Lithuanian lapènti (“to drink greedily (of pigs)”), Ancient Greek λάπτω (láptō, “idem”), Albanian lap (“to lick or lap up water”), Russian ло́пать (lópatʹ, “to gobble up”).

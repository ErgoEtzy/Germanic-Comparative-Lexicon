## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lubją

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *lubʰ-yo-, from *lewbʰ- (“herb”), and cognate with Proto-Celtic *lubī (“herb, plant”). The root is likely equivalent to *lewbʰ- in the "peel, break, damage" sense (likely whence Proto-Germanic *laubą (“leaf”)), which itself has been further derived from a Proto-Indo-European *lew- (“to cut, remove, prune, separate”); the semantic development would be something like "peeled-off plant matter" > "herb" > "potion (made from herbs)".
The sense of love potion is influenced by similar forms from the unrelated *lubō (“love”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *lew-, *lubʰ-yo-

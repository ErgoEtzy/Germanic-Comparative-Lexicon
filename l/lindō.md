# *lindō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/lind%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lindō

### Etymology

Assumed to stem from *linþaz (“bendsome; flexible”), via being the tree which gives weaving fibre (bast), comparatively also *līnǭ (“line”) to *līną (“flax”). Also compare Old Norse lindr (“linden”), which should stem from a *linþaz noun.
Has also been compared to Albanian lëndë (“wood, material”), Lithuanian lentà (“(linden) board”), Russian лут (lut, “(linden) bast”) (< Proto-Slavic *lǫtъ), thereby perhaps from a Proto-Indo-European *lentéh₂.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *lentéh₂

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈlin.dɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ linden tree, the tree which gives weaving fibre material (bast)
2. _(feminine, poetic, reconstruction)_ shield (made of linden wood)

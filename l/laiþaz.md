## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *laiþaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂leytos (“abhorrence, loath”), from *h₂leyt- (“to do something hateful, abhorrent”). Cognate with Proto-Celtic *lissus (“abhorrence”) (compare Old Irish lius, liuss (“loathing, disgust”)), Ancient Greek ἀλιτρός (alitrós, “sinful, wicked”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂leyt-, *h₂leytos

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈlɑi̯.θɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ loath; loathsome; disgusting
2. _(reconstruction)_ unwilling; averse; reluctant
3. _(reconstruction)_ offensive; hostile; hateful
4. _(reconstruction)_ grievous; sweer; sad; sorry

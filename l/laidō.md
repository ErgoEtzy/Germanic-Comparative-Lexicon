## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *laidō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *loyt-éh₂-, from *leyt- (“to go, go forth, die”) (whence also *līþaną (“to go through”), see there for cognates), perhaps an extension of the root *ley- (“to be slippery, glide, streak”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *loyt-éh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈlɑi̯.ðɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a leading
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a way, course, route, direction

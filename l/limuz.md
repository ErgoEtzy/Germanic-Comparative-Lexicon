# *limuz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/limuz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *limuz

### Etymology

Probably from a Proto-Indo-European root meaning “to bend, skew”, but which root in particular is uncertain: possibilities include *h₂el- (extended as *h₂l-ey-), *Heh₃l- (extended as *(H)h₃l-ey-), and *leyH-. Perhaps cognate with Latin līmus (“askew”), but with a slightly different derivation, and/or Lithuanian liemuõ (“log, torso”) (the latter for which Kroonen reconstructs *ley- as the base Indo-European root). Also compare *liþuz (“joint, bend, limb”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈli.muz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ limb; member
2. _(masculine, reconstruction, of a tree)_ branch; twig

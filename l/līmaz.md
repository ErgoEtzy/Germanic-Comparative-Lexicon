# *līmaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/l%C4%ABmaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *līmaz

### Etymology

Within Germanic, related to *laimaz (“loam, mud, clay”), *laizą (“clay”) (whence Old Norse leir).
From Proto-Indo-European *h₂leyH-mo- (whence also līmus (“mud”)), from *h₂leyH- (“smear”); compare Sanskrit लयते (layate, “to cling”), Ancient Greek ἀλίνω (alínō, “to smear”), Latin linō (“to smear”).
Alternatively, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ley- (“sticky, slimy”), in which case it is cognate with *slīmą (“slime, mucus”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)ley-, *h₂leyH-, *h₂leyH-mo-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈliː.mɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ glue, lime

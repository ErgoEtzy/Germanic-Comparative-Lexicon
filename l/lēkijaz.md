# *lēkijaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/l%C4%93kijaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lēkijaz

### Etymology

Uncertain; possibly from *lēkiz (“healing; medicine; healer”) + *-jaz, or borrowed from Proto-Celtic *leigis (“healer”) of the same root. Perhaps also from Proto-Indo-European *leǵ- (“to collect, gather”), and thus related to Latin legō. Alternatively, from Proto-Indo-European *leg- (“to leak”), with original sense of "blood-letter (leaker)" > "leech (used in therapy)" > "doctor", though this is speculative.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂leg-, *leg-, *leǵ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈlɛː.ki.jɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ doctor, physician

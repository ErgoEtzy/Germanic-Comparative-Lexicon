## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *lundō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ln̥dʰ-(y)eh₂-, from *lendʰ- (“kidney, loin”).
The semantic shift from "kidney" > "mood" in North Germanic languages and metaphorical usage of a non-heart organ to indicate mood is observed cross-linguistically, compare White Hmong siab (“liver; mood”) or Iranian Persian جگر (ǰigar, “liver; love; dear, darling, beloved”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *lendʰ-, *ln̥dʰ-(y)eh₂-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ loin
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ kidney
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ visceral fat

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -þaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *-tos and *-tós, derived from the original *-(∅)-tós (creating verbal adjectives) which operated only on zero-grade roots. Expanded in function during pre-Germanic to derive new adjectives from verbal roots in o-grade, though the exact function in such cases is unclear. Its eventual grammaticalization to a past participle marker has parallels in other Indo-European branches, such as Latin -tus and Proto-Slavic *-tъ.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-tos

### Suffix

1. _(idiomatic, morpheme, reconstruction)_ Derives some adjectives from roots.
2. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Marker of the past participle of weak verbs.

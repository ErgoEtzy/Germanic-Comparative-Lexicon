## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -ō

### Etymology

Predominantly from Proto-Indo-European *-(o)-éh₂, later merging with *-eh₂ (of several origins; see Etymology 2).

From Proto-Indo-European *-eh₂, *-éh₂, feminine to thematic *-o-, *-ó- and collective to thematic neuter *-om, *-óm.

From Proto-Indo-European *-oh₁.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-(o)-éh₂, *-eh₂, *-oh₁, *-éh₂

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ɔː/`

### Suffix 2

1. _(feminine, form-of, morpheme, nominative, reconstruction, singular)_ nominative feminine singular of *-az (adjectival)
2. _(accusative, feminine, form-of, morpheme, neuter, nominative, plural, reconstruction)_ nominative/accusative neuter plural of *-az (adjectival)
3. _(accusative, feminine, form-of, morpheme, nominative, plural, reconstruction, vocative)_ nominative/vocative/accusative plural of *-ą (suffix of neuter a-stem nouns)

### Suffix 3

1. _(form-of, instrumental, morpheme, reconstruction, singular)_ instrumental singular of *-az (“-er”)
2. _(form-of, instrumental, morpheme, reconstruction, singular)_ instrumental singular of *-az (suffix of masculine a-stem nouns in general)
3. _(form-of, instrumental, morpheme, reconstruction, singular)_ instrumental singular of *-ą (suffix of neuter a-stem nouns)

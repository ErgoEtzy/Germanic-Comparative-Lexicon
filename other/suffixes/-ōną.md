# -ōną

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/-%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -ōną

### Etymology

From two sources, both reflecting earlier *-ōjaną:
* From Proto-Indo-European *-eh₂yéti, an innovated compound suffix from *-éh₂ (eh₂-stem noun suffix) + *-yéti (verbal suffix). These were originally formed as denominative verbs from ō-stem nouns. When attached to thematic (a-stem) nouns, the thematic vowel was also retained, but the resulting compound suffix *-eyé- became part of the first weak class instead.
* From Proto-Indo-European *-(e)h₂yéti, an innovated compound suffix from *-(e)h₂ti (factitive verb suffix) which was originally athematic, but later extended with the thematic present suffix *-yéti.
Cognates include Latin -āre (the whole first conjugation in the present), Ancient Greek -άω (-áō, contracted verb), -अयति (-ayati, causative suffix), Proto-Celtic *-āti and Proto-Balto-Slavic *-ā́ˀtei (whence the infinite Proto-Slavic *-ati, referring again to the whole conjugation).
Note that Ringe (2017) reconstructs this suffix with a trimoraic vowel, *-ôną (or, following his own conventions, *-ō̄ną). See Ringe (2017: 160).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-(e)h₂yéti, *-eh₂yéti, *-h₂ti, *-h₂yéti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ Suffix of the infinitive. Creates denominative verbs from nouns.
2. _(reconstruction)_ Suffix of the infinitive. Creates factitive verbs from adjectives.

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -ǭ

### Etymology

Partial merger of Proto-Indo-European *-ō(n) and Proto-Indo-European *-éh₂ (see also *-ō). The former originally conjugated as Proto-Indo-European amphikinetic n-stems, but because of the *ā > *ō sound change they came to coincide with the latter in the nominative singular, which lead to the suffix ablaut being lost. Why the feminine and masculine *-ō(n) sounded differently in Germanic though, despite having originally sounded the same, is an open question.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-éh₂, *-ō, *-ō(n

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ -ess. Forms agent nouns, often from the zero-grade form of the base.

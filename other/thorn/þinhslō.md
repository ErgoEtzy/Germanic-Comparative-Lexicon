# *þinhslō

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BEinhsl%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þinhslō

### Etymology

Uncertain. Seemingly from Proto-Indo-European *tengʰs(e)leh₂, derived from the root *tengʰ- (“to pull, stretch, tensen”); Kroonen however is skeptical. In any case, compare Latin tēmō (“pole, yoke-beam”) and Old Prussian teansis (“drawbar”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *tengʰs(e)leh₂

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθĩːx.slɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a drawbar, cartpole, or thill

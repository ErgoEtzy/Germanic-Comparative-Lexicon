# *þeutǭ

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BEeut%C7%AD)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þeutǭ

### Etymology

From *þeutaną (“to sound, howl, roar”), from Proto-Indo-European *tu-, *tutu- (“bird-cry, shriek”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *tu-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθeu̯.tɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ That which causes a whirring, whistling, roaring, or rushing sound
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ A pipe
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ A stream; channel

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þerbaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *terp- (“to be stiff”). Cognate with Lithuanian tir̃pti (“to stiffen”), Latin torpeō (“to be stiff”), Old Church Slavonic трупети (trupeti, “to suffer”).
Older theories derive the root from Proto-Indo-European *(s)terbʰ- (“rigid; stiff; restricted; tight”), from *(s)ter- (“stiff”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)terbʰ-, *terp-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθer.βɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ unleavened (of bread)
2. _(reconstruction)_ fresh (of liquid)

# *þrawaz

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BErawaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þrawaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *trowH-o-s, from *trewh₁- (“to endure, suffer”); see the related *þrawō (“longing, suffering”) for more.
Older theories compared the word to Proto-Indo-European *ter- (“to rub, grind”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ter-, *trowH-o-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθrɑ.wɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ stubborn, obstinate

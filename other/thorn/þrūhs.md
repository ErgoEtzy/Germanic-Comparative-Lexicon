# *þrūhs

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BEr%C5%ABhs)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þrūhs

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *terh₃u- (“to rub, turn, drill, bore”).
Whether the word for “fetter, snare” is the same etymon is uncertain. However, it can plausibly be derived through the use for “stocks” (which is attested in Old High German).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *terh₃u-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθruːxs/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a hollow (wooden) object, such as a chute, pipe, trough, coffin
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ fetter, snare

# *þwerhaz

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BEwerhaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þwerhaz

### Etymology

Presumably from Proto-Indo-European *terkʷ- (“to turn”); compare Sanskrit तर्कु (tarku, “spindle”) and Latin torqueō (“to twist”). Kroonen assumes metathesis of Pre-Germanic *térkʷos to *twérkos.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *terkʷ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθwer.xɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ cross, adverse

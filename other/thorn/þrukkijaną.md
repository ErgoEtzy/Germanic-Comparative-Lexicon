# *þrukkijaną

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BErukkijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þrukkijaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *trewk- (“to press on and break”); cognates outside of Germanic include Welsh trychu (“to cut”), Lithuanian trū́kti (“to tear into pieces, burst”), trùkti (“to last, continue”). Other theories derive the root from Proto-Indo-European *terh₁- (“to rub, bore, twist”).
Has been suggested as the origin of Vulgar Latin *trūdicāre, but this appears unlikely.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *terh₁-, *trewk-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθruk.ki.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to press on
2. _(reconstruction)_ to crowd

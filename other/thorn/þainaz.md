## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þainaz

### Etymology

Either:
* from Proto-Indo-European *teh₂-y- (“to melt, flow”).
* back-formed from the root *þīn- (“to melt; to become wet”), found in *þīnaną (“to become moist”) and related to *þīdaz (“moistened; thawed”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *teh₂-, *teh₂-y-, *teyh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθɑi̯.nɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, West, reconstruction)_ wet; damp; moist

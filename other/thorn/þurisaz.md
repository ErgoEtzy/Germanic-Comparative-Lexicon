## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þurisaz

### Etymology

Derived from *þurēnan, which is etymologically connected to Vedic Sanskrit तुर (tura, “speedy, quick, strong, powerful, rich”) (from Proto-Indo-European *twerH- (“to hasten, be quick”)).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *twerH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθu.ri.sɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ giant; demon; monster
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ name of the rune ᚦ (þ)

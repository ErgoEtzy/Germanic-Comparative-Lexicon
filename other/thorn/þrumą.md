# *þrumą

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BErum%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þrumą

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. R. Meringer compares Ancient Greek τέρμα (térma, “end, boundary, goal”), Latin terminus (“boundary, term”), both from Proto-Indo-European *térmn̥ (“boundary”)), which, if correct, invokes an older sense of "stone or piece (used to mark a boundary)".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *térmn̥

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ stump, trunk, block; butt, end

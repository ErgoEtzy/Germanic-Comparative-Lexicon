# *þiudijaną

[Alphabetical index](../../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C3%BEiudijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þiudijaną

### Etymology

Origin obscure. Possibly a conflated word, partly from *þeudō (“people”), from Proto-Indo-European *tewtéh₂ (“people”); and partly from *þiudijaz, *þiudiz (“good, friendly”), from Proto-Indo-European *tew- (“to hearken, take notice, regard in a friendly manner”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *tew-, *tewtéh₂

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθiu̯.ði.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction, transitive, with-instrumental)_ to associate (with); engage with; attach to; join (with); connect
2. _(reconstruction)_ to interpret; point out; explain

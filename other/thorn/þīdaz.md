## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þīdaz

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *tih₁tós, the original, nasal-less past participle to *þīnaną (“to become moist”), ultimately from either Proto-Indo-European *teyh₁- (“~to get hot, cook, boil; to melt”) or *teh₂-y- (“to melt, thaw”). Compare Proto-Celtic *tinīti (“to melt”), Proto-Germanic *þainaz (“wet, moist”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *teyh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈθiː.ðɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ moistened, thawed

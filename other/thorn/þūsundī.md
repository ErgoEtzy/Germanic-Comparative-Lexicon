## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *þūsundī

### Etymology

Either from Proto-Indo-European *tuHsont-, *tuHsenti-, cf. also Proto-Slavic *tysǫti / *tysęti (“thousand”) and Lithuanian tūkstantis, or – as Kroonen suggests – from Proto-Indo-European *tuHsdḱmto-, compounded from a prefix *tuHs- (“fat”, from *tewh₂-) and *ḱm̥tóm (“hundred”). Compare Italian milione from mille + -one (“large thousand”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *tuHsdḱmto-, *tuHsont-

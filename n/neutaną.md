# *neutaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/neutan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *neutaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *newd- (“to seize; grasp; use”). Cognate with Lithuanian naudà (“utility, usefulness, note”), as well as perhaps Proto-Slavic *nùďa (“need, necessity”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *newd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈneu̯.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction, with-genitive)_ to use, to make use of
2. _(reconstruction, with-genitive)_ to enjoy (the use of)

# *newuntēhundą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/newunt%C4%93hund%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *newuntēhundą

### Etymology

Likely a remodelling of earlier *newunhundą on the model of *sebuntēhundą (“seventy”) and *ahtōutēhundą (“eighty”), interpreted as *sebun (“seven”) and *ahtōu (“eight”) + *-tēhundą (“-ty”), from *h₁néwn̥dḱomt, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁néwn̥ (“nine”) + *dḱm̥t (“ten”). For alternative explanations, see *-tēhundą.
By surface analysis, *newun (“nine”) + *-tēhundą (“-ty”), and if the above etymology is correct, ultimately equivalent to *newun (“nine”) + *teguz (“group of ten, decad; -ty”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

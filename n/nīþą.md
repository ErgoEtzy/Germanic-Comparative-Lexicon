# *nīþą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/n%C4%AB%C3%BE%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *nīþą

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *néyHto- or *níHto-, from Proto-Indo-European *neyH- (“to be angry”). Related to Old Irish níth (“battle, fury”), as well as perhaps Tocharian B ñyātse (“danger, plague, distress”) and Primitive Irish *ᚅᚓᚈᚐ (*neta, “warrior, champion”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *neyH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈniː.θɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ hate, envy, malice

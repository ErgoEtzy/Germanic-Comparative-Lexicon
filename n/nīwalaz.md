# *nīwalaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/n%C4%ABwalaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *nīwalaz

### Etymology

Likely from earlier *nīgwala-, denominative adjective from pre-Germanic *nih₃kʷ- (“looking down, facing down”), compound of Proto-Indo-European *ní (“down”) and *h₃ekʷ- (“to see”). Compare Sanskrit नीच (nīca, “below, down”). For a similar formation, see Ancient Greek ὀπιπεύω (opipeúō) < *h₁opi-h₃kʷ-.
There may be semantic conflation and phonetic collapse with the unrelated Proto-Germanic *nebulaz (“fog, darkness”) in some of the descendants, particularly the Old English.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ní

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈniː.wɑ.lɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ lying down, prone, prostrate
2. _(reconstruction)_ deep

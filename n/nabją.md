## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *nabją

### Etymology

Uncertain. Has been taken as a derivative from *nabō (“nave”), with sense development "nave" > "protuberance, knob" > "nose, beak", as well as to Gaulish nebba (“bill”). Compare also Proto-Germanic *snabulaz (“snout”), Lithuanian snãpas (“snout”).

No PIE form is listed or machine-resolvable in this snapshot.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈnɑβ.jɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction, of a bird)_ beak
2. _(neuter, reconstruction, of a human)_ nose

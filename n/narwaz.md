# *narwaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/narwaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *narwaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)ner- (“to turn, bend, twist, constrict”). Compare Proto-Germanic *snōrō (“cord, string, snare”), Gothic 𐍃𐌽𐍉𐍂𐌾𐍉 (snōrjō, “woven basket”) (< *snōrjō), Sanskrit नृत्यति (nṛ́tyati, “to dance”), Russian нерёт (nerjót, “a type of fish trap”), Lithuanian nérti (“to thread; to dive, plunge; to slip and fall”) and Latvian nãrs (“clamp”).
For the suffix, see *-wós.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)ner-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈnɑr.wɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ constricted; narrow

# *nautą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/naut%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *nautą

### Etymology

An a-stem of *neutaną (“to use, make use of; to enjoy”) (with original meaning likely "utilizable (cattle)"), from Proto-Indo-European *newd- (“to acquire, make use of”). Cognate with Lithuanian naudà (“utility, usefulness, note”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *newd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈnɑu̯.tɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ benefit; profit; foredeal
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ possession
3. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ livestock; cattle

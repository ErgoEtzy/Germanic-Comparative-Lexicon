## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -naną

### Etymology

Reconstructed by Ringe from an alternating suffix *-nō- (singular) ~ *-na- (nonsingular), from Proto-Indo-European *-néh₂- ~ *-nh₂-, from n-infix presents to laryngeal-final roots that were reanalysed as a separate suffix (as in several other branches). Cognate with Proto-Slavic *-nǫti.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-néh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/nɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ Forms fientive verbs, with a sense of 'to become (the base)'.

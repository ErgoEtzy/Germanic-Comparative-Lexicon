# *nurþraz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/nur%C3%BEraz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *nurþraz

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *nŕ̥t(e)ros, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *nér (“under (the surface)”). Compare Tocharian B ñor (“below”), Ancient Greek ἐνέρτερος (enérteros), νέρτερος (nérteros, “below (the earth)”), Umbrian nertru (“left”).
The meaning developed either from "region where the sun is below (the earth)" or from "left side of someone who turns to the east when praying".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *nér

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈnur.θrɑz/`

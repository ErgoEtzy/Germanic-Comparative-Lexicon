## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *natǭ

### Etymology

Uncertain; perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *neh₂d- (“to twist, knot, tie”), whence also *natją (“net”), as nettle fibers were used for making nets. In this case, possibly cognate with Old Irish nenaid (“nettle”), Proto-Slavic *natь (“leaves and stems of some vegetables”), the latter which may have been borrowed from Germanic.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *neh₂d-

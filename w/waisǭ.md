## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *waisǭ

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *weys- (“to flow”), whence also Proto-West Germanic *waisund (“windpipe, gullet”), Sanskrit वेषति (véṣati, “to flow”), and perhaps Proto-Germanic *waizą (“seaweed”) (whence Dutch wier (“idem”)).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *weys-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑi̯.sɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ mire, muck, slime

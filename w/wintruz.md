# *wintruz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wintruz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wintruz

### Etymology

Unknown. Several possibilities have been proposed, although none are particularly convincing:
* Per Boutkan, possible relation to *windaz (whence wind) (i.e. 'the windy season'), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂wéh₁n̥tos (“wind”), from earlier *h₂wéh₁n̥ts (“wind”), derived from the present participle of *h₂weh₁- (“to blow”).
* Relation to Proto-Celtic *windos (“white”).
* Relation to Proto-Indo-European *wódr̥ (“water”), whence also Proto-Germanic *watōr.
The first theory involves an analysis of Latin unda continuing *u-n-deh₂ rather than *ud-neh₂, while there is little semantic evidence for the second theory.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂wéh₁n̥tos, *wódr̥

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwin.truz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ winter
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ year (as a measure of time)

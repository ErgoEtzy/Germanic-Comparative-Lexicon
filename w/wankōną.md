# *wankōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wank%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wankōną

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, a nasal-infixed derivative from the verb *weganą (“to move”). Orel prefers a connection to *wenkjanana, which he derives from Proto-Indo-European *weng- (“to bow, bend”), and compares to Sanskrit वङ्गति (vaṅgati, “to limp, walk”), Lithuanian véngti (“to swerve, avoid”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *weng-

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to sway, to waver

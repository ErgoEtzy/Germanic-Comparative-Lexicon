# *waikwijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/waikwijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *waikwijaną

### Etymology

From *waikwaz (“weak”) + *-janą or *wīkwaną (“to yield, to fold”) + *-janą, which would make the original sense of this word 'to cause to yield'.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-ye-ti, *-yeti, *-yé-ti, *-yéti, *-éh₁-ti, *-éh₁ti, *-éye-ti, *-éyeti, *h₃woyg-wo-s, *h₃wéyg-we-ti, *weyg-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑi̯.kʷi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to make weak, to weaken, to soften

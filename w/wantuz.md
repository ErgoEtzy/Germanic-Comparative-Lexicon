## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wantuz

### Etymology

Likely from a pre-Germanic *wanttuz, presumably from *wantnuz via Kluge's law, from Proto-Indo-European *wondʰnú- (“glove”), from Proto-Indo-European *wendʰ- (“to wind, wrap”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wendʰ-, *wondʰnú-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑn.tuz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ glove, mitten

# *waikwaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/waikwaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *waikwaz

### Etymology

By surface analysis, *wīkwaną (“to yield, buckle, fold”) + *-az (adjectival), probably reflecting Proto-Indo-European *h₃woyg-wo-s and *h₃woyg-o-s (the latter either a late variant or a separate o-stem derivation directly from the root), with sense development “giving way” > “weak, pliable”.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₃woyg-wo-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑi̯.kʷɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ weak, soft, pliable

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *weþruz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *wétrus, from *wet- (“year”). Cognate with Latin vitulus (“calf”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wet-, *wétrus

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈweθ.ruz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ yearling lamb
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ wether, ram

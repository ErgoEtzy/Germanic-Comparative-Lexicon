## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wahsą

### Etymology

Of somewhat disputed origin. Cognate with Proto-Balto-Slavic *wáśkan (“wax”), from Proto-Indo-European *woḱso (“idem”), which was originally reconstructed by Pokorny as *wokso, *wos-ko-. However, others have compared the terms with Latin velum (“sailcloth”) and Dutch wiek (“windmill sail”), from *weg- (“to weave”) (though the semantic link appears tenuous). The Germanic and Balto-Slavic terms could instead be from a pre-Indo-European substrate.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

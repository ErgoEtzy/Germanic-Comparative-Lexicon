# *wilþijaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wil%C3%BEijaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wilþijaz

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, from Proto-Indo-European *welt-yo-s (“wild, savage”) (though Matasovic reconstructs the root as *gʷʰel-t-), and cognate with Proto-Celtic *gʷeltis (“wild; mad”) (Welsh gwyllt (“wild”), Irish gealt (“lunatic”)). Kroonen additionally suggests that the root is related to Proto-Germanic *walþuz (“forest”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *welt-yo-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwil.θi.jɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ wild

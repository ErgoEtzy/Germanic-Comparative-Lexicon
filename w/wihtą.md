## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wihtą

### Etymology

Most likely from Proto-Indo-European *wékʷ-ti-s ~ *ukʷ-téy-s, from *wekʷ- (“to speak”) + *-tis. Cognate with Proto-Slavic *vȅťь (“thing”) (whence e.g. Old Church Slavonic вещь (veštĭ)). Oryol implicitly considers *wihtiz to be the original form of this word, in which case the neuter a-stem would have to be secondary and perhaps split from the same original paradigm; he notes that Gothic 𐍅𐌰𐌹𐌷𐍄𐍃 (waihts) “shows traces of a root stem in pl[ural]”. In most West Germanic descendants, the neuter and feminine variants are nearly merged.

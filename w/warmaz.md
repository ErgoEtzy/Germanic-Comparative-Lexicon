# *warmaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/warmaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *warmaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Two different etymologies have been proposed.
# From Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰór-mo-s, from *gʷʰer- (“warm, hot”) + *-mos, related to Ancient Greek θερμός (thermós), Latin formus, Persian گرم (garm) and Sanskrit घर्म (gharma).
# From Proto-Indo-European *wór-mo-s, from *wer- (“to burn”) + *-mos, related to Hittite [script needed] (warnuzi).
The dispute is due to differing hypotheses on how Proto-Indo-European initial *gʷʰ- evolved in Germanic. Some maintain that *gʷʰ would have turned to *b, and therefore that the root *gʷʰer- would instead have given rise to *ber- (“to burn”) etc. (compare *banô < *gʷʰon-ō). A counterargument (e.g. Kroonen (2013: p. xxviii)) is that Germanic *w is in fact the usual outcome of *gʷʰ, citing uncontroversial examples such as *snaiwaz < *snóygʷʰos and *neurô < *négʷʰrō. There have also been etymologies proposing a merger of the two roots. However, it is generally thought that initial *gʷʰ regularly evolved into *b, while intervocalic *gʷʰ regularly evolved into *w in most cases, favoring the second hypothesis.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷʰer-, *gʷʰór-mo-s, *mos, *wer-, *wór-mo-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑr.mɑz/`

### Adjective

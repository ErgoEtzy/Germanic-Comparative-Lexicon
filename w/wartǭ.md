# *wartǭ

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wart%C7%AD)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wartǭ

### Etymology

Two possibilities:
# From Proto-Indo-European *werd- (“to blister”), which could be a d-extension of Proto-Indo-European *wer- (“to burn”). Cognate with Proto-Slavic *verdъ (“wart”) and Latvian apvir̂de (“blister”).
# From Proto-Indo-European *wers-. Cognate with Latin verrūca (“high place; excrescence, wart”), Lithuanian viršus (“top”), Old Church Slavonic врьхъ (vrĭxŭ, “top, peak”), Ancient Greek ἕρμα (hérma, “reef, rock, hill”), Sanskrit वर्ष्मन् (varṣman, “protrusion, summit, peak, top, vertex”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wer-, *werd-, *wers-

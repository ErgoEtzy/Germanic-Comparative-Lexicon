# *werþaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wer%C3%BEaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *werþaz

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *wértos. While Kroonen leaves the origin open, Matasović derives the word from Proto-Indo-European *wert- (“to turn”) through an intermediary meaning of "exchange", a development also seen in Celtic; compare Proto-Brythonic *gwerθ (“worth, price”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wert-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwer.θɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ worthy, valuable

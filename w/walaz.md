# *walaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/walaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *walaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *welh₃- (“to wound; death (in battle); slain (in battle)”). Cognate with Lithuanian vėlė (“ghost”), Czech válka (“war”).

From pre-Germanic *wolH-os, "turned or turning", from Proto-Indo-European *welH- (“to turn, wind”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *welH-, *welh₃-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑ.lɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ corpse (dead human-body, especially in a warring context)
2. _(broadly, masculine, reconstruction)_ battlefield (an area of many corpses)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wurmiz

### Etymology

From earlier *wúrmis, from Proto-Indo-European *wr̥mis (“worm”). Cognate with Latin vermis, Ancient Greek ῥόμος (rhómos), Lithuanian var̃mas (“bug, fly”), Old Prussian wormyan (“red”), Old East Slavic *вьрмиѥ (*vĭrmije, whence Ukrainian вермяний (vermjanyj, “red”)). The “red” meaning in some languages developed from the pigment derived from scale insects.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wr̥mis

### Pronunciation

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ worm
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ serpent, snake, dragon

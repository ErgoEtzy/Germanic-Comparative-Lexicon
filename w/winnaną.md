# *winnaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/winnan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *winnaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *wen- (“to win, achieve, overcome”); cognate with Sanskrit वनोति (vanóti, “to win, defeat, procure”). The root may be the basis of Proto-Indo-European *wenh₁- (“to love”), with semantic connection "to feel achieved with a given thing" > "to love".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wen-, *wenh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwin.nɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to labour, toil, work
2. _(reconstruction)_ to win, gain
3. _(reconstruction)_ to fight, strive

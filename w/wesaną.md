## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wesaną

### Etymology

From two different Proto-Indo-European roots:
* The present tense from *h₁ésti, from *h₁es- (“to be”).
* The past tense from *h₂wes- (“to dwell, stay”).

From Proto-Indo-European *wes- (“to graze”). Whereas the LIV suggests that Gothic 𐍅𐌹𐍃𐌰𐌽 (wisan) may derive from a Narten present *wḗs-ti ~ *wés-n̥ti,, Kroonen derives the Proto-Germanic form from a thematic root present *wés-e-ti. Regardless, the term is related to Proto-Celtic *westā (“food, feast”), Latin vēscor (“to make use of”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₁es-, *h₂wes-, *wes-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwe.sɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to be
2. _(reconstruction)_ to remain
3. _(reconstruction)_ to exist

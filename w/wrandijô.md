## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wrandijô

### Etymology

Unknown. Compare Old Irish dreän, Welsh dryw. Perhaps related to Icelandic rindill (“tail, wagtail”), and related to Old Norse rǫnd (“rim, edge”), Old English rand, German Rand.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwrɑn.di.jɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ wren (bird)

# *wanhō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wanh%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wanhō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *wonk- (“crooked, bent”), from Proto-Indo-European *wā- (“to bend, slant, incline, twist, turn”). Cognate with Sanskrit वङ्क (vaṅka, “curve, bend”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wonk-, *wā-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑ̃ː.xɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a bend, turn, corner, angle, curve, incline

# *wundō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wund%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wundō

### Etymology

Nominal formation from *wundaz (“wounded”), from Proto-Indo-European *wn̥-tó-s, from a root *wen- (“to strive, toil, win”), invoking a sense of "toiled (in battle)". Kroonen considers the word related to *winnaną (“idem”), while Orel compares Welsh ymwan (“to battle”) and perhaps Old Armenian վանդեմ (vandem, “to destroy”). It is possible that all these terms are related to each other, as well.
Older theories derive the word from a Proto-Indo-European *h₂wn̥tós (“wounded”), from a Proto-Indo-European *h₂wen- (“to strike, injure, hurt”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂wen-, *h₂wn̥tós, *wn̥-tó-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwun.dɔː/`

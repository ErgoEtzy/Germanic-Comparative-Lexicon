# *wēniz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/w%C4%93niz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wēniz

### Etymology

Possible vṛddhi gerundive of unattested *wenaną (“to be accustomed to, expect”), from Proto-Indo-European *wénh₁-e-ti, from *wenh₁- (“to love”). Orel however considers the word to be an adjectival derivative of the noun sense in Etymology 2, though ultimately traces the word back to *wenh₁- regardless.

Likely nominalized from the adjective, though Kroonen and Orel consider the noun to be the original form.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wenh₁-, *wénh₁-e-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɛː.niz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ likely, to be expected
2. _(reconstruction)_ promising, hopeful

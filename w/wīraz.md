## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wīraz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *weh₁iros (“a twist, thread, cord, wire”), from *weh₁y- (“to turn, twist, weave, plait”). Compare, in particular, Proto-Celtic *weiros (“crooked”), Lithuanian vairùs (“diverse, various”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *weh₁iros

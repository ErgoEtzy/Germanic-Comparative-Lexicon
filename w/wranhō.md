# *wranhō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wranh%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wranhō

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. Per Mikkola, perhaps from a Proto-Indo-European *wrónkeh₂, from the root *wrenk- (“to turn, twist, bend; to clutch, grasp”), and possibly formal cognate with Proto-Balto-Slavic *ránkāˀ (“clutch, hand, arm”). On the other hand, others, including Orel and de Vries, prefer a secondary derivation from *wringaną (“to squeeze, wring”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wrónkeh₂

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ angle, corner, nook

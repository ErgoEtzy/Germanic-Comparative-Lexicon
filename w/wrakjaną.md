# *wrakjaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wrakjan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wrakjaną

### Etymology

By surface analysis, *wrekaną (“to persue”) + *-janą. The LIV considers Gothic wrakjan to possibly reflect a Proto-Indo-European causative Proto-Indo-European *wrog-éye-ti, from *wreg-. It is possible that this same causative formation is the source of Sanskrit vrājáyati.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wreg-, *wrog-éye-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwrɑk.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to track, to follow
2. _(reconstruction)_ to persecute

# *wargaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wargaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wargaz

### Etymology

Normally derived from Proto-Indo-European *werǵʰ- (“to turn, twist, press, constrict, strangle”). An alternative opinion takes it as a borrowing from Proto-Slavic *vorgъ, e.g. *izъvьrgnǫti (“to cast out”), regularly cognate to Latin urgēre and in Germanic *wrekaną (“to pursue, to persecute”). See also *argaz.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *werǵʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwɑr.ɣɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ outlaw, criminal
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ wolf

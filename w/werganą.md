# *werganą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wergan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *werganą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *wérǵʰ-e-, from *werǵʰ- (“to bind, squeeze”). Cognate with Lithuanian ver̃žti (“to tighten, string”), Proto-Slavic *povorzъ (“cord, rope”), as well as perhaps Albanian vjerdh (“to instill, bring close”), Latin urgeō (“to press, push”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *werǵʰ-, *wérǵʰ-e-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwer.ɣɑ.nɑ̃./`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to strangle

### Germanic descendants

- **Middle High German:** *wergen
- **Middle High German:** erwergen

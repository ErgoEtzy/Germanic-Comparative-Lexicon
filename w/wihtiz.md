## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wihtiz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *wék⁽ʷ⁾tis (“utterance; object, thing”), most likely from *wekʷ- (“to speak”). Cognate with Proto-Slavic *veťь.
Kroonen alternatively compares Proto-Celtic *wextā (“time, course, run”) and *weganą (“to carry, to move”) (from Proto-Indo-European *wéǵʰeti), suggesting *wéǵʰtis, which would make this word a doublet of *wihtiz (“weight”, etymology 2). He also appears to tentatively assume devoicing of the root coda before *-t- in Slavic, but it may need to be considered unrelated in this interpretation. A less supported connection is to Proto-Indo-European *weḱ- (“to choose, wish”).

From *weganą (“to weight”) + *-þiz.

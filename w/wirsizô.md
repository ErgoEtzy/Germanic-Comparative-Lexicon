# *wirsizô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/wirsiz%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wirsizô

### Etymology

Derived from Proto-Indo-European *wers- (“to rise; peak”). Probably related to Irish fearr (“better”), which has the opposite meaning. The original meaning could therefore be 'further outside, more extreme' for both the Germanic and Irish, albeit developing in opposite directions connotationally. Maybe related to Lithuanian viršùs (“upper part, peak”), viršuti̇̀nis (“upper, outer”), and Sanskrit वर्षीयस् (várṣīyas, “higher”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *wers-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwir.zi.zɔːː/`

### Adjective

1. _(comparative, form-of, reconstruction)_ comparative degree of *ubilaz

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wīlą

### Etymology

Related to Middle Irish fell (“deception”), Lithuanian vi̇̀lti (“to deceive”), with further origin of the terms somewhat disputed:
* From Proto-Indo-European *welh₁- (“to choose, want”), with semantic shift "will, want" > "change one's wills" > "deceive" (though Smoczynski does not mention the Germanic cognates);
* or from Proto-Indo-European *weh₁y- (“to turn, bend”) + *-ilą (“neuter agentive suffix”). The suffix is the neuter form of *-ilaz.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *weh₁y-, *welh₁-

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ craft
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ deceit

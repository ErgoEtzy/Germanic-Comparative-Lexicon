## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wedrōną

### Etymology

From *wedrą (“weather”) + *-ōną.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-(e)h₂yéti, *-dʰrom, *-eh₂yéti, *-h₂ti, *-h₂yéti, *-trom, *h₂weh₁-, *h₂wḗh₁ti, *wedʰrom

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈweð.rɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to expose to the weather
2. _(reconstruction)_ to change (of the weather)
3. _(reconstruction)_ to be in a particular state (of the weather)

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/w%C4%ABdaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *wīdaz

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *h₁weydʰh₁os (“set apart, asunder, in two”), from Proto-Indo-European *dwi- (“two”) (see *dwóh₁) + *dʰeh₁- (“to put”), apparently with dissimilation of the first d-. Cognate with Proto-Germanic *widuwǭ (“widow”), *widuz (“wood”), Proto-Celtic *weidus (“wild”) and Latin dīvidō (“separate, sunder”), vītō (“avoid, shun”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dwi-, *h₁weydʰh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈwiː.ðɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ wide
2. _(reconstruction)_ broad

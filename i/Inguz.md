## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *Inguz

### Etymology

Uncertain; possibly from *inguz, *unguz (“mortal; man”), from Proto-Indo-European *n̥ḱús (“dead, mortal”), from *neḱ- (“to die, perish”) + *-us. Thought to be the human incarnation of Nerthus, and the original name of the Norse fertility god Freyr.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *neḱ-, *néḱus, *n̥ḱús, *us

### Proper noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ Ing, Yngvi, god of fertility
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ Son of Mannus, progenitor of the Ynglings and Ingaevones.
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ name of the rune ᛜ, ᛝ (ng)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *irzijaną

### Etymology

From *irzijaz (“bewildered; erring”) + *-janą (factitive suffix). Cognate with Latin errō (“to wander, roam, stray, lose one's way”) and Lithuanian įširsti (“to become angry, fly into passion”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₁ers-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈir.zi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to delude, mislead

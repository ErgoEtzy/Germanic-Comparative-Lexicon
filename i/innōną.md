## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *innōną

### Etymology

From *inn (“in, into”) + *-ōną.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-(e)h₂yéti, *-eh₂yéti, *-h₂ti, *-h₂yéti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈin.nɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to take inside
2. _(reconstruction)_ to take into possession
3. _(reconstruction)_ to accomplish

# *erþahūsą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/er%C3%BEah%C5%ABs%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *erþahūsą

### Etymology

Compound of *erþō (“earth”) + *hūsą (“house”). According to Tacitus in his Germania, the Germanic peoples made use of underground caves for shelter and storage.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈer.θɑ.ˌxuː.sɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ underground dwelling, cave

# *bandijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bandijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bandijaną

### Etymology

By surface analysis, *bindaną + *-janą. Possibly inherited from Proto-Indo-European *bʰondʰ-éye-ti, from the root *bʰendʰ-, with an exact parallel in Avestan 𐬠𐬀𐬧𐬛𐬀𐬌𐬌𐬈𐬌𐬙𐬌 (baṇdaiieⁱti).. The sense to "bend, inflect" (Old Norse and Old English only) is probably derived from the act of bending a bow in order to bind it.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰendʰ-, *bʰondʰ-éye-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑn.di.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction, transitive)_ to bind, fetter, restrain; stretch
2. _(reconstruction, transitive)_ to bend

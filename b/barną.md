# *barną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/barn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *barną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰor-no-m, from *bʰer- (“to bear, carry”). Cognate with Latvian bērns (“child”), Lithuanian bérnas (“lad, servant”), berniukas (“boy”). Compare another cognate Latin forda (“cow in calf”).
Compare typologically Russian недоно́сок (nedonósok) (akin to нести́ (nestí)).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰer-, *bʰor-no-m

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑr.nɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ child

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *brōks

### Etymology

From earlier *brāks, from Pre-Germanic *bʰrāg-, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰreg- (“to break, crack, split”). Cognate with Latin frangō.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰreg-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbrɔːks/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ crotch; rear end, rump, hindquarters
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ leggings, pants, trousers

# *bautaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bautan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bautaną

### Etymology

Uncertain. Kroonen considers it most likely derived from earlier *bauttan-, with *-tt- by Kluge's law from a root *bʰewt- or *bʰewdʰ- combined with a suffix starting with *n. Traditionally derived from a root *bʰewd-. Alternatively from a root *bʰew- with a dental extension/suffix.
Possibly related to the West Germanic adjective *buttaz (“stumpy; cut off; blunt”).
Possible cognates outside of Germanic include Latin *fūtō (“to strike”), fūstis (“stick, club”); Albanian butë (“tender”); Middle Irish búailid (“to beat, strike”); Old Irish fo·botha (“he threatened”); Albanian bahe (“sling”); Lithuanian baudžiù; Old Armenian բութ (butʻ).
The Latin verb implies *bʰewt-, but this violates the rule that PIE roots do not contain a voiced aspirate along with a voiceless plosive. De Vaan suggests there was a substrate root of the form *būt-/bŭt-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰew-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑu̯.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to beat, knock, bump, push

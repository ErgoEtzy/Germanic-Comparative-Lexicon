# *badją

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/badj%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *badją

### Etymology

Uncertain; possibly from Proto-Indo-European *bʰodʰh₂-yó-m (“dug-out plot of land”), from *bʰedʰh₂- (“to dig, burrow; to pierce, stab”) + *-yóm (the supposed intermediate meaning would be paralleled in Proto-Celtic *bedom (“ditch, grave”) and explain the sense “flowerbed” in West Germanic); or perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *bʰh₁-tó-s (“warm”), from *bʰeh₁- (“to warm”) + *-tós, whence *baþą (“bath”), Dutch betten (“to warm up”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰedʰh₂-, *bʰeh₁-, *bʰh₁-tó-s, *bʰodʰh₂-yó-m, *tós, *yós

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑð.jɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ bed
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ bedding, cushion
3. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ plot of ground

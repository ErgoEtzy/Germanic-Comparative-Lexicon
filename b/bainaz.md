# *bainaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bainaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bainaz

### Etymology

Of disputed origin.
Orel prefers a derivation, based on the derivative *bainą (“leg, bone”) (< *bʰoy-no-m), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰoy-no-s, from *bʰeyh₂- (“to hit, strike, beat”), thus "a striking object" > "bone" > "straight like a bone".
Kroonen does not mention this theory, and tentatively prefers to take the word from Proto-Indo-European *bʰh₂-oy-no-, a no-formation from an i-present *bʰh₂-ey-, from *bʰeh₂- (“to shine”), with sense development "straight beam or ray (of light)" > "straight post or stake" > "straight leg, bone".
Bammesberger (1990) suggests a relationship to Latin fīnis (“limit, border, end”), which together with Germanic could point to a Proto-Indo-European n-stem *bʰeyH-n- (“straight line, pole”), though its root is unclear.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰeyh₂-, *bʰh₂-oy-no-, *bʰoy-no-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑi̯.nɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ straight, right
2. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ ready, willing

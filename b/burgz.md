# *burgz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/burgz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *burgz

### Etymology

Either:
* from Proto-Indo-European *bʰérǵʰs (“something high up and fortified”)
* or from Proto-Indo-European *bʰergʰ- (“to take care of, protect, preserve”).
Both theories are formally and semantically reasonable. For a semantic parallel, compare Latin arx, which goes through the meanings of *burgz and of *bergaz (“hill, mountain”); perhaps both Indo-European roots conflated and contributed to the proto-Germanic term's development.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰergʰ-, *bʰérǵʰs

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/burɣz/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ fortification, stronghold
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ city

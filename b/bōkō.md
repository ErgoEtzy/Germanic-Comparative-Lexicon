# *bōkō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/b%C5%8Dk%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bōkō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰeh₂ǵos (“beech”). The Indo-European root is also the source of Ancient Greek φηγός (phēgós, “oak”) and Latin fāgus (“beech”).
The origin of the senses in Gothic (“book, writing”) is unclear, but they may be transferred by conflation from *bōks f (see there for more), or both senses “beech” and “writing” may have been originally present in both words; in the latter case, *bōkō can safely be derived from *bōks in pre-Germanic.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰeh₂ǵos

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɔː.kɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ beech
2. _(East, Germanic, feminine, reconstruction)_ writing, text, book

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *buskaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Traditionally derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *bʰuH- (“to swell, grow”) via an *‐s‐ extension and the thematic velar suffix *-kos, yielding the pre‐form *bʰuH-s-ko-s (“growth, growing thing”) (compare Ancient Greek φῠτόν (phŭtón, “plant”), Russian быльё (bylʹjó)). Alternatively, it may be a deverbative noun derived from the related *būaną (“to dwell, reside”) via the suffix *-skaz, with regular shortening of the long vowel before the heavy consonant cluster (from an earlier *bū‐skaz, literally ‘clearing’ or ‘brush cut for a dwelling’, shifting metonymically to ‘thicket’ or ‘brushwood’). However, because the word is restricted entirely to the Germanic branch and denotes local flora, it is frequently suspected to be a loanword from a pre‐Germanic substrate.

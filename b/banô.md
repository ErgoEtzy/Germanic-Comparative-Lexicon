# *banô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/ban%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *banô

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰon-ō, an individualizing n-stem derivative of *gʷʰonós (“one who strikes down”), from the root *gʷʰen- (“to strike, kill”), though the sound change required from *gʷʰ > *b is controversial (but appears to be regular in word-initial position).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷʰen-, *gʷʰon-ō

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑ.nɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ murderer, killer, slayer
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ bane, death

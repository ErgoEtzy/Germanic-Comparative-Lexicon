# *blautaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/blautaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *blautaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰlowdh₂-o-s, from *bʰlow- (“emaciated, soft”). Cognate with Ancient Greek φλυδάω (phludáō, “to be soft, weak”). Despite similar phonetics and semantics, not related to Proto-Germanic *blauþuz (“soft, weak”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰlow-, *bʰlowdh₂-o-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈblɑu̯.tɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ soft
2. _(reconstruction)_ exhausted; emptied out; spent
3. _(reconstruction)_ fainthearted
4. _(reconstruction)_ bare; naked

# *breutaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/breutan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *breutaną

### Etymology

Related to, perhaps via back-formation, to *brutōną (“to bud”), itself from Proto-Indo-European *bʰrewd- (“to bud”), which Kroonen compares to Latin frōns (“foliage, leaves”). Others have compared the word to Old East Slavic бръснути (brŭsnuti, “rake”), Old English brȳsan (“to break”), Latin frustum (“piece, crumb”).
Whether the intransitive counterpart *breuþaną is related is unclear.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰrewd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbreu̯.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to destroy, crush, break

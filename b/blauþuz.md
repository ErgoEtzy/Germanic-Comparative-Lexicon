## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *blauþuz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *mlów-tu-s, from *mlew- (“to crush, soften”). Related to Proto-Germanic *blewwaną (“to beat (up), blow”), Ancient Greek ἀμβλύς (amblús, “blunt; dim, faint”), Avestan 𐬨𐬭𐬎𐬙𐬀 (mruta, “crushed (?), weak”). Despite similar phonetics and semantics, not related to Proto-Germanic *blautaz (“soft, bare”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *mlów-tu-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈblɑu̯.θuz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ weak, feeble, emaciated
2. _(reconstruction)_ lacking, void, naked
3. _(reconstruction)_ soft, faint-hearted, gentle

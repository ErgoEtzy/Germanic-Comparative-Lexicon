## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bruzdaz

### Etymology

A zero-grade variant of *brazdaz (“edge, brim”), probably from Proto-Indo-European *bʰros-dʰ-o-, from the root *bʰers- (“top, point”). Cognate with Old Irish brot (“goad, spike”).
May be (along with related terms) from an original n-stem *brezdô ~ *burzdiniz, in view of the ablaut.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰers-, *bʰros-dʰ-o-

### Pronunciation

### Noun

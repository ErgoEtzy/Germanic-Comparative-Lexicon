## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *berô

### Etymology

Conventionally from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerH-on-, from the root *bʰerH- (“brown”), as a tabooistic reference to the bear as "the brown one". Ringe, doubting the existence of such a root, suggests instead *ǵʰwer- (“wild animal”); however, as Kroonen notes, this derivation depends on the sound change from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰw- > Proto-Germanic *b-, whose validity is disputed. Blažek (2017) alternatively suggests a derivation from *bʰerH- (“to bore, pierce”), from which several IE terms for beehive are derived, e.g. Proto-Slavic *bъrtь (“hive of wild bees”).

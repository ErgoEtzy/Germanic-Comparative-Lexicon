## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *brōaną

### Etymology

An o-grade intensive, Pre-Germanic *bʰroh₁-, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰreh₁- (“to burn, brew”), with no certain cognates outside of Germanic. Note, however, strong semantic and phonetic resemblance to Proto-Indo-European *bʰrewh₁- (“to boil, brew”) (whence Proto-Germanic *brewwaną (“to brew”)). See also Proto-West Germanic *brāan (“to smoke, smell”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰreh₁-, *bʰrewh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbrɔː.ɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to singe, warm, brew

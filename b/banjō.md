## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *banjō

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰon-yeh₂, from *gʷʰen- (“to strike, kill”), although disputed by Kroonen due to the odd-in-Germanic sound change required from *gʷʰ > *b, who instead derives it from *bʰon-eye-, and adduces no further cognates in this latter theory.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷʰen-, *gʷʰon-yeh₂

# *beuzą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/beuz%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *beuzą

### Etymology

Uncertain. Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewsóm, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰews- (“dross, sediment; brewer's yeast”). If so, cognate with Swedish buska (“freshly brewed beer, new beer”), Middle Dutch and Middle Low German bûsen (“to feast, booze, drink heavily”), Middle High German būs (“a swelling”), and indirectly (via bowse, ultimately from Middle Dutch) booze. Non-Germanic cognates may include Albanian mbush (“to fill, stuff”).
Alternatively reconstructed by Kroonen as *beurą, a dissimilation of earlier *breurą, derived from *brewwaną (“to brew”). In a parenthetical side remark, Hyllested speculatively suggests an origin in Oghur.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰews-, *bʰewsóm

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbeu̯.zɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ beer

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bullǭ

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰln-, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (“to blow, inflate, swell, bubble”). Synchronically analyzable as an ablaut form of *balluz (“ball”). See also *bullô.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰel-, *bʰln-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbul.lɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ round object, ball
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ round vessel, bowl

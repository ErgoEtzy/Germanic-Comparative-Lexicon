## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *barō

### Etymology

Uncertain. Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *bʰerH- (“to strike; pierce”). Compare Latin forus (“gangway, plank”), Russian забо́р (zabór, “fencing, paling, fence”). Compare also Ancient Greek φάρος (pháros, “piece of land, furrow, marker, beacon, lighthouse”). Alternatively from Proto-Indo-European *bʰoréh₂ via a meaning “support”, from *bʰer- (“to bear, carry”). Compare in that case Old Norse barar f pl (“handbarrow, bier”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

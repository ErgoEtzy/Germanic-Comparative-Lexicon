# *bredą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bred%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bredą

### Etymology

An e-grade variant of *burdą (“board; edge”).
Kroonen prefers to reconstruct the term as a z-stem *brezdaz, noting that the loss of the first *-z- in the Old High German form may be via dissimilation in the plural form (*brertira < *brezd-izō). He claims that Old English bred is from Old Norse, Dutch berd is from *burdą (which at least in Limburg cannot be true), and fails even to mention Old Saxon bred. The predominant plural bretir in Old High German proves nothing, because the latter also has feldir, grebir and others.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ board, plank, shelf

# *bugjaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bugjan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bugjaną

### Etymology

Orel derives the root from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewgʰ- (“to bend; curve, arch”), with semantic shift "to bend" > "to take away, deliver" > "to deal, buy" comparable to that seen in Ancient Greek πέλω (pélō, “to turn, move”) > πωλέω (pōléō, “to sell”). Kroonen (2013) is skeptical of the proposed semantics, and leaves the origin open.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰewgʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbuɣ.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to deal; do business; exchange
2. _(reconstruction)_ to purchase; buy
3. _(reconstruction)_ to accept

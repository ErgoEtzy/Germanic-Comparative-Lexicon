# *blakaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/blakaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *blakaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Perhaps from Pre-Germanic *bʰlogos, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰleg-, -g- extended form of Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (“to blaze, burn”). Compare Proto-Indo-European *bʰleyǵ- (“to shine, burn, scorch”).
Alternatively, perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *ml̥h₂gos, from Proto-Indo-European *melh₂-. Compare Ancient Greek μέλᾱς (mélās, “dark, black”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰel-, *bʰleg-, *bʰleyǵ-, *melh₂-, *ml̥h₂gos

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈblɑ.kɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ burnt
2. _(reconstruction)_ black, swart

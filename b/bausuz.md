## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bausuz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰowHs-o-s, from a root *bʰewHs- (“to blow, inflate, swell”) of apparent onomatopoeic origin, which Kroonen compares with Russian бу́хнуть (búxnutʹ, “to swell up”), Kashubian bucha (“pride, haughtiness”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰowHs-o-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑu̯.suz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ inflated; swollen
2. _(reconstruction)_ puffed up; proud; arrogant
3. _(reconstruction)_ bad

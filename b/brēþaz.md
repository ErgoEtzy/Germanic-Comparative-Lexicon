## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *brēþaz

### Etymology

Related to Sanskrit भुरति (bhurati, “to stir, palpitate”), both from Proto-Indo-European *bʰer- (“to boil, seethe”). By synchronic analysis, an adjectival formation from *brējaną (“to roast, waft”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰer-, *bʰrewh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbrɛː.θɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ rushed; in a hurry
2. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ quick; nimble

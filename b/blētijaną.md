# *blētijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bl%C4%93tijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *blētijaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰleh₁- (“to howl, cry, bleat”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (“to make a loud noise”). Cognate with Latvian blēšana (“to bleat”), Russian бле́ять (bléjatʹ, “to bleat”), Latin fleō (“cry, weep”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰel-, *bʰleh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈblɛː.ti.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to bleat

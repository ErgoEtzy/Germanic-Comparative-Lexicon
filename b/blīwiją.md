# *blīwiją

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bl%C4%ABwij%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *blīwiją

### Etymology

Unknown; possibly from a pre-form of Proto-Indo-European *mliH-wó-s, which appears to be a regional Wanderwort of substrate origin. Compare Ancient Greek μόλῠβδος (mólŭbdos, “lead”), Latin plumbum (“lead”).
Older theories tentatively derived the word from *mleh₁y- (“to be weak, weary, tired”); compare *blīþiz (“soft, mild”), and, outside of Germanic, Sanskrit म्लायति (mlā́yati, “to be weary”), Proto-Celtic *mlīnos (“weary, tired”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *mliH-wó-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbliː.wi.jɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ lead (metal)

# *balluz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/balluz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *balluz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰol-n- (“round thing, bubble”), from *bʰel- (“to blow, inflate, swell”). Cognate with Latin follis (“windbag, balloon”).
According to Kroonen, this was originally an n-stem (as preserved in Old High German ballo, German Ballen), and the North Germanic *balluz is an early back-formation from the old accusative plural **balluns.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰel-, *bʰol-n-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑl.luz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ round object, ball
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ ball of the hand, foot, etc.

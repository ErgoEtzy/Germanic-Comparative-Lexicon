# *blīþiz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bl%C4%AB%C3%BEiz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *blīþiz

### Etymology

From *blī-, *bli- (“to shine, gleam, glow; be light, be glad”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰlī- (“light, fine, pleasant”), from *bʰleh₁-, *bʰel- (“to shine”). Cognate with Lithuanian bliva (“violet”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰlī-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbliː.θiz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ serene, mild
2. _(reconstruction)_ pleasant, pleasing, delightful, friendly

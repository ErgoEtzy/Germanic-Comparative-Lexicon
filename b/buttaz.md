# *buttaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/buttaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *buttaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰudnós, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewd- (“to beat, push”), an extension of *bʰew- (“to blow, swell”).

Either:
* From Proto-Indo-European *bʰudʰnós (“bottom”), later thematic variant of *bʰudʰmḗn ~ *bʰudʰn- (leveled to the weak stem), perhaps ultimately from *dʰewbʰ- (“deep”). Doublet of *butmaz, of which it split off from the same pre-Proto-Germanic paradigm.
* Deadjectival from etymology 1, with an original sense “chopped-off part”.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰewd-, *bʰudnós, *bʰudʰmḗn, *bʰudʰnós

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbut.tɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, West, reconstruction)_ cut off; chopped; stumpy

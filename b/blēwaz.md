# *blēwaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bl%C4%93waz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *blēwaz

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *bʰleh₁wos, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰleh₁- (“blue, gray, yellow”). The formation is similar to *grēwaz. Kroonen reconstructs an ablauting u-stem, *bʰléh₁-, *bʰl̥h₁- in order to reconcile the vowels of the Germanic word with Latin flāvus (“yellow”), which is likely cognate; compare also Proto-Celtic *blāwos (“yellow”). It is uncertain whether this root is related to Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (“light, bright”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰel-, *bʰleh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈblɛː.wɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ blue
2. _(reconstruction)_ a dark bluish or grey color, black

# *bakaburdaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bakaburdaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *bakaburdaz

### Etymology

From *baką + *burdaz, a variant of Proto-Germanic *burdą (“board”). So named as the steersman's back is toward this side of the vessel when facing the fore.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *bʰeg-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈbɑ.kɑ.ˌbur.ðɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ the larboard side of a ship, backboard

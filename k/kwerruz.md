# *kwerruz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kwerruz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kwerruz

### Etymology

From a Proto-Indo-European *gʷerH- (“to crumble, weaken”), and related to Latvian gur̃t (“to become weak, slacken”) and Lithuanian gùrti (“to crumble, weaken, subside”). The root *gʷerH- may be further identical to *gʷerh₂- (“heavy”), from the same semantic tree as Proto-Indo-European *gʷréh₂wō (“quern, millstone”), thus "to grind with a quern" > "to weaken, make lax" > "to make still, quiet".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷerH-, *gʷréh₂wō

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkwer.ruz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ calm; still; quiet

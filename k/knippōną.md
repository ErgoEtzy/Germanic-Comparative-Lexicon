# *knippōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/knipp%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *knippōną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ǵnibʰ-néh₂-ti, an iterative verb from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneybʰ- (“to pinch”) which also gave rise to the strong verb *knīpaną (“to pinch”). Compare Lithuanian gnýbti (“idem”), žnýbti (“to pinch, snuff a candle”). Whether the root further derives from a Proto-Indo-European *ǵen-, *gen- (“to pinch, squeeze, bend, press, ball up”) (whence perhaps *knudaną (“to knead”)) is unclear.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ǵen-, *ǵneybʰ-, *ǵnibʰ-néh₂-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈknip.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to pinch
2. _(reconstruction)_ to cut, to snap
3. _(reconstruction)_ to wink

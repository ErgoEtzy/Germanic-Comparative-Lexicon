# *klinganą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/klingan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *klinganą

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *glengʰ- (“sound”), perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *gal-, *gelH- (“to call, sound”) (compare *golH-s- (“voice, cry”)), the root whence also *kalzōną, and/or ultimately onomatopoeic, similar to Latin clangō (“to clang”).

From Proto-Indo-European *gley- (“to glue, stick together”). Originally a regular verb assimilated into strong verb class 3.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gal-, *gley-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkliŋ.ɡɑ.nɑ̃/`

# *klappōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/klapp%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *klappōną

### Etymology

Onomatopoeic and influenced by (or related to) verbs such as *klupjaną (“to grip, grasp, clasp”), analyzable as *klappaz (“a hit, clap”) + *-ōną (“denominal suffix”). Compare Proto-Slavic *klepati (“to knock, clap”), Old English clipian (“to make sound with the voice”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-(e)h₂yéti, *-eh₂yéti, *-h₂ti, *-h₂yéti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈklɑp.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ To clap, strike or pound (especially two things against each other)
2. _(reconstruction)_ To make loud noises, especially breathing or pulsating.
3. _(reconstruction)_ To chatter.

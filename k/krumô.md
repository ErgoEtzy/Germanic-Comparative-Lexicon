## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *krumô

### Etymology

Unknown. Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *gru(H)-mon- (“something scraped together, lumber, junk”), from *grew(H)- (“to claw, scratch”), hypothetically an extension of *ger-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gru(H)-mon-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/kru.mɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(alt-of, alternative, feminine, reconstruction)_ alternative form of *krūmô (“fragment; crumb”)

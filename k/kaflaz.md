## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kaflaz

### Etymology

Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *ǵóp-wl̥ ~ *ǵép-uns, from *ǵep- (“to eat, chew”). Cognate with Old Norse kjaptr (“jaw”), from Proto-Germanic *kebutaz both paralleling *habulō (“head”), *haubudą (“head”), from *kap- (“head”). Perhaps also cognate with Proto-Slavic *žьvàti (“to chew”) (whence Serbo-Croatian žvakati), Proto-Celtic *gobbos (“muzzle, snout”) (whence Middle Irish gop). See also Proto-Slavic *zȍbь (“fodder”).

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kutą

### Etymology

Origin uncertain. Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *gewd- (“to stretch, curve, vault”).
Alternatively, of non-Indo-European origin, but possibly borrowed from Uralic; compare Finnish kota (“hut, house”) and Hungarian ház (“house”), both from Proto-Finno-Ugric/Proto-Uralic *kota. However, compare Dutch and English hut.

# *kanhtaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kanhtaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kanhtaz

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. The traditional reconstruction of the Germanic as *kawataz and subsequent derivation from Proto-Indo-European *geh₂w- (“to rejoice”), whence Latin gaudeō (“to rejoice”), is doubtful due to the form of Elfdalian ką̊t requiring a nasal. Based on this, Kroonen reconstructs the term as *kanhtaz, which appears to stem from a Proto-Indo-European *genk- (“to gladden, rejoice”), albeit with no known cognates outside of Germanic.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *geh₂w-, *genk-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkɑ̃ːx.tɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ happy, glad

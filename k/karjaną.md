## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *karjaną

### Etymology

Mayrhofer appears to derive the root from a Proto-Indo-European *ǵewH- (“to force, drive”); see Sanskrit जू (jū, “to press forward”) for more.
Other theories connect the verb to Proto-Indo-European *ger- (“to scrape”) or Proto-Indo-European *gew- (“to turn, bend”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ger-, *gew-, *ǵewH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkɑr.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to scratch, scrape
2. _(reconstruction)_ to sweep

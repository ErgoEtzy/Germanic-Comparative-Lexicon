# *kwinkaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kwinkan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kwinkaną

### Etymology

Uncertain. According to Vladimir Orel, possibly a derived term formed from Proto-Indo-European *(s)gʷes-, the root of Ancient Greek σβέννῡμι (sbénnūmi, “I extinguish, quench”), Proto-Slavic *gasìti (“to extinguish”), Lithuanian gèsti (“to extinguish”), Sanskrit जासयति (jāsáyati, “to exhaust, weaken”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)gʷes-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkʷiŋ.kɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to go out
2. _(reconstruction)_ to vanish, disappear

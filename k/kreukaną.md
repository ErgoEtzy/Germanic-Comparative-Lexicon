# *kreukaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kreukan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kreukaną

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, the verb was associated by the Germanic tribes with the unrelated *kreupaną (“to crawl”) from an early stage. He suggests that the root final -k arose from an earlier geminate -kk, which was adopted from the iterative verb *krukkōną. Related to Old Irish gruc (“wrinkle”); according to Pokorny, both are from Proto-Indo-European *ger- (“to turn, bend, twist”), see also *krukjō (“crutch”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ger-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkreu̯.kɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to fold, wrinkle
2. _(reconstruction)_ to stoop, crawl

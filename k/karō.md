[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kar%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *karō

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *ǵh̥₂reh₂ or *ǵeh₂réh₂, *ǵoh₂réh₂ with pretonic shortening, according to Kroonen, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵeh₂r- (“voice, exclamation”). Cognate with Latin garriō (“to prate, chatter”), Ancient Greek γῆρυς (gêrus, “voice, call, sound”).

From Proto-Indo-European *ger- (“to scrape”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ger-, *ǵeh₂r-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkɑ.rɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ complaint, lament, grievance, moan
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ worry, sorrow
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ care, concern

# *kagô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kag%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kagô

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. Kroonen proposes a derivation from a northern Proto-Indo-European *ǵogʰ-on-, from a root *ǵogʰ- (“shrub”), and adduces Lithuanian žãgaras (“twig”), Latvian žagari (“shrubs”) as cognates. The ultimate origin could be related to English cog, or Old English cǣġ (“key”); see also Proto-West Germanic *kaiju (“peg, key”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ǵogʰ-on-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkɑ.ɣɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ bush
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ stalk, stem
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ tree stump

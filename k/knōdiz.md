## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *knōdiz

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₃-ti-s, from *ǵneh₃- (“to know”). Cognate with Sanskrit ज्ञा॒ति (jñātí) and Latvian znots.
Alternatively, Kroonen proposes derivation from Pre-Germanic *ǵnoh₁tís, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₁-, variant of *ǵenh₁- (“to produce, to beget”). However, this would assume irregular o-grade for a noun on *-tis.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ǵneh₁-, *ǵneh₃-ti-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈknɔː.ðiz/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ lineage, descent, clan

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *knuppaz

### Etymology

From a Proto-Indo-European *gnewbʰ-o-s, from *gnewbʰ-, an extension of *gen- (“to compress”), and cognate with Old Irish gnobh (“knot, knag”), Lithuanian gniáubti (“to embrace”), gniùbti (“to lose firmness, sink”). See also *knudaną (“to knead”), which continues a different extension of the same root.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gnewbʰ-o-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈknup.pɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ lump, clod

# *kruppaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kruppaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kruppaz

### Etymology

Traditionally derived from a Proto-Indo-European *grewb- (“to curve, bend, crawl”), from a root Proto-Indo-European *ger- (“to turn, wind (into a mass)”), and compared with Persian گروه (goruh, “group”), though the reconstruction appears outdated (as more recent research reconstructs the proto-root as *grewbʰ-, see the entry for *kreupaną linked below), and the existence of a more fundamental root *ger- appears quite uncertain.
Kroonen tentatively considers the word an a-stem nominal formation related to *kruppōną (“to crawl”), itself the iterative to *kreupaną (“to crawl, writhe”), via sense development "to bend down and crawl" > "to bunch oneself into a clump-like shape".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ger-, *grewb-, *grewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkrup.pɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a body; mass
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a compressed, round mass; lump; ball
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a group; bunch; collection
4. _(masculine, reconstruction, of harvest)_ a crop; bunch; cluster; head (of grain)
5. _(masculine, reconstruction, of a bird)_ a crop

# *knukô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/knuk%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *knukô

### Etymology

Probably from a Proto-Indo-European *ǵnu-gon-, itself potentially related to *ǵónu (“knee”). The nature of the relation of the root to Lithuanian gniáužti (“to close one's hand”) is unclear.
Older theories derived the root from Proto-Indo-European *gnew- (“a bundle; knot”), extended from the root *gen- (“to pinch; clench; ball up”), with the suffix *-kô used in some names of body parts, and compared with Ancient Greek κονδός (kondós), Ancient Greek κόνδυλος (kóndulos), Sanskrit कन्द (kanda).
In any case, compare Italian gnocco.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gnew-, *ǵnu-gon-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈknu.kɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ joint, bone

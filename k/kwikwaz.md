# *kwikwaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kwikwaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kwikwaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *gʷih₃wós (“alive”).
This word has been supposed to be an example of an h₃w > gʷ > kw shift termed Cowgill's Law (see there for more). However, Kroonen, Steer, and Kölligan give their own alternative explanations without invoking such a sound law:
* Kroonen reconstructs *gʷih₃wós as a reduplicated *gʷigʷh₃wós, with *kwiwaz and other Indo-European relatives dissimilating the second labiovelar to *w; *kwikwaz would be due to irregular preservation or secondary restoration of the reduplication.
* Steer presumes that *kwiwaz came first from a shortening of *ī (<*ih₃) before the once-accented final syllable, and the *-kw- came from assimilation to the word-initial consonant.
* Kölligan rejects derivation of *kwikwaz from *gʷih₃wós altogether, instead proposing a different reduplication *gʷi-gʷh₂-ó-s from *gʷeh₂- (“to step”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷeyh₃-, *gʷih₃wós

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkʷi.kʷɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ alive
2. _(reconstruction)_ lively, quick

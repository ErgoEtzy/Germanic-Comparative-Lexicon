## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kuttô

### Etymology

Of unknown origin. Traditionally derived from a Proto-Indo-European *gudnós, *gʷewd- (“woolen clothes”), and compared with Ancient Greek βεῦδος (beûdos, “costly woman's dress”); however, the Greek is thought to be a loan from Phrygian [script needed] (beudos, “statue of a goddess”), that ultimately traces back to Proto-Indo-European *bʰewdʰ- (“to be awake, aware”), and thus cannot be cognate.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰewdʰ-, *gudnós

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkut.tɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ cowl, woolen cloth, coat

# *kweþuz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kwe%C3%BEuz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kweþuz

### Etymology

Of disputed origin.
Orel, reconstructing the Germanic as *kwiþuz, prefers to derive the term from Proto-Indo-European *gʷih₃-tu-s (and thus formally identical to Proto-Celtic *bitus (“world”)), from *gʷeyh₃- (“to live”), with a similar semantic shift "life" > "belly" as seen in Proto-Slavic *živòtъ (“life; belly”).
Kroonen is skeptical of the putative semantic shift required, and leaves the origin open.
Older theories deriving the word from a Proto-Indo-European *gʷet-, *gūt- (“swelling, rounding; stomach, gut”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʷu-, *gū- (“to bend, curve, bow, camber, vault, distend”), appear formally unconvincing.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷet-, *gʷih₃-tu-s, *gʷu-

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ belly, stomach
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ womb

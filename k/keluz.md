## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *keluz

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to *kelǭ (“throat”). Kluge compares Ancient Greek γαυλός (gaulós, "merchant vessel," originally "milk pail"), which itself has been connected to *keulaz (“ship”), while Orel compares Old Irish gelid (“to swallow, eat”), gaile (“stomach”) (though these two terms are thought to not be cognate with each other).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

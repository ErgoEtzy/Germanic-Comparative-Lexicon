## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kwastuz

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *gwosdos (“piece of wood”), which seems like a nominal derivative from a root *gʷes- (“twig; leafwork”). Compare Proto-Celtic *buzdos (“tail, penis”) (whence Middle Irish bot (“tail, penis”), Welsh both (“hub, nave”)), Albanian gjeth (“leaf, foliage”), Proto-Slavic *gvozdь (“nail, tack, peg”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gwosdos

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkwɑs.tuz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ tuft
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ tassel

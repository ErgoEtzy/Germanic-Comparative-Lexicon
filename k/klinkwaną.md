# *klinkwaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/klinkwan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *klinkwaną

### Etymology

According to Kroonen the verb is closely related to *klinganą (“to cling, to stick”), in his reconstruction both verbs originally had the meaning 'to shrink'. He suggests a connection with Lithuanian gležti (“to become flabby”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkliŋʷ.kʷɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to be afraid, to be shocked, to be appalled
2. _(reconstruction)_ to lament
3. _(reconstruction)_ to cry, to weep

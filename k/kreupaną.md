# *kreupaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kreupan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kreupaną

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, the derivations containing -p(p)- and -b(b)- show that the root was originally Pre-Germanic *kreupp-, from Proto-Indo-European *grewbʰ- (“to crawl”), rather than the traditionally given *grewb-. He gives Lithuanian grubinė́ti (“to stumble”) as a cognate.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *grewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkreu̯.pɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to writhe, twist, wind
2. _(reconstruction)_ to creep, crawl

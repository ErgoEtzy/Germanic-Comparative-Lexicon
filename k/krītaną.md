## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *krītaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *greyd-, *grey- (“to shout”), which appears to be connected to *gerh₂- (“to cry hoarsely; crane”); the assignment of this reconstruction to the level of Proto-Germanic rests on the Crimean Gothic hapax eriten (taken to be a misspelling of *criten or the like). Cognate with Lithuanian gùrti (“to sound soundly”), Middle Irish grith (“a cry”), Welsh gryd (“a scream”), Latin gingriō (“cackle; squawk; quack”, verb). Thought by some to be the origin of Vulgar Latin *crītāre.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *greyd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkriː.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to call out, scream, shout, proclaim

# *kuppaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kuppaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kuppaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Possibly from earlier *kubnáz, from Proto-Indo-European *gubʰ- (“round object, knoll”), from an extension of *gew- (“to bend, curve, arch, vault”). Compare perhaps Latvian gubt (“to bend”), Proto-Slavic *gъbnǫ̀ti (“to bend, flex”), though there are formal issues. However, such an Indo-European root is unlikely to have existed.
Alternatively, borrowed from Proto-Finnic *koppa.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gew-, *gubʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkup.pɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ round object; orb
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ vessel; container; bowl
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ knoll; hilltop; summit
4. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ crown (of the head); harnpan; skull; head

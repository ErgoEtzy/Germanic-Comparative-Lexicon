# *knēaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kn%C4%93an%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *knēaną

### Etymology

Likely from Proto-Indo-European *ǵneh₃- (“to know”), the same root as *kunnaną, but the exact mode of derivation is unclear. It may reflect a back-formation from an older *knō-, which could be an original perfective or stative formation.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ǵneh₃-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈknɛː.ɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to know, recognise, understand

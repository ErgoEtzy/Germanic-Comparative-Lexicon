# *kaizwijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/kaizwijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *kaizwijaną

### Etymology

Uncertain, as the variations between *kaiz- and *kar- cannot be explained as being inherited from Proto-Indo-European. Pokorny compares Old Armenian կքեմ (kkʻem, “to bow, bend”) and Russian жи́хать (žíxatʹ, “to bend”), from Proto-Indo-European *ger- (“to bend, turn”), but this is purely speculative. Kroonen instead derives the verb from a Proto-Indo-European *geys- (“to turn, become”), and compares Hittite [script needed] (kīša(ri) ~ kišanta(ri), “to happen, occur, become”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ger-, *geys-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈkɑi̯.zwi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to turn
2. _(reconstruction)_ to direct

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *knīpaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ǵneybʰ- (“to pinch”). The root final -p was adopted from or influenced by the iterative *knippōną (“to pinch, cut, snap”); see there for more.
Likely related to *hnīpaną (“to pinch, squeeze”), but it's unclear how the variation of the initial consonant arose.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ǵneybʰ-

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to pinch

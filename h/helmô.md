# *helmô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/helm%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *helmô

### Etymology

Usually thought to be from Proto-Indo-European *ḱél-mō, earlier *ḱél-mn̥, from the root *ḱel- (“to cover”); equivalent to *helaną + *-mô. Cognate with Sanskrit शर्मन् (śárman, “shelter, protection”). However, the semantic connection is weak.
An alternative theory by Kroonen links *halmaz and *helmaz (“haulm, straw, stalk, cane”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱélh₂-m(on)-, assuming the original meaning of *helmô was closer to “cane” and referred just to the shaft of a handle implement.
Per Vasmer related to Russian чёлн (čoln) < Proto-Slavic *čьlnъ, Ancient Greek σκαλμός (skalmós).
Note the presence of *(s)- in the related synonyms.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱel-, *ḱél-mō, *ḱélh₂-m(on)-

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ handle, especially for steering a boat: helm, tiller
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ rudder

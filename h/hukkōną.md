# *hukkōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hukk%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hukkōną

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *kuk-néh₂-, a zero-grade iterative to Proto-Indo-European *kewk- (“to bend, curve, elevate”). The strong verb *hūkaną was likely back-formed from the iterative. Related to Sanskrit कुचति (kucáti, “to make crooked”), Lithuanian kaũkas (“lump”), Proto-Slavic *kukati ~ Proto-Slavic *kučati (“to squat”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kewk-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxuk.kɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to squat, sit hunched, cower

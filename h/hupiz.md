## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hupiz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Most simply, from a late Proto-Indo-European *kewb- (“to recline”); cognate with Latin cubō (“lie down, recline”), Latin cubitus (“elbow, bend, arch”), Proto-Celtic *kuxsketi (“to sleep”). Compare however Proto-West Germanic *hubil (“bump, hill”), *hump (“hump, hunch”), Danish huv (“hull”) and English hub, hob, all of uncertain origin. Has been compared to Proto-Germanic *huppōną (“to hop”), for a pre-Germanic or Proto-Indo-European root “to bend”, which has been reconstructed in various ways. More at *kh₂em-, Ancient Greek κύβος (kúbos, “hollow in the hips”), Albanian sup (“shoulder”), Sanskrit शुप्ति (śúpti, “shoulder”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kewb-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxu.piz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ hip, haunch, upper part of the thigh

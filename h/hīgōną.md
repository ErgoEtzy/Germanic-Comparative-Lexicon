# *hīgōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/h%C4%ABg%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hīgōną

### Etymology

Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *ḱey(H)gʰ- (“to move quickly, jump; swift, fierce”), thereby cognate with e.g. Russian сига́ть (sigátʹ, “to spring, jump”) (Proto-Slavic *sigáti) and maybe Sanskrit शीघ्र (śīghrá-, “quick, fast”); if so, Old English may preserve the original sense. Further derived from *ḱey- (“to move”); compare Latin cieō (“set in motion, invoke, provoke”), Ancient Greek κινέω (kinéō, “move, set in motion”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱey(H)gʰ-, *ḱeygʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxiː.ɣɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to breathe (heavily), pant, snort, wheeze
2. _(possibly, reconstruction)_ to move quickly, hasten

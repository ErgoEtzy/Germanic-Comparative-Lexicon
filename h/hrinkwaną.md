## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hrinkwaną

### Etymology

Unclear. According to Pokorny, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kreng-, nasalized variant of *(s)ker- (“to bend”).
Thought to be related to Old High German hranca and Middle Dutch ranke (“tendril, twig”). Furthermore possibly related to Old Church Slavonic крѫгъ (krǫgŭ, “circle”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kreng-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxriŋʷ.kʷɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to bend
2. _(reconstruction)_ to curl
3. _(reconstruction)_ to recoil, to give way

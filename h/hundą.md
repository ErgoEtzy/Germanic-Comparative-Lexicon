# *hundą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hund%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hundą

### Etymology

From earlier *humdan, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱm̥tóm (“hundred”). Cognate with Latin centum, Old Irish cét, Lithuanian šimtas, Sanskrit शत (śatá).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱm̥tóm

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxun.dɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction, with-genitive)_ a great hundred (120)
2. _(neuter, reconstruction, with-genitive)_ a hundred (100)
3. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ a hundred (an administrative division and a group of a hundred men sent out to fight as noted by Tacitus in his Germania)

# *handuz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/handuz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *handuz

### Etymology

Uncertain.
* It may be conjectured to be from pre-Germanic *(k/ḱ)ontús, related to and possibly derived from the strong verb *hinþaną (“to reach for, obtain”).
* It has been suggested to derive from Proto-Indo-European *ḱómt ~ *ḱm̥tés (“hand”), assuming this is also the source of *déḱm̥.
* Seebold connects this to the word family of Ancient Greek κεντέω (kentéō, “to stab”).
* Finally, it is often considered of non-Indo-European origin.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱómt

- IPA: `/ˈxɑn.duz/`

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hawjaną

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kowh₁-éye-ti, a causative o-grade formation from *(s)kewh₁- (“to observe, perceive”). Older theories derived the word from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kew- (“to cut, hew, equip”), comparing Ancient Greek σκεῦος (skeûos, “vessel, device”), or from Proto-Indo-European *kewh₂- (“to cut, hew”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kew-, *(s)kowh₁-éye-ti, *kewh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɑu̯.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to carry out; perform; execute; fulfil

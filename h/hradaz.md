# *hradaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hradaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hradaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Per Kroonen, most likely from Proto-Indo-European *kret- (“to shake, move suddenly”), whence also the verbs *hurtoną and *hrattōną (“to rush, tumble”); cognate with Old Irish crothaim (“I shake”), Latvian kratît (“to shake out”), Lithuanian kratýti (“id.”), Latvian krist (“to fall, drop, die”), Lithuanian kri̇̀sti (“id.”), and Lithuanian kretė́ti (“to tremble (with old age)”).
Formerly derived from Proto-Indo-European *kerd- (compare Ancient Greek κραδάω (kradáō, “I swing, brandish”), Sanskrit कूर्दति (kūrdati, “(s/he) leaps”), Proto-Celtic *kerdeti (“puts, lays; moves”)), but the root codas (PGmc *d ~ *þ versus PIE *d) do not match. Compare Proto-Germanic *herzô (“hinge”) and Old High German scerdo (“hinge”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kerd-, *kret-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxrɑ.ðɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ quick, hasty

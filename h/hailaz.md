# *hailaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hailaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hailaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *kéh₂ilos (“healthy, whole”). Cognate with Proto-Balto-Slavic *káilas (> Proto-Slavic *cělъ (“whole”), Old Prussian kailūstiskan (“health”)), as well as the marginally attested Ancient Greek κοἶλυ (koîlu, “goodness, beauty”).

Probably of similar derivation to the above. Per Matasovic, potentially related to Proto-Brythonic *koɨl (“omen, augury”) (whence Welsh coel (“belief, trust”)), Latin caelum (“sky”), though Kroonen rejects relations to the Italo-Celtic forms.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kéh₂ilos

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɑi̯.lɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ whole, unbroken, intact
2. _(reconstruction)_ entire, complete
3. _(reconstruction)_ hale, healthy, sound
4. _(reconstruction)_ hale, healthy, sound; hail!

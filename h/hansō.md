## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hansō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱómsōd (“union, gathering”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱóm (“beside, by, with, along”) + Proto-Indo-European *sed- (“to sit”). Cognate with Latin consilium (“council, advisory body”); < *consodium, Russian сосе́д (soséd, “neighbour”).
Alternative etymology suggests possible derivation from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ken-, *(s)kend- (“to split off, secede”). If so, then related to *skinþą.
Kroonen (2013, 209-10) suggests that a possible derivation from a derivative of Proto-Indo-European *seh₂- (“to bind”) would be “conceivable.”; He cites as a possible cognate Hittite išḫai ~ išḫii̯anzi from Proto-Indo-European *sh₂-ói-ei ~ sh₂-i-énti.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)ken-, *kom-sh₂-eh₂, *sed-, *seh₂-, *sh₂-ói-ei ~ sh₂-i-énti, *ḱóm, *ḱómsōd

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɑn.sɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ A gathering, summation, mass, quantity, amount
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ A coalition, crowd, multitude, troop

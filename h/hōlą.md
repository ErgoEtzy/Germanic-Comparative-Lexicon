# *hōlą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/h%C5%8Dl%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hōlą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱeh₁l-, *keh₁l- (“to beguile, deceive”).
Cognate with Latin calvor (“deceive, plot against”), calumnia (“false accusation, deceit, defamation”), Russian хвала́ (xvalá, “praise, laud”), Ancient Greek κηλέω (kēléō, “to charm, bewitch, beguile, appease”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱeh₁l-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɔː.lɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ vain speech, flattery
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ vain speech, slander

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hnaskuz

### Etymology

From earlier *hnaskwuz, from Proto-Indo-European *knes- (“to scrape; scratch; rub”), which may be an extension of Proto-Indo-European *ken- (“to scrape; scratch; rub”). Cognate with Lithuanian kni̇̀sti (“to dig up; tear apart”), Sanskrit किक्नस (kiknasa, “crushed grain; meal; grits”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ken-, *knes-

### Pronunciation

### Adjective

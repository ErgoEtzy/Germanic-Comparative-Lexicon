## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *herutaz

### Etymology

From the root Proto-Indo-European *ḱerh₂- (“horn”). Cognate with Lithuanian karvė (“cow”), Russian коро́ва (koróva, “cow, neat”), Latin cervus (“deer, stag”), Welsh carw (“hart, stag”). The Proto-Germanic may contain Kroonen's "Germanic animal suffix" *-utaz. As *-utaz is not generally accepted as an affix in PGmc, it may more likely be from an irregular development of *ḱérh₂tos, with PIE *-H- > PGmc *-u- in non-initial syllables, cf. Old High German anut (“duck”) < PGmc *anud- < Proto-Indo-European *h₂énh₂t-. Regardless, both derivations stem from the same root.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂énh₂t-, *ḱerh₂-

### Noun

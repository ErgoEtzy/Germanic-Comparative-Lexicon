## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hradjaną

### Etymology

Equivalent to *hradaz (“fast”) + *-janą; the original meaning was probably "to be quick", which underwent semantic shift > "to drive, save".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-ye-ti, *-yeti, *-yé-ti, *-yéti, *-éh₁-ti, *-éh₁ti, *-éye-ti, *-éyeti, *kerd-, *kret-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxrɑð.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(Germanic, West, reconstruction)_ to loosen; set free; deliver; rescue; save

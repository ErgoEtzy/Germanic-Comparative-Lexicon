## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *heufaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *kew- (“to howl, scream”). Possibly related to Lithuanian šaukiù and šaũkti (“shout, yell, cry”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kew-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxeu̯.ɸɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to lament, grieve, wail

# *hrēką

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hr%C4%93k%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hrēką

### Etymology

Apparently from a Proto-Indo-European *kreg- (“to spit, caw”), and related to Lithuanian kregė́ti (“to speak loudly, grunt”). Despite Orel, probably not related to Ancient Greek κρώζω (krṓzō, “to croak”), though both terms do appear to be of ultimate sound-symbolic origin.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kreg-

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ saliva
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ snot

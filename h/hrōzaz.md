# *hrōzaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hr%C5%8Dzaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hrōzaz

### Etymology

From pre-Germanic *kroHsos, from Proto-Indo-European *kreHs- (“to stir”). Compare perhaps Proto-Slavic *krāsà (“beauty”); see also the related *hrōzijaną (“to touch, move, stir”) for more potential cognates.
Alternatively a vṛddhi derivative of *hrasaną, an otherwise unreflected strong verb from Proto-Indo-European *kres-; compare *hraznō (“wave”) and the causative *hrōzijaną (“to stir”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kreHs-, *kres-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxrɔː.zɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ active, stirring, moving; agile

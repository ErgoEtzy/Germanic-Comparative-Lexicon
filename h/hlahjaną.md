# *hlahjaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hlahjan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hlahjaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *klek- (“to laugh, cackle”), a seemingly-onomatopoeic root attested in Germanic and Slavic languages. Cognate with Russian клекота́ть (klekotátʹ, “to scream (of birds of prey)”). Possibly related to Proto-Celtic *klokkos (“bell”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *klek-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxlɑx.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to laugh (+ genitive of thing laughed at)

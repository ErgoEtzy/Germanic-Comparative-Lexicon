## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hlaidrijō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱley- (“to lean”) + *-tro- or *-dʰro- + Proto-Germanic *-jō. Cognate with Middle Irish clithar (“hedge”), Ancient Greek κλῖμαξ (klîmax, “ladder”), and related to Gothic 𐌷𐌻𐌴𐌹𐌸𐍂𐌰 (hleiþra, “booth, tent”, literally “that which leans”). Compare also Latin clathri (“trellis”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱley-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxlɑi̯.dri.jɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ ladder

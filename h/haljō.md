# *haljō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/halj%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *haljō

### Etymology

Likely from Proto-Indo-European *ḱel-yo- or *ḱol-yeh₂-, from *ḱel- (“to cover, hide, conceal”), and cognate with *helaną (“to cover”), though note also similar words in Finno-Ugric, including Finnish koljo (“giant”) and Udmurt кыль (kyľ, “evil spirit”), which have been considered as native Uralic words and not borrowings from Germanic.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱel-, *ḱel-yo-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɑl.jɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ the netherworld, the underworld, hell

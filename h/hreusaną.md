## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hreusaną

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *krows- (“to bump, strike, push”); related to Ancient Greek κρούω (kroúō, “to strike”), Lithuanian krùšti (“to trample”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *krows-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxreu̯.sɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to fall
2. _(reconstruction)_ to rush

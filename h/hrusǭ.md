# *hrusǭ

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hrus%C7%AD)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hrusǭ

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *krus- (“crust”). Cognate with Latin crusta (“crust”), Ancient Greek κρύος (krúos, “frost, icy cold”), κρύσταλλος (krústallos, “crystal, ice”), Avestan 𐬑𐬭𐬎𐬰𐬛𐬭𐬀 (xruzdra, “hard”), Sanskrit क्रूड् (krūḍ, “thicken, make hard”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *krews-, *krus-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxru.sɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ ground; crust
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ ice

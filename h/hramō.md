[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hram%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hramō

### Etymology

Uncertain. Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *(s)krem-, which appears to be a derivative of *(s)ker- (“to cut”), and cognate with Proto-Slavic *krōmà (“edge, boundary; slice”). An alternative theory links the root to Proto-Indo-European *ḱremh₂- (“to hang”), whence Ancient Greek κρεμάννῡμῐ (kremánnūmĭ, “to hang up”) (though Beekes is skeptical of this comparison).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)ker-, *(s)krem-, *ḱremh₂-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ frame, framework
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ rack
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ cross
4. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ crossbar, pole

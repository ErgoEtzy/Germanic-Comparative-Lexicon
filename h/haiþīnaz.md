## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *haiþīnaz

### Etymology

From *haiþī (“heath; untilled land”) + *-īnaz (“made of; having the characteristics of”). The sense “heathen, pagan” is a semantic loan from Latin pāgānus (literally “rural, rustic”); the word is attested as a tribal name by Ptolemy long before Christianity had spread among the Germanic peoples.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-iHnos, *kay-t-os, *kayt-

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ from or of uncultivated land
2. _(reconstruction)_ heathen, pagan

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ "a person from uncultivated land"; one of the Chaedini, a Germanic people of western Scandinavia mentioned by Ptolemy in his Geographia
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ heathen, pagan

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hardį̄

### Etymology

From *harduz (“hard”) + *-į̄ (“-th, abstract nominal suffix”).

Of uncertain origin, with a quasi-Indo-European form along the lines of *kort-íh₂-. Kroonen adduces no extra-Germanic cognates and leaves the origin open, while Orel considers the term originally identical with the "rigidity" sense of Etymology 1, referring to the firmness of shoulders.

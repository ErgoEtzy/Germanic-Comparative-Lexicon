## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hurną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱr̥h₂-nó-m, from *ḱerh₂- (“head, horn”). Cognate with Latin cornū, Sanskrit शृङ्ग (ṡṛṅga, “horn”), Old Church Slavonic сръна (srŭna, “roedeer”), Hittite [script needed] (surna, “horn”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱerh₂-, *ḱr̥h₂-nó-m

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxur.nɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ horn

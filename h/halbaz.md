# *halbaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/halbaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *halbaz

### Etymology

Apparently from a pre-Germanic *kolp-ó-s, of unclear further origin. Possibly from a p-extension of Proto-Indo-European *(s)kelH- (“to cut”) (through a sense “divided”), and cognate with Sanskrit कॢप् (kḷp, “to manage, succeed, be fit”) (though Kroonen is skeptical due to the wide semantic gap), or from a stem *ḱol-bʰo-, and cognate with Lithuanian šali̇̀s (“side, strip of land”). Alternatively, a recent theory suggests borrowing from Proto-Finnic *halpa, *halba- (“cheap, reduced”) (compare Finnish halpa (“cheap”), Estonian halb (“bad”)), itself possibly from Proto-Germanic *salwaz (“sallow, dirty”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kelH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɑl.βɑz/`

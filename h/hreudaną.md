## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hreudaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *krewH-dʰh₁-e-ti, from Proto-Indo-European *krewH- (“to heap up”) and cognate with Lithuanian kráuju, kráuti (“to put on top of each other”) and Proto-Slavic *krỳti (“to cover”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *krewH-, *krewH-dʰh₁-e-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxreu̯.ðɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to cover
2. _(reconstruction)_ to adorn, to decorate

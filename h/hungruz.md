## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hungruz

### Etymology

From pre-Proto-Germanic *kunkr-ú-s, derived from some Proto-Indo-European zero-grade r-stem *kn̥k-r- (perhaps with the suffix *-rós, but the Germanic u-stem noun is suspicious), from the root *kenk- (“to hunger, ache”). Cognate with Lithuanian keñkras (“lean, emaciated”), keñkti (“to ache”), Ancient Greek κέγκει (kénkei, “is hungry”), κάγκανος (kánkanos, “arid, barren”), and possibly further with Hittite 𒅗𒀀𒉌𒅔𒋾 (/⁠kāninti⁠/, “thirst”, loc.sg.). Note also strong resemblance with Sanskrit काङ्क्षति (kāṅkṣati, “to desire”), though a formal relation appears difficult.

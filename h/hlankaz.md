## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hlankaz

### Etymology

Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *kleng-, *klenk- (“to bend; wind; twist”), though with no certain cognates outside Germanic. Possible extra-Germanic comparanda include Latin clingō (“to gird”) (which is only marginally attested), Tocharian B kleṅke (“vehicle”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kleng-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxlɑŋ.kɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ flexible; bendsome
2. _(reconstruction)_ slim; slender; sleek

# *hurhwą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hurhw%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hurhwą

### Etymology

Possibly from a Pre-Germanic *kr̥k-wo-, which Kroonen suggests might be connected with Middle Irish corcach (“moor”), for a Proto-Indo-European *kork-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kork-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxur.xʷɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ dirt, mucus, dirty substance

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hnappōną

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *knobʰnéh₂-, an o-grade iterative to Proto-Indo-European *knebʰ- (“to pick”). Related to Lithuanian knabt (“to peck”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *knebʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxnɑp.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to pick, pluck, tear off

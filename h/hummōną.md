# *hummōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/humm%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hummōną

### Etymology

Inherited from Proto-Indo-European *km̥H-néh₂-ti, probably onomatopoeic from a root *kemH- (“to hum”). Cognate with Lithuanian ki̇̀mti (“wheeze, become hoarse”), kimùs (“hoarse”), Proto-Slavic *čьmèľь (“bumblebee”), *komãrъ (“mosquito”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *km̥H-néh₂-ti

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to hum

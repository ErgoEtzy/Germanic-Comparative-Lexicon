# *hrōzijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hr%C5%8Dzijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hrōzijaną

### Etymology

A causative formation, analyzable as *hrōzaz (“active, stirring, moving”) + *-janą. Possibly inherited from Proto-Indo-European *kroHs-éye-ti: according to Kroonen, a direct cognate of Avestan 𐬟𐬭𐬁-𐬑𐬭𐬃𐬢𐬵𐬀𐬌𐬌𐬀 (frā-xrā̊ŋhaiia, “to be shocked”), and according to Orel, of Proto-Slavic *krasiti (“to ornate”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kroHs-éye-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxrɔː.zi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to stir

# *haupaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/haupaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *haupaz

### Etymology

From earlier *hauppaz, reflecting pre-Germanic *kouHp-nó- or *keh₂up-nó-, probably part of an ablauting n-stem paradigm *haufô ~ *huppaz, from which *hūpô also split off; ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂wp- (“heap, pile”), a stem of unusual formation. Related to Avestan 𐬐𐬀𐬊𐬟𐬀 (kaofa, “mountain, hump”), Old Irish cúan (“pile”), Lithuanian kaũpas (“heap”), Proto-Slavic *kupъ (“heap”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *keh₂wp-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɑu̯.pɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, West, masculine, reconstruction)_ heap, pile
2. _(Germanic, West, masculine, reconstruction)_ crowd, throng

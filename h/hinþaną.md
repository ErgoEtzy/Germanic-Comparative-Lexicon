# *hinþaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hin%C3%BEan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hinþaną

### Etymology

Possibly from a Proto-Indo-European *ḱent- (“to reach, sting”) (alternatively reconstructed as *kent-). While Kroonen adduces no cognates, Orel compares Ancient Greek κεντέω (kentéō, “to sting, goad”), which is supported by Beekes; see the Greek for more cognates.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱent-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxin.θɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to reach for, obtain, catch

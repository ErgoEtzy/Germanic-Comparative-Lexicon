[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hr%C4%ABdr%C7%AD)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hrīdrǭ

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *kréydʰrom, from the root *krey- (“to divide; part; separate; sift”). Cognate with Latin crībrum (“sieve, riddle”), Old Irish criathar (“sieve”), Old Welsh cruitr (“winnowing-shovel”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kréydʰrom

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxriːð.rɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ sieve

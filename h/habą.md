## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *habą

### Etymology

As if from Proto-Indo-European *kap-ó-m. Derived from the root *kap- (“to seize; take up; lift”). The likely original abstract meaning “lift, lifting”—still perceivable in derivatives like Old Norse upphaf (“beginning”) and Old High German urhab (“cause, reason”)—was concretized to “sea” because of the way the sea swell lifts ships.

The original sense of the above, synchronically analyzed as a nominal formation from *habjaną (“to life, heave”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

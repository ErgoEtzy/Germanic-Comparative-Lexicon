# *huzdą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/huzd%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *huzdą

### Etymology

Uncertain. Possibly from pre-Germanic *kudʰ-tó-m, with *-zd- perhaps arising from a special Germanic case of Bartholomae's law (but *-ss- is expected; cf. *wissiz < *wédʰtis), from Proto-Indo-European *kewdʰ- (“to cover, conceal, hide”) (compare Ancient Greek κεύθω (keúthō, “to cover, hide”) and Proto-Brythonic *kʉðɨd (“to hide”)) + *-tóm, thus the original meaning would be “that which is concealed or hidden”.
Compare typologically Russian сокро́вище (sokróvišče) (< сокры́ть (sokrýtʹ), akin to скрыва́ть (skryvátʹ)).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kewH-, *-tós, *kewdʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxuz.dɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ hidden treasure

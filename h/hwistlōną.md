# *hwistlōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hwistl%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hwistlōną

### Etymology

Traditionally derived from a Proto-Germanic root *hwis- (“to hiss”), from a Proto-Indo-European *ḱweys-, *ḱwey- (“to hiss, whistle, whisper”), and compared with *hwīnaną (“to whiz, whine”), Latin quiritō (“scream, screech, shriek, squall”). However, it appears more likely that *hwis- and its derivatives are onomatopoeic innovations within Germanic. Note similarities to Proto-Indo-European *ḱwes- (“to puff, sigh”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱwes-, *ḱwey-, *ḱweys-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxʷist.lɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to whistle, hiss

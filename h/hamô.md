## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hamô

### Etymology

Uncertain; possibly from unattested strong verb *hamaną + *-ô (compare Norse forms), perhaps cognate with Sanskrit शामूल (śāmūla, “woolen shirt”), seemingly from Proto-Indo-European *ḱem- (“to cover?”). Hyllested instead suggests borrowing from Proto-Finnic *haamoi.

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱom-o-, from *ḱem- (“to restrain; shaft, collar”). Cognate with Sanskrit शम्या (śámyā, “yoke pin, plug, wedge”), Avestan 𐬯𐬌𐬨𐬁 (simā, “yoke pin”), Ancient Greek κάμαξ (kámax, “pole, bar, shaft”), Old Armenian սամիք (samikʻ, “facewoods of a yoke”).

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ shape, physical form
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ wrapping, binding
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ skin, flesh
4. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ garment, clothing

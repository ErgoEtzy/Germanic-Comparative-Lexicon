## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hērą

### Etymology

Perhaps derived from Proto-Indo-European *kēs-ró-m, a lengthened-grade formation from *kes- (“to scrape, comb”), and compared in particular to Old Irish cír (“comb”). Alternatively, Gasiorowski derives the Germanic from an earlier *hezra-, with loss of -z- before -r- and compensatory lengthening of the vowel, from Pre-Germanic *kes-ró-m (“that which is combed”), substantivization of an adjective *kes-ro-s, ultimately from the same root *kes- as above.
Older theories traced the word to a Proto-Indo-European *ḱer-, *ḱeres- (“rough hair, bristle”), though this leaves the lengthened grade unaccounted for.
Despite superficial phonetic and exact semantic similarities, not related to Old Armenian հեր (her, “hair”).

# *hnītaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hn%C4%ABtan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hnītaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *kneyd- (“to scratch, knock”), sometimes considered a derivative of a root *ken- (“to scratch; scrape; rub”). Compare Ancient Greek κνῐ́ζω (knĭ́zō, “to scratch, pound”), Middle Irish cned (“wound”), Latvian knidēt (“to itch”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *kneyd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxniː.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to push; knock; butt against

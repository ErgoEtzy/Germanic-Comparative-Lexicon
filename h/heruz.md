## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *heruz

### Etymology

From a Proto-Indo-European *ḱérus or *ḱérh₂us (“pointed stick, arrow, spear, sword”), possibly from the root *ḱerh₂- (“head; horn”) or from *(s)ker- (“to cut”). Cognate with Sanskrit शरु (śáru, “missile, dart, arrow”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱérus

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxe.ruz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ sword, dagger

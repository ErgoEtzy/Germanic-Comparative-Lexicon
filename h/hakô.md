## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hakô

### Etymology

Appears to stem from a pre-Germanic *kh₂/₃k-on-, with no certain cognates outside of Germanic, due to a degree of semantic uncertainty with words of similar sound and meaning in other branches. Orel compares Hittite [script needed] (kaka-, “tooth”) and Proto-Slavic *kogъtь (“claw, talon”). The Germanic has been traditionally reconstructed further to a Proto-Indo-European *keg-, *keng- (“peg; hook”), and, in addition to the Slavic, compared with Persian چنگ (čang, “claw, talon”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *keg-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxɑ.kɔːː/`

# *hwīnaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/hw%C4%ABnan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *hwīnaną

### Etymology

Mechanically, from a Proto-Indo-European *ḱwey- (“to hiss, whistle, whisper”), which appears to be onomatopoeic, and of similar formation to *ḱwes- (“to puff, sigh”). Possibly related to Proto-Slavic *kviliti, *kvilěti (“to neigh, to squeak, to wail”). See also *hwistlōną (“to whistle”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ḱwey-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈxʷiː.nɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to rush, make a rushing sound, whizz, hiss, squeal, whine

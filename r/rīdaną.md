# *rīdaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/r%C4%ABdan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rīdaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *Hreydʰ- (“to set in motion, ride”), likely a suffixed form of *h₃reyH- (“to move, churn”).
Cognate with Proto-Celtic *reideti (“to ride”) (whence Old Irish réidid (“to ride, drive”), ríad (“ride”, noun)), Proto-Celtic *reidis (“easy, smooth”), Lithuanian riedė́ti (“to roll”), raidù (“quick”), and Latvian raĩdît (“to send hastily, urge, (reflexive) hurry”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *Hreydʰ-, *h₃réydʰh₁eti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈriː.ðɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to ride

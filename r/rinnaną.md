# *rinnaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/rinnan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rinnaną

### Etymology

Two main etymological pathways have been proposed.
* Most likely from the root Proto-Indo-European *h₃reyH- (“to undulate, churn”), via either a nasal-infix present *h₃ri-né-H-ti or a *-néwti present *h₃riH-néw-ti.
** These forms are supported by cognates Sanskrit रिणाति (rinā́ti, “to make turbulent, cause to flow”), Ancient Greek ὀρί̄νω (orí̄nō, “to churn up”), and Russian ри́нуться (rínutʹsja, “to rush”).
** Under this etymology, further cognates outside Germanic include Middle Irish rian (“river, way”), Proto-Slavic *rěka (“river”), Latin rīvus (“stream”), and Gaulish Rēnos (“that which flows”), which is the source of the name of the river Rhine.
* Alternatively from Proto-Indo-European *h₃r̥-néw-ti (“to flow, move, run”)., whence also Sanskrit ऋति (ṛti, “course, way”).
Either way, both paths ultimately trace back to the same root, *h₃er- (“to move, stir”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₃er-, *h₃reyH-, *h₃r̥-néw-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈrin.nɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to run, to rush (move quickly)
2. _(reconstruction, of liquid)_ to flow

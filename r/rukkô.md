[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/rukk%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rukkô

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. Probably from Proto-Indo-European *h₃rewk- (“to dig, till”), via a secondary formation. Other theories derive the term from Proto-Indo-European *rukn-, *rÁkn- (“weaving, web”), from Proto-Indo-European *ruk-, *rug- (“to spin, make clothes”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₃rewk-, *ruk-, *rukn-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈruk.kɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ spinning wheel, distaff

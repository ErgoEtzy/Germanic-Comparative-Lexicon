# *rūskōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/r%C5%ABsk%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rūskōną

### Etymology

Uncertain, though apparently onomatopoeic of leaves moving in the wind.
Others have taken the word as a variant (with formative k) of *rūsōną (“to storm, whiz, roar, make a noise, be fierce”), from Proto-Indo-European *Hrews-, *HrewH- (“to move, drive, agitate”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *Hrews-

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to make a noise; whiz; whirr
2. _(reconstruction)_ to startle

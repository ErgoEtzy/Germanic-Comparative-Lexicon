# *raubijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/raubijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *raubijaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *Hrewp- (“to snatch”). It is perhaps inherited from Proto-Indo-European *Hrowp-éye-ti, whence also possibly Sanskrit रोपयति (ropáyati). Kroonen also suggests that the verb may have emerged from a merger of:
* An intensive formation equivalent to *reufaną (“to tear”) + *-janą.
* A denominal formation equivalent to *raubaz (“robbery”) + *-janą.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *Hrewp-, *Hrowp-éye-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈrɑu̯.βi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to rob, steal, plunder
2. _(reconstruction)_ to break, tear off

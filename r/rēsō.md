## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rēsō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₁reh₁s- (“to flow, rush”). Related to Ancient Greek ἐρωή (erōḗ, “rush, impulse”); see there for more cognates.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₁reh₁s-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈrɛː.sɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a running
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a course
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a rush (of water)

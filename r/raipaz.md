# *raipaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/raipaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *raipaz

### Etymology

Apparently from a quasi-Indo-European *h₁royp-nó-s (“band, strip, strap”), of uncertain origin. Kroonen considers the term limited to Germanic, but others have further derived it from Proto-Indo-European *h₁reyp- (“to peel off, rip, tear; strip, strap”), though if so, the irregular sound shift from PIE *-p- > PGm *-p- rather than PGm *-f- remains to be explained.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₁reyp-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈrɑi̯.pɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ strip; margin
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ strap; band; rein
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ cord; rope; cable
4. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ circular band; circlet; ringlet

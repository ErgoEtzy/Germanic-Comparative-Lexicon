## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rastō

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, from the same root as *rēsōną (“to rush”) and *rēsō (“running, course, rush”), from Proto-Indo-European *Hre(h₁)s- (“to flow, rush”) + *-teh₂. Alternatively compared to the root *h₁reh₁- (“to rest”), though Kroonen does not favor this latter theory.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-teh₂, *Hre(h₁)s-, *Hreh₁s-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ interval
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ rest

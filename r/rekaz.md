## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rekaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₃rḗǵs (“chief, king”). Cognate with Latin rēx (“king”). Doublet of *rīks which is an early borrowing from Proto-Celtic.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₃reǵ-, *h₃rḗǵs

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈre.kɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ A ruler, chief
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ A king
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ A prince

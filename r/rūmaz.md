# *rūmaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/r%C5%ABmaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *rūmaz

### Etymology

From pre-Germanic *(H)ruHmos, an adjectival mo-stem from Proto-Indo-European *(H)rewH- (“to tear open”). Related to Tocharian A räw- (“to open”), Avestan 𐬭𐬀𐬎𐬎𐬀𐬵- (rauuah-, “open space”), Latin rūs (“country”), Old Irish róe (“field, open land”), Old Church Slavonic равьнь (ravĭnĭ, “open”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(H)rewH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈruː.mɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ roomy, spacious, open

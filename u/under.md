## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *under

### Etymology

From a merger of two originally distinct prepositions as a result of Verner's law: Pre-Germanic *untér and *undʰér, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁n̥tér (“inside”), from which also Latin inter; and *(H)n̥dʰér (“under”), whence Latin īnfrā, Sanskrit अधस् (adhás, “below”), Ossetian дӕлӕ (dælæ, “below”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(H)n̥dʰér, *h₁entér, *h₁n̥tér

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈun.der/`

### Preposition

1. _(reconstruction, with-dative)_ among, between
2. _(reconstruction, with-dative)_ under, beneath (location)
3. _(reconstruction, with-accusative)_ under (direction)

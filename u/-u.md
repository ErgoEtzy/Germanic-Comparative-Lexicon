## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -u

### Etymology

Possibly from Pre-Germanic *-we, from Proto-Indo-European *-we, which would make it cognate to Latin -ve, Ancient Greek ἤ (ḗ), Tocharian B wa and Sanskrit वा (vā).
Alternatively, from *h₂u, whence possibly also Ancient Greek αὖ (aû) and Sanskrit उ (u, “and”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-we

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/u/`

### Particle

1. _(East, Germanic, reconstruction)_ Added to the end of a word in a phrase (usually the first word) to make it a question.

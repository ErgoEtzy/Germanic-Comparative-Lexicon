# *ungwô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/ungw%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ungwô

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂éngʷʰis, with a change to an n-stem (pre-Germanic *h₂éngʷʰō ~ *h₂n̥gʷʰnés) and subsequently leveled to the weak stem. The original form of the Germanic reflex of this root, *angw-, is better preserved in the related word (Elbe Germanic) *angraz. See the reconstruction notes.
Likely already rare or poetic in Proto-Germanic, as it came to be completely displaced by synonymous words for “snake” in all attested Germanic languages except for Old High German (where it had already begun to shift to referring to other reptiles and amphibians).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂engʷʰ-, *h₂éngʷʰis

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, West, masculine, reconstruction, uncommon)_ snake; snakelike animal

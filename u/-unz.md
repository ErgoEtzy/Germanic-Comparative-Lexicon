## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -unz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *-ums (u-stem accusative plural), from *-us (u-stem suffix) + *-ms (accusative plural suffix); compare Old Church Slavonic -ꙑ (-y), Ancient Greek -ῡς (-ūs) (dialectal -ῠνς (-ŭns)), Latin -ūs, Lithuanian -us, and Sanskrit -ऊन् (-ūn).

From the bare Proto-Indo-European accusative plural suffix *-m̥s; compare Ancient Greek -ᾰς (-ăs), Latin -ēs, and Sanskrit -अः (-aḥ).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-ms, *-ums, *-us

### Suffix

1. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Forms the accusative plural of masculine/feminine u-stem nouns.
   - _sunuz (“son”) + *-unz → *sununz (“sons”)_

### Suffix 2

1. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Forms the accusative plural of masculine/feminine consonant stem nouns.
   - _gans (“goose”) + *-unz → *gansunz (“geese”)_

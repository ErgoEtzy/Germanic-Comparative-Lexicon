# *ūdarą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/%C5%ABdar%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ūdarą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₁éwHdʰr̥, *h₁uHdʰ-n- (“udder”), a paradigmatic variant of *h₁ówHdʰr̥; either reflecting different thematicized derivatives thereof or, according to Kroonen, inherited with the ablaut intact (see §Inflection below) but with leveling of the *-r- throughout, with a possible athematic form in *-ur (< PIE *-r̥) having survived long enough to account for the North Germanic reflexes. Cognate with Latin ūber, Sanskrit ऊधर् (ū́dhar).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

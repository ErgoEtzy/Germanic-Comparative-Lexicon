## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *uber

### Etymology

From earlier ufér, from Proto-Indo-European *upér, *supér, ultimately from the root *upo. Cognate with Ancient Greek ὑπέρ (hupér, “above”), Latin super (“above”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *upér

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈu.βer/`

### Preposition

1. _(reconstruction)_ over (moving across)
2. _(reconstruction)_ over, above (positioned above)

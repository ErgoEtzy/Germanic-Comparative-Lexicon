## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *unþī

### Etymology

Probably from Pre-Germanic *h₂n̥tih₂, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂en- (“to draw water”). Compare Ancient Greek ἄντλος (ántlos, “hold of a ship, bilgewater”), Hittite 𒄩𒀀𒉌 (ḫa-a-ni, “to draw (liquids)”). Not related to Latin unda (“wave”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂en-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈun.θiː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ wave

# *atberaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/atberan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *atberaną

### Etymology

PIE word
 *h₂éd
From *at + *beraną.
The formation may be older, as identical constructions are found in other Indo-European languages: compare Proto-Italic *adferō (whence Latin adferō, later afferō), Proto-Celtic *adbereti, Phrygian αββερετ (abberet), Proto-Iranian *ābarati (whence e.g. Younger Avestan 𐬁𐬠𐬀𐬭𐬀𐬌𐬙𐬌 (ābaraⁱti)), Sanskrit आभरति (ābharati).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bʰer-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˌɑt.ˈbe.rɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to bear to, bring forward

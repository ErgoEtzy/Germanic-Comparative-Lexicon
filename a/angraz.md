## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *angraz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂eng- (“corner”), perhaps in the sense “narrow meadow beside waterway”.

A North-European word probably from *h₂éngʷʰis (“snake, serpent, larva”) (whence Latin anguis, Lithuanian angi̇̀s, Old Armenian աւձ (awj)) or related to it via the root, *h₂engʷʰ-; compare Old High German unc (“snake, serpent; (later) newt, lizard; toad”). Cognate with Proto-Balto-Slavic *angurjás (“eel”) (Proto-Slavic *ǫgořь /*ǫgъřь, Old Prussian angurgis, Old Lithuanian ungurỹs). Given the etymology, likely originally referred to the larval form.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *aganą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂eh₂ógʰe (“to have become afraid”), the perfect of *h₂egʰ- (“to become afraid, saddened”). The full-grade root *ōg- served as the basis for the Gothic infinitive, while the zero grade survived in other petrified forms, compare 𐌰𐌲𐌹𐍃 (agis). Cognate with Old Irish ad·ágadar, Old English eġe, Ancient Greek ἄχος (ákhos, “pain, grief”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂egʰ-, *h₂eh₂ógʰe

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑ.ɣɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to fear, to be afraid

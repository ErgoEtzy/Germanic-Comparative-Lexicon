## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ardiz

### Etymology

Of disputed origin:
* From Proto-Indo-European *h₂ér-ti-s ~ *h₂r̥-téy-s, from *h₂er- (“to join, fit together”); see also *armaz (“arm”), Latin ars (“art”).
* Alternatively (but semantically more difficult), from a specialized use of *ardiz, a by-form of *arþiz (“plough(ing); acre, farmland; crop, produce”) (compare Old Norse ǫrð, Old English irð, Old High German art), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂erh₃- (“to plough”) (whence also *arjaną (“to plow”) and *arþrą (“plow”); compare Latin area).

# *akaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/akan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *akaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂éǵeti, from *h₂eǵ- (“to drive”). Cognate with Latin agō (“drive”), Ancient Greek ἄγω (ágō, “direct, drive, lead, draw, go”), Sanskrit अजति (ájati, “he drives”).

According to Orel and Seebold identical to the above verb “to drive”. Compare Latin agitō in the sense “to disturb, torment” for a similar development.
Kroonen prefers to reconstruct the proto-form as *h₂eǵ- and adduces as cognates, in contrast to the above, Sanskrit आजि (ājí, “race, competition, fight”), Avestan 𐬁𐬰𐬌 (āzi, “greed, desire”), Persian آز (âz, “greed”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂éǵeti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑ.kɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ to drive, sail, navigate

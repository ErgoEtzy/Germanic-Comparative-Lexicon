# *ammǭ

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/amm%C7%AD)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ammǭ

### Etymology

A Lallwort ultimately of onomatopoeic or sound-symbolic origin. Compare Latin amita (“paternal aunt”), Ancient Greek ἀμμά (ammá, “mother”). Similar words for “mother” occur sporadically in many languages, such as the probably unrelated Hindi अम्मा (ammā) (from Sanskrit अ॒म्बा (ambā́) and/or Proto-Dravidian *amma) and Proto-Semitic *ʔimm-, *ʔumm-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑm.mɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ mother
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ wet nurse
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ grandmother

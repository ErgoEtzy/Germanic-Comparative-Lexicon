[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/ai%C3%BEaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *aiþaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Probably related to Proto-Celtic *oitos (“oath”), which may imply an inheritance from Proto-Indo-European *Hóytos. This term has itself been connected with the root *h₁ey-, which would entail a reconstruction *h₁óytos. Kroonen, however, doubts the PIE status of the word, given that it is perhaps confined to two neighboring Indo-European branches that existed in a shared cultural zone. It is, however, possible that the term more broadly connects to Ancient Greek οἶτος (oîtos, “fate, doom”), though Beekes provides an alternative etymology for the Greek term.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *Hóytos

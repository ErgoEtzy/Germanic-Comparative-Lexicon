## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *aldǭ

### Etymology

Possibly cognate with Proto-Slavic *oldi (“boat”), Lithuanian eldijà (“hollowed out tree trunk; canoe”), pointing to Proto-Indo-European *Holdʰ-eh₂, *h₂eldʰ-eh₂, though ultimately of unknown origin. Semantically, compare Proto-Germanic *trugaz (“trough”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dóru (“tree, wood”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *Holdʰ-eh₂, *dóru

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑl.dɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ hollow (of a tree)
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ trough

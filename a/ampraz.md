## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ampraz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *amrós (probably *h₂h₃mrós; see *h₂eh₃mós). Cognate to Sanskrit अम्ल (amlá, “sour; wood-sorrel”) and Latin amārus (“bitter”). Kroonen, skeptical, compares Semitic (e.g. Hebrew מַר (mar, “bitter”)).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *amrós

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ sharp (in taste), sour

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -ai

### Etymology

Two main etymologies have been suggested:
* From Proto-Indo-European *-oy (o-stem dative singular), from *-os (o-stem suffix) + *-i (locative singular suffix), paralleling the derivation of other dative singulars from PIE locatives; furthermore, Ringe suggests that *-oey would become **-ōi.
* From Proto-Indo-European *-oey (o-stem locative singular), from *-os (o-stem suffix) + *-ey (dative singular suffix), allowing the early Old English instrumental singular fācni (“using a torch”) to be explained as an Proto-Indo-European locative singular *-ey without awkwardly positing that one form of the locative became a dative and another an instrumental.

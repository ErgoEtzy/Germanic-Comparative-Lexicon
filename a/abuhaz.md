# *abuhaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/abuhaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *abuhaz

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *ápōkʷos, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂époh₃kʷos, with alteration based on adjectives in *-ugaz. The term is ultimately from a compound of *h₂epó (“away from”) + *h₃ekʷ- (“eye”). Cognate with Sanskrit अपाञ्च् (ápāñc, “located backwards, behind”), Proto-Slavic *opakъ (“inverted”). The word would be, by respective roots, morphologically equivalent (but not semantically) to French aveugle (“blind”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂epó, *h₂époh₃kʷos, *h₃ekʷ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑ.βu.xɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ turned the wrong way, inverted
2. _(reconstruction)_ wicked, bad

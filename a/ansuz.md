## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ansuz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂émsus, from *h₂ems- (“to engender, beget”). Compare Hittite 𒈗𒍑 (ḫa-aš-šu-uš /⁠ḫaššuš⁠/, “king”), Old Avestan 𐬀𐬵𐬎 (ahu) / Younger Avestan 𐬀𐬢𐬵𐬎 (aŋhu, “lord”), Sanskrit असुर (ásura, “godlike, powerful”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂émsus

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑn.suz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ deity, god
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ name of the rune ᚨ (a)

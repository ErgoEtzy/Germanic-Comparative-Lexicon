# *aiwukiz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/aiwukiz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *aiwukiz

### Etymology

A derivation from *aiwaz (“age, eternity”), *aiwō. The *-k- suffix is uncertain, but Kroonen favors a comparison with Latin iūgis (“continual”) and Ancient Greek ὑγιής (hugiḗs, “healthy, sound, wholesome”) from Proto-Indo-European *h₂yew-gʷih₃- and *h₂yu-gʷih₃- respectively, to assume a reconstruction of pre-Germanic *h₂oyu-gʷih₃- with an original meaning of “ever-living”.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂ey-, *h₂yew-gʷih₃-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑi̯.wu.kiz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ eternal, lasting forever

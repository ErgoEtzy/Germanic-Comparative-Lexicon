[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/agluz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *agluz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂egʰ-l-u-, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂egʰ- (“repulsive, offensive, hateful, disgusting”). Cognate with Old Irish áil (“ignomy, insult”), Ancient Greek ἀχλύς (akhlús, “manure, dung”), Ancient Greek ὀχλέω (okhléō, “to vex, harass”), Sanskrit अघल (aghala, “bad, terrible”), Sanskrit अघ (agha, “evil, pain”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂egʰ-, *h₂egʰ-l-u-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑɣ.luz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ cumbersome, tedious, tiresome, burdensome

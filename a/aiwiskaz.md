## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *aiwiskaz

### Etymology

Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eygʷʰ- + *-iskos; for the root, compare Sanskrit अनेहस् (an-ehás, “flawless, unrivalled”) (< *n̥-h₂eygʷʰós).
Formerly often compared with Ancient Greek αἶσχος (aîskhos), but this hypothesis has largely been abandoned due to its formal difficulty.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂eygʷʰ-

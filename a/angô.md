# *angô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/ang%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *angô

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂énk-ō, from *h₂enk- (“curve, bend”).

Cognate with Old Armenian անձն (anjn, “soul, person”), possibly from Proto-Indo-European *h₂én(h₁)ǵʰō or *h₂en(h₁)ǵʰḗn (“breath”), from a suffixed/extended form of the root *h₂enh₁- (“to breathe”); equivalent to *ananą + *-gô.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂enh₁-, *h₂enk-, *h₂én(h₁)ǵʰō, *h₂énk-ō

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑŋ.ɡɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a bend; crook
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ hook; barb; angle
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ spear; dart
4. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ arrow

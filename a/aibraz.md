## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *aibraz

### Etymology

Apparently from a Proto-Indo-European *h₂eybʰros (“sharp, severe, astringent”). Kroonen assigns no extra-Germanic cognates, giving only German Eifer (“zeal, eagerness”) as a possible relation, while others have suggested cognacy with dialectal Lithuanian aibrùmas (“waters in the mouth”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂eybʰros

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑi̯.βrɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ sharp, bitter
2. _(reconstruction)_ vehement, dire
3. _(reconstruction)_ violent

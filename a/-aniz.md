## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -aniz

### Etymology

Inherited from Proto-Indo-European *-ones, from *-ō (masculine n-stem suffix) + *-es (nominative/vocative plural suffix).
Compare Ancient Greek -ονες (-ones) and Sanskrit -आनस् (-ānas).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-es, *-ones, *-ō

### Suffix

1. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Forms the nominative and vocative plurals of masculine an-stem nouns.
   - _bugô (“bow”) + *-aniz → *buganiz (“bows”)_

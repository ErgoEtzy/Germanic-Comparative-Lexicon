## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ambô

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₃émbʰō ~ *h₃m̥bʰnés (< *h₃énbʰ-on-), variant of *h₃nobʰ- (“hub, nave”), whence also *nabō (“nave”) and *nabalô ~ *nabulô (“navel”). Compare Latin umbō (“boss (of a shield)”) (< *h₃nbʰ-n-).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₃émbʰō ~ *h₃m̥bʰnés

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑm.bɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ belly

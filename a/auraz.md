## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *auraz

### Etymology

Uncertain; possibly a secondary vṛddhi full-grade of Proto-Indo-European *Huh₁-ró-s (compare Lithuanian jáura (“marsh”), jū́ra (“sea”), Old Prussian wurs (“pool”)), from *Hweh₁- (“water”) + *-rós. Cognate with Sanskrit वार् (vā́r, “water; pond, pool”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *Huh₁-ró-s, *Hweh₁-, *rós

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑu̯.rɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ wet sand or earth, mud
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ liquid, water, sea

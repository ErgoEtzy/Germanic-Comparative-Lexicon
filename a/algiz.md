## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *algiz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₁élḱis, *h₁ólḱis (“elk”), from *h₁el- (“deer”). Cognate with Vedic Sanskrit ऋश्य (ṛ́śya, “antelope”), Lithuanian elnias (“deer, stag, hart”), Russian оле́нь (olénʹ, “deer”), Russian лось (losʹ, “elk”), Ancient Greek ἔλαφος (élaphos, “deer”), Old Armenian եղն (ełn, “hind”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₁élḱis

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑl.ɣiz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ elk
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ name of the rune ᛉ (z)
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ Alcis (in the plural), a pair of divine brothers allegedly worshipped by the Naharvali according to Tacitus.

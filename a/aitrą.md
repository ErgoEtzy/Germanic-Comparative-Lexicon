## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *aitrą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂eyd-ro-m, from *h₂eyd- (“to swell; swelling, tumour, abscess”). Cognate with Ancient Greek οἶδος (oîdos, “tumour, swelling”) and the first element *οἰδῐ́- (*oidĭ́-) of the name Οἰδῐ́πους (Oidĭ́pous, “Oedipus”, literally “swollen-footed”), as well as with Old Church Slavonic ꙗдра (jadra, “bosom, embrace”, pl.).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂eyd-, *h₂eyd-ro-m

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɑi̯.trɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ poison
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ pus

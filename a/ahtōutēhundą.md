## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ahtōutēhundą

### Etymology

Likely a remodelling of earlier *ahtōhundą due to a reinterpetation of *sebuntēhundą (“seventy”) as *sebun (“seven”) + *-tēhundą (“-ty”), from *oḱtódḱomt, from Proto-Indo-European *oḱtṓw (“eight”) + *dḱm̥t (“ten”). For alternative explanations, see *-tēhundą.
By surface analysis, *ahtōu (“eight”) + *-tēhundą (“-ty”), and if the above etymology is correct, ultimately equivalent to *ahtōu (“eight”) + *teguz (“group of ten, decad; -ty”).

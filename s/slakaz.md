# *slakaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/slakaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *slakaz

### Etymology

Maybe from Proto-Indo-European *slogos, from the root *(s)leg- (“to weaken”). Compare Old Irish lacc (“slack”), Latin laxus (“slack, loose”), Tocharian A slākkär (“sad”), and perhaps Ancient Greek λαγαρός (lagarós, “slack, loose; thin, weak”) and Sanskrit श्लक्ष्ण (ślakṣṇa, “tender, soft”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)leg-, *slogos

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈslɑ.kɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ weak
2. _(reconstruction)_ slack

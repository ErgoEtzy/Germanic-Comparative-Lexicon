## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *strandō

### Etymology

Possibly a derivative of Proto-Indo-European *sterh₃- (“to broaden, spread out”), and cognate with Old Irish trét (“herd”), Lithuanian trénta (“place, region, direction”). Kroonen however assigns no etymology.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sterh₃-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstrɑn.dɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ edge; rim
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ shore, strand

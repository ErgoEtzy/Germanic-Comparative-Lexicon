## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sadaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *sh₂-tó-s, from *seh₂- (“to satiate, satisfy”), related to Latin satis (“enough”). Despite Kroonen, not related to Old Armenian հաճ (hač, “satisfied”), which instead stems from Proto-Indo-European *sekʷ- (“to follow”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *seh₂-, *sekʷ-, *sh₂-tó-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsɑ.ðɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ full, sated

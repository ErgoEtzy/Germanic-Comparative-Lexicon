# *sunnô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sunn%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sunnô

### Etymology

Suggested to be a nominalization of a lost adjective, possibly from *sunnaz (“sunny”) + *-ô (agent suffix), from Proto-Indo-European *sh₂un-wó-s, from *sóh₂wl̥ ~ *sh₂wéns (“sun”) + *-wós, or leveled from earlier paradigm *sundō ~ *sunᵈnaz, from *sundaz (“sunny”) + *-ô (agent suffix). from *sh₂un-tó-s, from *sóh₂wl̥ ~ *sh₂wéns + *-tós. Feminine *sunnǭ (“sun”) perhaps constructed in an opposing gender pair with masculine *mēnô (“moon”), akin to Latin cognate sōl m (“sun”) and lūna f (“moon”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-tós, *-wós, *sh₂un-wó-s, *sóh₂wl̥

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ the sun

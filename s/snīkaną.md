# *snīkaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sn%C4%ABkan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *snīkaną

### Etymology

Probably from a Proto-Indo-European *sneyg- (“to crawl”), and cognate with Latvian snīgt (“to hand, pass, reach”). See also the semantically and phonetically similar *sneganą (“to crawl, creep”), though a formal relation is difficult.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sneyg-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsniː.kɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to crawl
2. _(reconstruction)_ to creep

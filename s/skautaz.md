# *skautaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/skautaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skautaz

### Etymology

Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kewd- (“to throw, shoot, pursue, rush”), whence *skeutaną (“to shoot”); cognate with Proto-Slavic *kỳdati (“to fling”), Sanskrit चोदति (códati, “to impel”). The semantic development would be “to shoot out” > “to jut out” > “projection, edge”.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kewd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑu̯.tɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ corner; wedge, protrusion
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ the corner of a piece of cloth or garment: lap, flap, fold
3. _(broadly, masculine, reconstruction)_ the corner of a piece of cloth or garment: lap, flap, fold; lap
4. _(broadly, masculine, reconstruction)_ the corner of a piece of cloth or garment: lap, flap, fold; lap; womb
5. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ sheet
6. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a line from the foot of a sail

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *stappōną

### Etymology

Apparently from a Proto-Indo-European *(s)tep- (“to step”), and cognate with Proto-Slavic *stōpà (“foot, footstep”), *stepenь (“level”) (whence Serbo-Croatian stȅpēn (“level, degree”)), Lithuanian stapýti (“to stay put”). Whether a relation to Proto-Indo-European *stebʰ- (“to stand still, harden, support”) exists is unclear.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)tep-, *stebʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstɑp.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to walk

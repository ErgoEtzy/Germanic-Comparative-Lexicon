# *steuraz

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *steuraz

### Etymology

Uncertain.
* The term is sometimes connected with Avestan 𐬯𐬙𐬀𐬊𐬭𐬀 (staora, “large domestic animal”), which could perhaps indicate a PIE form *stéh₂wros (“big”), though a pre-form of this exact shape is also suggested to be the source of Proto-Germanic *stōraz.
* The EIEC reconstructs a shared pre-form *stewros (“large (domestic) animal”) and presumes that the semantics of the Germanic terms were influenced by *táwros (“bull”), whence also Proto-Germanic *þeuraz (“bull”).
* Alternatively, the term may instead have been inherited from the same source as Proto-Germanic *þeuraz (“bull”), from Proto-Indo-European *táwros, though Kroonen doubts this possibility, arguing that it requires ablaut with the non-IE word *táwros (which is probably from a substrate source) and also s-mobile.
* Ultimately, Kroonen suggests that both *steuraz and *þeuraz were borrowed from a substrate source with "irregular sound substitution" of a non-IE consonant, thereby producing the two variant forms. As an example of the possible non-IE phoneme in question, Kroonen suggests /θ/, which does appear in Etruscan θevrumines (“minotaur”). Kroonen has further proposed that the Germanic forms may have been borrowed twice at different stages of the language, in which case the term *steuraz was perhaps borrowed before Grimm's law and thus before the language had dental fricatives. Alternatively, Kroonen postulates that the differing initial consonants could merely reflect a series of sound substitutions as the wanderwort spread across various languages.

# *streupaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/streupan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *streupaną

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, a back-formation from the iterative *strupōną (“to writhe”), with both traditionally derived from Proto-Indo-European *strewb-; he prefers to derive them from a root *strebʰ- (“to twist”), adducing Ancient Greek στρέφω (stréphō, “to twist”) as a cognate. This has been disputed, however.
Older theories derived the term from Proto-Indo-European *strew-, *sterw-, *ster- (“strip; beam; streak; ray”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *strew-, *strewb-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstreu̯.pɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to touch, graze, brush
2. _(reconstruction)_ to squeeze
3. _(reconstruction)_ to strip

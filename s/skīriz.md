# *skīriz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sk%C4%ABriz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skīriz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *sḱeyr- (“luster, gloss, shadow”), which may be related to *(s)ḱeh₃- (“darkness, shadow”), though the formal details are unclear if so. Cognates include Albanian hir (“grace, favor, mercy”), Proto-Slavic *ščirъ (“sincere, frank”), Ancient Greek σκίρον (skíron, “parasol”), and perhaps Irish cír (“pure”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sḱeyr-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskiː.riz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ pure, clear, sheer

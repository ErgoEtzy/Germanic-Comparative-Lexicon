# *skellaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/skellan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skellaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)kel-, probably from Proto-Indo-European *kelh₁- (“to call, to shout, to sound”) with a s-mobile.
Cognate with Latvian skaļš (“loud; noisy”) and Russian скули́ть (skulítʹ, “to whine”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kel-, *kelh₁-, *skel-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskel.lɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to sound (strong), clatter, ring

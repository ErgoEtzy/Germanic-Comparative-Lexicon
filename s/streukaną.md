# *streukaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/streukan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *streukaną

### Etymology

Probably originally *streuganą, with -k- due to influence from the iterative *strukkōną, from a Proto-Indo-European *strewgʰ- (“to stroke, shave”). Related to Proto-Slavic *strъgati (“to scrape”), Lithuanian strùgas (“short, cropped”). Compare also *strīkaną (“to stroke”), which is apparently unrelated, despite strikingly similar phonetics and semantics.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *strewgʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstreu̯.kɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to stroke, wipe

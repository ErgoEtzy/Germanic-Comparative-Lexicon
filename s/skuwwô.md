## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skuwwô

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱeh₃w-ō ~ *(s)ḱuh₃-nés (“(one's) shadow, reflection”), from *(s)ḱeh₃w- (“shadow”) + *-ō. Alternatively, per Kroonen, from or influenced in meaning by Proto-Indo-European *(s)kewh₁- (“to perceive, observe”), while Orel prefers a derivation from *(s)kewH- (“to cover, hide”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kewh₁-, *(s)ḱeh₃-, *(s)ḱeh₃w-ō ~ *(s)ḱuh₃-nés, *ō

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ reflection, mirror image
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ shadow

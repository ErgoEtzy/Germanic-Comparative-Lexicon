# *strīdaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/str%C4%ABdan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *strīdaną

### Etymology

Likely from Proto-Indo-European *stréy-dʰe-ti, from *strey- (“to resist”) + *-dʰeti. Probably related to Latvian strīdēties, striêtiês (“to resist, quarrel”), Lithuanian strainùs (“unruly”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-dʰeti, *strey-, *stréy-dʰe-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstriː.ðɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to stand upright
2. _(reconstruction)_ to step up to
3. _(reconstruction)_ to endeavour; strive for
4. _(reconstruction)_ to contend; quarrel

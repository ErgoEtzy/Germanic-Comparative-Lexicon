# *sturnāną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sturn%C4%81n%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sturnāną

### Etymology

Kroonen suggests that this verb could be nasal infix present to Proto-Indo-European *sterh₁- (“to be stiff”).
The infix caused a consonant alternation in the root, the singular *str-néh₁-ti would result in a root with *sturr-.
Whereas the plural *str-nh₁-énti would result in a root with *sturn-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ster-, *sterh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstur.nɑː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to be stiff, be rigid
2. _(reconstruction)_ to stand out
3. _(reconstruction)_ to stare

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *smeuganą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)mewk- (“to slip, slide”), *smewg- (“to slip, glide; be slimy”), with -g- instead of expected -h-, likely due to influence from the causative or iterative formations. Cognate with Lithuanian smaũkti (“to pull, don, roll up”), Slovene smúkati (“to scurry, slip”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)mewk-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsmeu̯.ɣɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to creep, slip through or into

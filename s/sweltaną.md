## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sweltaną

### Etymology

Uncertain. Kroonen suggests that the term may derive from Proto-Indo-European *swéld-e-ti, a thematic present to a root of the shape *sweld-. However, the reconstruction of this root is uncertain. According to Kroonen, *sweld- may relate to the root *swelH- (“to burn”), whence perhaps *swelaną. It is from this same term, *swelaną, that Oryol suggests *sweltaną may have emerged. The term is often compared to Old Armenian քաղց (kʻałcʻ).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *swéld-e-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈswel.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to die; succumb

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skammaz

### Etymology

Of unknown origin. Has been compared with Proto-Indo-Iranian *kambʰ- (“few, small”) (whence Avestan 𐬐𐬀𐬨𐬥𐬀 (kamna, “little”), Persian کم (kam, “few, little”)), itself possibly related to Proto-Indo-European *ken- (“to arise, begin”). Other theories include a derivation from a Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱem- (“mutilated, hornless”), whence *hamalaz (“mutilated, truncated”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)ḱem-, *ken-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑm.mɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ short; blunt; stunted

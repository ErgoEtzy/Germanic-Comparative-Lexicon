## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *swēraz

### Etymology

Vṛddhi gerundive of an otherwise unattested strong verb *sweraną, from Proto-Indo-European *swer- (“heavy”). Cognate with Lithuanian sver̃ti (“to weigh, balance”), Latvian svars (“weight”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *swer-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈswɛː.rɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ heavy
2. _(reconstruction)_ grave, grievous, serious, important

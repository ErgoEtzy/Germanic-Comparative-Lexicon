## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sagjaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *sokʷh₂ṓy (“retainer, follower”). Cognate with Latin socius (“sharing, associated”).

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *sek- (“to cut”), assuming an original sense of "sharp grass" (note Old English seċġ (“sword”)), or from a Proto-Indo-European *sak- (“marsh plant”) of potential substrate origin (compare Welsh hesg (“sedge, rushes”)), though the latter comparanda have been considered as derived from the former.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

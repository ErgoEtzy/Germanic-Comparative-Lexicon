# *skeuhaz

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skeuhaz

### Etymology

Further origin unknown; assumed to be from pre-Germanic *skéwkos. Possibly ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *skewbʰ-, *skūbʰ- (“to push, drive, move forward”) (for which see Proto-Germanic *skeubaną (“to push, drive”) for cognates) or *(s)kewH- (“to cover, hide”).
For semantic development compare Russian засте́нчивый (zasténčivyj) connected with стена́ (stená) or with стень (stenʹ).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskeu̯.xɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ frightened
2. _(reconstruction)_ shy; timid; bashful; coy; reserved; cautious
3. _(reconstruction)_ abominable; odious; loathsome; vile

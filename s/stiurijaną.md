# *stiurijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/stiurijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *stiurijaną

### Etymology

From *steurō, *stiurijō (“steering, rudder”) + *-janą. The first element is of uncertain origin:
* Traditionally, from Proto-Indo-European *stew-r- ~ *stēw-r-, but this is now reconstructed as *steh₂-w-r-, from the adjective *steh₂urós based on a *w-extension of *steh₂- (“to stand, be firm”) (see *stāną), and is thus phonetically impossible.
* More likely, from *(s)twerH- (“to turn, twist, stir, agitate; to propel”), whence also *þweraną and Western *sturjan, *staurijan, with vowel/semivowel metathesis (*-wer- > *-ewr-).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)twerH-, *steh₂-, *stew-r- ~ *stēw-r-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstiu̯.ri.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

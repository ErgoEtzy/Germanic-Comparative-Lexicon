# *snewaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/snewan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *snewaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *snewH- (“to be fleet; flow; run; trickle”). Cognate with Proto-Slavic *snuti (“to shuttle, to warp (yarn, thread)”), Latvian snaujis (“noose”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *snewH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsne.wɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to rush, hurry

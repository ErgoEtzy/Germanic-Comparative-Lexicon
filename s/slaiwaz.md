# *slaiwaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/slaiwaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *slaiwaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. Expected to reflect either pre-Germanic *sloy(H)-wo-s or *slo/aH-i-wo-s. Kroonen prefers the former, tentatively comparing Sanskrit स्रीव्यति (srī́vyati, “to turn out badly, fail”) for Proto-Indo-European *sliH-u-. For the latter, compare Ancient Greek λαιός (laiós, “left (side); awkward”), Latin laevus, Proto-Slavic *lěvъ (“left”) (originally meaning *“askew”?), but the semantic connection is weak. Kroonen rejects this along with any relation to Ancient Greek λιαρός (liarós, “mild”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)leyH-, *sliH-u-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈslɑi̯.wɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ blunt; dull
2. _(reconstruction)_ exhausted; weary; faint; weak; sluggish
3. _(reconstruction)_ listless; torpid
4. _(reconstruction)_ dim-witted; slow
5. _(reconstruction)_ slack; lazy

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skrinkwaną

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. Apparently from a Proto-Indo-European *skringʷ-onom, which may be from a derived root from Proto-Indo-European *sker- (“to turn, bend (wrinkle, wither)”); compare Polish kurczyć; also compare Lithuanian skrę̃sti (“to be covered with a crust”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sker-, *skringʷ-onom

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskriŋʷ.kʷɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to shrink, pull together, shrivel

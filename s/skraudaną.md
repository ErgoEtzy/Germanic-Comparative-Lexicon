## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skraudaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *skrówdʰ-e-ti, from *skrewdʰ- (“to cut, shred”); cognate with Lithuanian skriaudùs (“blunt, insulting; torrential; brittle”). The root may be an extension of a root *skrew- (“to cut; cutting tool”), itself an extension of *(s)ker- (“to cut”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skrówdʰ-e-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskrɑu̯.ðɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to cut up, to shred

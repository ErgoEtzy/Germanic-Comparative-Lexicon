# *sleutaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sleutan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sleutaną

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin.
Traditionally derived from Proto-Indo-European *(s)klewd- (“to lock”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kleh₂w- (“nail, hook, pin, key”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kel- (“to crook, bend”), and compared with Latin clāvis (“key, bar, bolt”), Latin claudō (“close”), Lithuanian kliudýti (“to hamper, obstruct”), Middle Irish cló (“nail”).
Kroonen, however, considers the word restricted to Germanic, and leaves its origin open, while Orel tentatively connects the word to *lūtaną (“to bow down”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kel-, *(s)kleh₂w-, *(s)klewd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsleu̯.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to bolt, lock
2. _(reconstruction)_ to shut, close

# *smēhaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sm%C4%93haz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *smēhaz

### Etymology

Apparently from a pre-Germanic form *smeh₁k-o-s, with no certain cognates outside of Germanic. The traditional comparison with Ancient Greek μῑκρός (mīkrós, “little, small”) and Proto-Germanic *smikraz (“mild, elegant, fair; tender”), traditionally derived from a Proto-Indo-European *(s)meyg-, *(s)mēyg- (“small, thin, delicate”), is phonetically untenable (contrast *mey(h₁)- (“to be small”)), and the Greek is thought to be derived from a substrate, rendering the Germanic formation isolated. Possibly sound-symbolic; compare *smalaz, which is likewise difficult to derive from a common root.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)meyg-, *(s)mēyg-

### Pronunciation

### Adjective

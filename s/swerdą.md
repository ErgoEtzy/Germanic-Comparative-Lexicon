# *swerdą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/swerd%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *swerdą

### Etymology

Of disputed origin. Traditionally derived from a Proto-Indo-European *swerdʰ- (“sharp tool”), often taken as an extension of *swer- (“to hurt”), and compared with Proto-Slavic *svьrdьlъ (“drill, auger”). This is uncertain, however, since all other descendants, such as Proto-Germanic *sweraną, only suggest an original meaning 'to ache, hurt, fester'.
Alternatively, Nikolaev (2015) suggests derivation from Proto-Indo-European *sw-er, locative of *seh₂w- (“sharp”) (with a loss of *h₂ between *s and *w) and cognate to Luwian [script needed] (šiḫual-, šiwal-, “sword”), [script needed] (šiwa-, “sharp”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sw-er, *swerdʰ-

### Pronunciation

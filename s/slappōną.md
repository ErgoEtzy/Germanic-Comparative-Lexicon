# *slappōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/slapp%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *slappōną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *slobʰ-néh₂-ti, an iterative verb related to *slēpaną (“to sleep”). The root is reconstructed by Kroonen as *slobʰ- (“to be slack, weak”), and compared with Proto-Slavic *slàbъ (“weak”), dialectal Lithuanian slãbnas, slõbnas (“weak”), Latvian slãbs (“weak”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *slobʰ-néh₂-ti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈslɑp.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to be slack

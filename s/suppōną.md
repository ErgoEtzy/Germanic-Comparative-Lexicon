# *suppōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/supp%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *suppōną

### Etymology

Kroonen reconstructs the Pre-Germanic form as *su(H)ph₃néh₂-, with dissimilation of the first laryngeal, a compound of Proto-Indo-European *sewH- (“to pour; moisture, rain, juice”) + *peh₃- (“to drink”). The strong verb *sūpaną (“to guzzle, soak”) was likely back-formed from the iterative. Related to Sanskrit सूप (sūpa, “soup, broth”), as well as Hittite [script needed] (šuḫḫai, “to pour”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sewH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsup.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to soak

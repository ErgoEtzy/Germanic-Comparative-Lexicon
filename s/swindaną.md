## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *swindaną

### Etymology

Of uncertain origin. Probably from a Proto-Indo-European *(s)wendʰ- (“to dwindle”), and compared with Proto-Slavic *vę̀dnǫti (“to fade, wither”) as well as perhaps Old Armenian քանդել (kʻandel, “to demolish, break up”). Alternatively, perhaps related to Proto-Germanic *swīnaną, *dwīnaną, *þwīnaną (“to dwindle”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰgʷʰey- (“to dwindle”), though this is phonetically difficult.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)wendʰ-, *dʰgʷʰey-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈswin.dɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *silubrą

### Etymology

Uncertain. Likely a Wanderwort of non-Indo-European substrate origin. Compare Proto-Balto-Slavic *sirabras (“silver”), Basque zilar (“silver”), Celtiberian silabur (“money”), and possibly Ancient Greek σῐ́δηρος (sĭ́dēros, “iron”). A Semitic origin has also been proposed, comparing Proto-Berber *a-ẓrəf (“silver”), Akkadian 𒀫𒁍𒌝 (ṣur-pu-um /⁠ṣurpum⁠/, “silver”), 𒍝𒊏𒁍𒌝 (ṣa-ra-pu-um /⁠ṣarāpum⁠/, “to smelt, refine”); however, this derivation has been rejected by some scholars as phonologically and geographically implausible.
The displacement of unattested Proto-Germanic *urkundą, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ŕ̥ǵn̥tom (“silver”), has been connected with the adoption of more advanced metallurgical techniques and terminology.

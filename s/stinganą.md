# *stinganą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/stingan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *stinganą

### Etymology

Traditionally derived from a Proto-Indo-European *stengʰ- (“to be stiff, sting; rod, blade”), and compared with Ancient Greek στᾰ́χῡς (stắkhūs, “ear of grain”) (< *stngʰ-u-s). However, a substrate origin for the Greek is quite possible, based on the variant ἄστᾰχῠς (ástăkhŭs, “ear of corn; bandage”), which would render the Germanic without outside cognates.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *stengʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstiŋ.ɡɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to stick out; protrude
2. _(reconstruction)_ to sting

# *spiltaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/spiltaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *spiltaz

### Etymology

Unknown. It may or may not be cognate with Russian по́лба (pólba, “spelt”) and Latin puls (“porridge (usually of spelt)”) and Ancient Greek πόλτος (póltos, “porridge (usually of spelt)”), in this case from Proto-Indo-European *pel- (“flour, dust”). Otherwise from Proto-Indo-European *(s)pelH- (“to split off, separate”), or borrowed from an unknown substrate by Germanic and Latin.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)pelH-, *pel-

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ spelt (a type of wheat)

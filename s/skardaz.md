# *skardaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/skardaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skardaz

### Etymology

From pre-Germanic *skordʰ-o-s, from Proto-Indo-European *skerdʰ- (“to cut up”); cognate with Old Irish scerdaid (“to peel, scrape off”), Lithuanian sker̃sti (“to cut, slaughter”). The Indo-European root may be an extension of *sker- (“to cut”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skerdʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑr.ðɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ cut up, damaged

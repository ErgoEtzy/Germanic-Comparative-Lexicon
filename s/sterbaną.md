# *sterbaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sterban%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sterbaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *sterbʰ- (“to be stiff, become stiff”); cognate with Ancient Greek στέρφνιος (stérphnios, “hard, firm, tight”).
Another theory derives the verb from Proto-Indo-European *(s)terp- (“to lose force; lose sensibility, become numb; be dead, be motionless”), connecting Latin torpeō.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)terp-, *sterbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈster.βɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to become stiff
2. _(reconstruction)_ to die, become lifeless

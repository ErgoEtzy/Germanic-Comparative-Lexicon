# *slībaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sl%C4%ABban%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *slībaną

### Etymology

Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ley- (“smooth; slick; sticky; slimy”), the same source as *slīmą (“slime”) and *līmaz (“glue, clay, lime”). For the semantics, compare Proto-Germanic *slīpaną (“to whet, sharpen”), Old High German slīman (“to make smooth”), Proto-Celtic *limāti (“to polish, sharpen”) and Latin līma (“carpenter's file”), from extensions of the same root.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)ley-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsliː.βɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(Germanic, West, reconstruction)_ to split

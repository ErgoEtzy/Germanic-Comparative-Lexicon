# *sūliz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/s%C5%ABliz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sūliz

### Etymology

From a pre-Germanic ablauting paradigm *sou-l- ~ *suH-l- (with full-grade retained in the Gothic descendant), apparently from a Proto-Indo-European *sweHl- (“log, wedge, firewood”), which Kroonen considers of unclear further origin. Orel connects the Germanic with Ancient Greek ῡ̔́λη (hū́lē, “wood”) and perhaps Latin solum (“bottom”).
Older theories derived the term from Proto-Indo-European *ḱsewl-, *ḱswel- (“log”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱsew-, *ḱes- (“to scratch, comb”), with Ancient Greek ξῠ́λον (xŭ́lon, “wood”) often compared. Beekes supports this comparison, but considers the words to be borrowed from a common substrate, rather than inherited from Proto-Indo-European. Note also Proto-Germanic *sulī (“balk, bar, sill”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sweHl-, *ḱsew-, *ḱsewl-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsuː.liz/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ beam, post
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ column, pillar

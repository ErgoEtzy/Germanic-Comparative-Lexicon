## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skelfō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)kel(H)-p-, from *(s)kelH- (“to cut, split, divide”); compare Latin scalpō (“to scratch, carve”). Orel connects the word to *skelbaną (“to shiver, tremble”), though this is semantically dubious.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kel(H)-p-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ crag, cliff
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ flat surface (e.g. floor, deck, shelf, ledge, etc.)

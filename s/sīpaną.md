## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sīpaną

### Etymology

Perhaps from a Proto-Indo-European *seyp-, *seybʰ- (“to pour, dribble, trickle, sieve, strain, sift, run, drip”), whence also Proto-West Germanic *sibi (“sieve”). Speculated cognates outside of Germanic include Ancient Greek εἴβω (eíbō, “to drip, spill”) and Latin sēbum (“tallow, grease”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *seyp-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsiː.pɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to drip, to pour

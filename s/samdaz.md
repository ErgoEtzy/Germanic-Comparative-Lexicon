## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *samdaz

### Etymology

Likely borrowed from a Western European substrate. Cognate with Ancient Greek ψᾰ́μμος (psắmmos), ἄμαθος (ámathos, “sand”), Latin sabulum (“coarse sand, gravel”).
Ringe reconstructs the ultimate pre-form of the word as *sámh₂dʰos (whence an earlier and intermediate form *samədaz), though the EIEC considers such a PIE form to be impossible. Kroonen instead provides a reconstruction of Proto-Indo-European *sám-dʰ-o-s.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

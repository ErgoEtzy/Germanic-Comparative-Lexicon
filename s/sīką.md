## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sīką

### Etymology

Unknown. Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *seygʷ-, variant of *seykʷ- (“to pour out; gush; stream; flow; trickle”) (whence *sīhwaną (“to percolate, filter”) and *saiwiz (“sea”)), from *sey- (“to be wet; dribble; run”), but the delabialization would be unexpected.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *seygʷ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsiː.kɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ trickling water; a dribble
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ a slow brook
3. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ a marshy place

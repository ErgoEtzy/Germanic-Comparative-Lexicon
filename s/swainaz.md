## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *swainaz

### Etymology

Either from Proto-Indo-European *s(w)e- (“separate; apart; oneself; one's own”), or derived from *swīną (“pig, swine”), that is, a "pig herder" > "young man, relative". It is also possible that both derivations converged and conflated in the descendant languages.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *s(w)e-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈswɑi̯.nɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ relative; kinsman
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ young man
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ herder; herdsman, swineherd

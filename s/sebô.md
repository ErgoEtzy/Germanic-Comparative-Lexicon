## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sebô

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *sep- (“to taste, test, try out”). Cognate with Latin sapiō (“to smack, taste, savour, have sense, discern”), Ancient Greek σοφός (sophós, “wise, clever”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sep-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈse.βɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ sense, taste, perception
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ mind, understanding

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *seuþaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂sewt- (“to move about, roil, seethe”), perhaps a t-extension of *(h)sew- (“to burn”), for which see *swīþaną (“to burn, ache”) for more. Cognate with Proto-Slavic *šutiti (“to joke, jest”), Lithuanian siaũsti (“to play, rage”), Ancient Greek ἀϋτμή (aütmḗ, “breath, scent”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂sewt-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈseu̯.θɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to seethe; boil

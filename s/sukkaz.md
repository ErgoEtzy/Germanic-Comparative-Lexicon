## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sukkaz

### Etymology

From Latin soccus, from Ancient Greek σύκχος (súkkhos, “a kind of shoe”), probably from Phrygian, Anatolian, or another substrate language from Asia Minor.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No PIE form is listed or machine-resolvable in this snapshot.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/suk.kɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ light footwear
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ sock

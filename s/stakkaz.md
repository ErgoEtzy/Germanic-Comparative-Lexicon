## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *stakkaz

### Etymology

Per Kroonen, with *-kk- via Kluge's law from earlier *stogʰ-nós, from Proto-Indo-European *stogʰ-, cognate with Proto-Slavic *stogъ (“stack”); the original *-gʰn- may be the source of *-kn- in Proto-Finnic *sakna (“sauna”) if this was borrowed from pre-Proto-Germanic. Ancient Greek στόχος (stókhos, “brick pillar”) would also likely be cognate but is not mentioned. Doublet of *stakô (“stake”) according to this theory; see also its synonym *stangō.
Traditionally since Pokorny derived from Proto-Indo-European *(s)teg- (“beam, stake”) and cognate instead with Latin tignum (“tree trunk, beam, log”)..

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)teg-, *stegʰ-, *stogʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstɑk.kɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ pile of hay; haystack

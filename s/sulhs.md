## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sulhs

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, from Proto-Indo-European *sl̥k-, an old root noun from *selk- (“to pull”). Cognate with Latin sulcus (“furrow, ploughing”), Ancient Greek ἕλκω (hélkō, “to drag, pull”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *selk-, *sl̥k-

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ plough
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ that which is ploughed or furrowed; furrow

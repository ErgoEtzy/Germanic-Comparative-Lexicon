# *steubaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/steuban%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *steubaną

### Etymology

Traditionally derived from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewbʰ- (“to whisk, smoke, obscure”), which may be an extension of Proto-Indo-European *dʰewh₂- (“to whirl, waft, stink, shake; steam, haze, smoke”), and compared with Ancient Greek τῡ́φω (tū́phō, “to smoke”) (though Orel is skeptical of this derivation). Other theories include derivation from a Proto-Indo-European *stewbʰ- (“to drive about”), and comparison with Ancient Greek στυφελίζω (stuphelízō, “to strike hard, dash, beat, drive away”).
However, Kroonen prefers to derive the word from a Proto-Indo-European *tsewbʰ- (“to fly about”), with the same metathesis from Proto-Indo-European to Proto-Germanic as seen in *stelaną (“to steal”), and compares Lithuanian siaũbti (“to dash about”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰewbʰ-, *dʰewh₂-, *stewbʰ-, *tsewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsteu̯.βɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to fly or whirl about
2. _(reconstruction)_ to fume; smoke; smolder

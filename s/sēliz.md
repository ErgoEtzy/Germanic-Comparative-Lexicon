[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/s%C4%93liz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sēliz

### Etymology

Traditionally compared (e.g., by Pokorny) with Latin sōlor (“to comfort, console”), which leads to the reconstruction of Proto-Indo-European *selh₂- (“to calm, quiet, be favorable”). This comparison does not seem to be maintained by modern researchers, including Orel, who instead take the word as related to *saljaną (“to offer, sell”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *selh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsɛː.liz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ happy, fortunate
2. _(reconstruction)_ kind, good

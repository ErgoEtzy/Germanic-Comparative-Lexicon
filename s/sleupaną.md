# *sleupaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sleupan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sleupaną

### Etymology

According to Kroonen, a strong verb back-formed from the iterative verb *sluppōną, from a Proto-Indo-European *slewp- (“to slip, glide”), and cognate with Lithuanian įslupti (“to slip into”), Latvian šļupt (“to slip”).
However, de Vaan reconstructs the root as coming from earlier forms *sleuppaną < *sleubnaną, and derives it from a Proto-Indo-European *slewbʰ- (“to slip, slide”), comparing Latin lūbricus (“slippery”). If this connection is accepted, the root may have only belonged to a late western Indo-European dialect.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *slewbʰ-, *slewp-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsleu̯.pɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to creep, slink, sneak, slip (move quietly or stealthily)

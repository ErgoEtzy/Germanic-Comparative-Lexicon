# *sumblą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sumbl%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sumblą

### Etymology

From an earlier *sumlą. The first element of the word is likely Proto-Indo-European *sm̥- (“with, together”), while the second element is unknown. According to Paul Bauschatz, the second element is *alu with the whole meaning being "gathering or coming together of ale".
Compare ensemble, ultimately from Latin insimul (“in gathering”), from simul (“together”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sm̥-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsum.blɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ gathering
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ banquet

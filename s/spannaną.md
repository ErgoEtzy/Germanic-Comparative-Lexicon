# *spannaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/spannan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *spannaną

### Etymology

Originally a strong verb of the seventh class, the long -n- comes from an earlier form *spanwaną-. Perhaps ultimately an ablautive form from the same source as *spinnaną (“to spin”), though Kroonen alternatively derives the verb from a Proto-Indo-European *spend- (“to stretch”), comparing Lithuanian spę́sti (“to snare”), Latvian spanda (“strap”), Old Church Slavonic спѫдъ (spǫdŭ, “corn-measure, measure, peck”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *spend-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈspɑn.nɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to stretch, span

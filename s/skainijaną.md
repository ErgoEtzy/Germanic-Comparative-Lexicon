## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skainijaną

### Etymology

Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *skey- (“to cut, split”), related to *skinō (“piece, shinbone”).

Possibly from *skīnaną (“to shine”) + *-janą.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skey-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑi̯.ni.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to scratch, to wound
2. _(reconstruction)_ to break

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skītaną

### Etymology

According to Orel, from Proto-Indo-European *skéydt (“to cut off”), from *skeyd- (“to split, to divide”) (though Kroonen is implicitly skeptical). Cognates include Lithuanian šikti (“to defecate, shit”), Proto-Slavic *cěditi (“to strain, filter”), Ancient Greek σχῐ́ζω (skhĭ́zō, “to split”).
Compare typologically Proto-Germanic *turdą (<? Proto-Indo-European *der- (“to split, to separate; to tear”)).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *der-, *skéydt

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskiː.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to defecate, to shit

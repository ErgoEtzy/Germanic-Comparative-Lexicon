## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skaþô

### Etymology

Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *(s)keh₁tH- (“to damage, harm”), and cognate with Ancient Greek ἀσκηθής (askēthḗs, “intact, safe, sound”, literally “without damage”), Old Irish scíth (“weary, tired”), as well as perhaps Old Armenian ցաւ (cʻaw, “pain, suffering”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)keh₁tH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑ.θɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ damage, scathe
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ one who causes damage; injurer

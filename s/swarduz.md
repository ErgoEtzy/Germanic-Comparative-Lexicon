# *swarduz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/swarduz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *swarduz

### Etymology

Of unknown origin. Often the only cognate compared is Latvian šērve (“pigskin, thick (and dirty) skin layer”) and a possible reconstruction Proto-Balto-Slavic *sverdvē, though the Latvian term itself could have been borrowed through Germanic from German Low German Schörwe.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈswɑr.duz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ rind, tough skin
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ turf

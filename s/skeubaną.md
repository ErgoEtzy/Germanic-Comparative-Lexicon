# *skeubaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/skeuban%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skeubaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *skewbʰ-, *skūbʰ- (“to push, drive, move forward”), possibly metathesized from *ksewbʰ- (compare Sanskrit क्षुभ् (kṣubh, “to throw, shake, agitate”)). Cognate with Lithuanian skubė́ti (“to hurry, rush”), Polish skubać (“to pull quickly, pluck”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskeu̯.βɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to push, drive

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/spreutan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *spreutaną

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *sprewd-, but also possibly back-formed from the iterative, from Proto-Indo-European *sper- (“to strew”), extended form *sprewd-. Related to Lithuanian sprausti (“to thrust”), Ancient Greek σπείρω (speírō, “I sow”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sper-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈspreu̯.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to sprout
2. _(reconstruction)_ to spring, come out

# *skūrō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sk%C5%ABr%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skūrō

### Etymology

Of disputed origin:
* From a Proto-Indo-European root variously reconstructed as Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱēwer- (“north; north wind; cold wind; rain shower”) or Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱyeh₂w- (“north; north wind; cold wind; rain shower”), in the latter case in the ablaut form *(s)ḱyh̥₂u-. Cognate with Proto-Slavic *sě̀verъ (“north”), Lithuanian šiaurỹs (“north wind”) and Lithuanian šiáurė (“north”). This theory is tentatively supported by Orel, but rejected by Kroonen, apparently due to the rather tenuous semantics.
* From Proto-Indo-European *(s)ḱew- (“to stir up, excite”); or from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kewH- (“to cover”).
* According to Kroonen, from an earlier *skewur, from Proto-Indo-European *skéh₃-ur, the ur-stem of *(s)ḱeh₃- (“shade, shadow”) (which Kroonen reconstructs as *skeh₁-).

Related to an earlier *sku(w)enjō- (whence modern German Scheune (“barn”)), from *sku(w)en-, possibly from the dative form *sku(w)eni, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *skuH-ro-, from *(s)kewH- (“to cover”), related to Sanskrit स्कुनाति (skunāti, “he covers, protects”). Alternatively, the pre-form *sku(w)enjō- is considered by Kroonen to stem from the same root as the "shower" sense of Etymology 1.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)kewH-, *(s)ḱeh₁w-, *(s)ḱew-, *(s)ḱyeh₂w-, *(s)ḱēwer-, *skuH-ro-, *skéh₃-ur

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskuː.rɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ storm
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ short shower (of rain / hail)

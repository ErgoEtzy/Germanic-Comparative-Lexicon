# *skankô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/skank%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skankô

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)keng- (“to limp; be crooked; slant”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)keng-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑŋ.kɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ that which is bent
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ shank; thigh

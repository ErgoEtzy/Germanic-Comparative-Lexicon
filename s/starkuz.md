## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *starkuz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *storg-o/u-s, an o-grade adjective reconstructed either as an o-stem or u-stem, derived from a root *sterg- (“to harden, be rigid, stiff”), cognate with Lithuanian strė́gti (“to harden, congeal, freeze”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *storg-o/u-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstɑr.kuz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ stiff; rigid
2. _(reconstruction)_ strong

# *skuldiz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/skuldiz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skuldiz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *skéltis, from Proto-Indo-European *skel- (“to be obliged, be beholden to, owe, be guilty”), equivalent to Proto-Germanic *skulaną (“to be obligated, owe”) + *-þiz. Cognate with Lithuanian kaltė̃ (“guilt”).

Derived from *skulaną (“to owe”), and thus of the same origin as the "debt" sense of Etymology 1.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skel-, *skéltis

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskul.diz/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ obligation
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ debt
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ fault, blame, guilt

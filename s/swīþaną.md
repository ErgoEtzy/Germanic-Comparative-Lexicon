## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *swīþaną

### Etymology

Possibly from a Proto-Indo-European *swey- (“to burn”), which could be an extension of *sew- (“to braise”). Compare Avestan 𐬒𐬬𐬀𐬉𐬥𐬀 (x́vaēna, “red-hot, glowing”), from Proto-Indo-European *(H)swoy-no-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(H)swoy-no-, *swey-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈswiː.θɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to burn, singe

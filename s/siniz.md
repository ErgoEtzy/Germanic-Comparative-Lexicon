## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *siniz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *sénos (“of last year, old”) with change to an i-stem. Cognate with Lithuanian sẽnas (“old, previous, ancient”), Latin senex (“old, aged, advanced in years; old man”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *sénos

### Pronunciation

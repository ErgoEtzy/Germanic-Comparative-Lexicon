# *sneganą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/snegan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *sneganą

### Etymology

Unknown; often cited as being related to Sanskrit नाग (nāgá, “snake”), however the Sanskrit requires Proto-Indo-European *(s)neg-, whilst the Germanic seems to demand aspirated *snegʰ-. A connection to *snīkaną (“to crawl, creep”) is very tempting but difficult to reconcile morphologically.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)neg-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsne.ɣɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to crawl, creep

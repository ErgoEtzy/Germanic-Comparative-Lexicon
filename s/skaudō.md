## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skaudō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *skówdʰ-eh₂, from *(s)kewH- (“to cover, hide”). Cognate with Ancient Greek σκῦτος (skûtos, “skin, hide”), Latin scūtum (“shield, defense”). Within Proto-Germanic, compare in particular *hūdijaną (“to hide”) for a similar formation from the same root.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skówdʰ-eh₂

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑu̯.dɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ sheath, husk

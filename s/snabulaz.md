# *snabulaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/snabulaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *snabulaz

### Etymology

From *snabjaz + *-ulaz (diminutive suffix), which Pfeifer implicitly considers to be from a series of onomatopoeic words restricted to Germanic. Probably from the same ultimate source as *snūtaz (“snout”). Compare also Lithuanian snãpas (“snout”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsnɑ.βu.lɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ snout; muzzle
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ mouth

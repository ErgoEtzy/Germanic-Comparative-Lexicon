# *slakwjaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/slakwjan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *slakwjaną

### Etymology

Causative of *slekwaną (“to extinguish, go out”). Seebold leaves the origin open, while Orel compares Lithuanian slė́gti (“to press, squeeze”) and Latin langueo (“to be faint”), apparently considering the verb related to *slakaz (“weak, slack”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)lew-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈslɑkʷ.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ (transitive) to quench, extinguish

# *skuppōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/skupp%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skuppōną

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *skubʰnéh₂-, an iterative formation to *skeubaną (“to shove”). Particularly close in formation to Lithuanian skùbinti (“to rush, hurry”).

Considered by Kroonen to be a secondary zero-grade iterative to a Proto-Indo-European *skep- (“to mock”), and not equivalent to the "shove" sense of Etymology 1. Probably related to Ancient Greek σκώπτω (skṓptō, “to mock”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skep-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskup.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to shove, push, swing

# *strangaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/strangaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *strangaz

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *strongʰos, an adjective formed from the strong verb *stringaną, from Proto-Indo-European *strengʰ- (“pulled tight, straight, constricted”), from Proto-Indo-European *ster- (“stiff, rigid”). Cognate with Latvian strangs (“brave, brisk, fresh”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ster-, *strengʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈstrɑŋ.ɡɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ tight, strict, straight
2. _(reconstruction)_ strong

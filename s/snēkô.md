## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *snēkô

### Etymology

From a vṛddhi derivative of *sneganą (“to crawl, sneak”) (or a related verb; compare *snakaną → Proto-West Germanic *snakan) + *-ô (agent suffix). Kroonen suggests an original *snēgô ~ *snakkaz paradigm, making Proto-West Germanic *snakō a parallel formation, in which case the verbs with *k are secondary. The traditional etymology posits a Germanic root *snek- (“to crawl, creep; snake”), but his explanation points to *sneg- (with Kluge variants *snekk- and *snegg-) along the lines of “to sting ~ pointy object”: compare Proto-Germanic *snakkōną (“to snap at”, among other meanings) and Icelandic snagi (“pin”).

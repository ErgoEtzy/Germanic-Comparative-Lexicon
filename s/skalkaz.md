## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *skalkaz

### Etymology

Of unknown origin, though the primary meaning of the root underlying the term may have been along the lines of "wry, wrong". Orel prefers connecting the word to Proto-Slavic *xolkъ (“bachelor”) and *xolpъ (“brat; serf”), reconstructing the shared root as a Proto-Indo-European *skel-; he appears to favor equating this reconstruction with *(s)kelH- (“to cut”), taking the semantic development as "cut" > "maimed" > "servant".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *skel-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈskɑl.kɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ servant

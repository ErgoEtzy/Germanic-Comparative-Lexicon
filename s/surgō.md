# *surgō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/surg%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *surgō

### Etymology

From earlier *swurgō, from Proto-Indo-European *swergʰ- (“to watch over, worry; be ill, suffer”). Cognate with Lithuanian sir̃gti (“to be ill, be sick”), Polish srogi (“stern, severe, harsh”), Old Irish serg (“sickness, disease”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *swergʰ-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ worry, care, sorrow

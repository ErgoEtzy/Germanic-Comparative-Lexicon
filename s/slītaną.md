# *slītaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/sl%C4%ABtan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *slītaną

### Etymology

Of unknown origin. Has been taken to be from the same root as Proto-Germanic *skalō (“shell, husk”), though the lack of a *-k- in the word's proto-form makes this suspect. Others take the word from a Proto-Indo-European *sleyd- (“to rend, injure, crumble”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)leyd-, *sleyd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈsliː.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to tear apart

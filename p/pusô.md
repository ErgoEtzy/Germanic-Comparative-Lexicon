## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *pusô

### Etymology

From *pus- (“to blow, inflate”); further origin uncertain. Has been compared to *puhô (“bag, pouch”) and onomatopoeic Proto-Indo-European *bew- (“to swell, inflate”). See also Middle Irish búas (“bag”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bew-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈpu.sɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ bag, sack, scrip

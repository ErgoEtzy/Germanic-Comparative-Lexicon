# *pōlaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/p%C5%8Dlaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *pōlaz

### Etymology

From presumed pre-Germanic *bōlos, from virtual Proto-Indo-European *bel-, *bal- (“bog, marsh”), which may be a European substrate term borrowed into multiple Indo-European branches. Potential cognates include Proto-Slavic *bolto (“swamp”), Modern Greek βάλτος (váltos, “swamp, bog”), Romanian baltă (“swamp, puddle”), Albanian baltë (“mud, swamp”), as well as Proto-Slavic *bala, *balъka (“pool; ravine”), Lithuanian balà (“bog; marsh; swamp; pool”), Latvian bala (“a muddy, treeless depression”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bel-, *bal-

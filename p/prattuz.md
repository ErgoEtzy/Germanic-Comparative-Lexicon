## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *prattuz

### Etymology

Perhaps from pre-Germanic *brod-nú-, from Proto-Indo-European *bred- (“to wander about”). Compare Proto-Slavic *brъditi and Latvian bradāt, both meaning “to talk nonsense”. More at prate, prattle.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *bred-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈprɑt.tuz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ boastful talk
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ deceit, trickery, stratagem, list, cunning

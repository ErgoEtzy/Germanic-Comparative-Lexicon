# *paþaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/pa%C3%BEaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *paþaz

### Etymology

Possibly borrowed from Iranian during the Alan invasion (compare Old Persian 𐎱𐎰 (p-θ /⁠paθi⁠/)), from Proto-Iranian *pántaHs, from Proto-Indo-Iranian *pántaHs, from Proto-Indo-European *póntoh₁s, from *pent- (“path”). If so, related to Germanic *funsaz (“ready, willing”) and *finþaną (“to find, to discover, to come upon”), as well as Sanskrit पन्था (pánthā, “way, path”).
However, the above theory has been disputed, with one alternative taking the word as borrowed from Proto-Celtic *batos (“death, pestilence”) (whence Old Irish bath (“death”), Welsh bad (“pest, plague”)), itself from Proto-Indo-European *gʷeh₂- (“to step, go”), at an early stage prior to Grimm's shift; this theory has the additional constraint that the borrowing must have happened before the Celtic underwent the semantic shift "to go" > "to pass away" > "death", and Kroonen appears to ultimately favor Iranian over Celtic origin, despite suggesting both.
In either case, a likely Wanderwort.

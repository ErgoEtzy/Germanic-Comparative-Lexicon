## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *jabą

### Etymology

Extended form of either *ja (“yes”), or from Proto-Indo-European *Hyós (“already, that [demonstrative/relative pronoun]”), assuming they're seperate terms etymologically, which they may not be (see *jainaz for more details on this). The meanings were probably a figurative expansion of "that which is of importance or an issue."
Since terms derived from Proto-Indo-European *Hyós largely have fixed stress on the first syllable, a -bʰ- extension undergoing Grimm's law (*Hyó-bʰ-om > *yóbon > *jabą) is a more likely explanation for a for the presence of medial -b- than a -p- extension undergoing Verner's law, as that change that only happens in unstressed syllables (*Hyo-p-óm > *yopón > *jafą́ > *jabą), even though both processes technically lead to the same outcome.

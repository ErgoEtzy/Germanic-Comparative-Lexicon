# -janą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/-jan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** -janą

### Etymology

This suffix is a merger of multiple Proto-Indo-European suffixes:
* Proto-Indo-European *-ye-ti and *-yé-ti (primary suffixes), attached to directly to roots
* Proto-Indo-European *-yé-ti (denominative suffix), attached to nominal stems. If the base is a thematic stem the connecting vowel is *-e-. Cognates include Ancient Greek denominatives with -y- metathesis, verbs in -ίω (-íō) and -ύω (-úō), Sanskrit denominative verbs in -यति (-yáti).
* Proto-Indo-European *-éye-ti (causative suffix). Cognates include most Latin -īre (fourth conjugation) verbs, some -ēre (second conjugation) verbs, Proto-Slavic *-iti causative verbs, most Ancient Greek -έω (-éō) contracted verbs, Sanskrit causative verbs in -अयति (-áyati).

From Proto-Indo-European *-éh₁-ti (stative verb suffix), which was athematic, but later rebuilt with the thematic present suffix *-yé-ti into *-h₁-yé-ti based on the past participle in *-h₁-to-s. Cognate with Latin stative verbs in -ēre (second conjugation).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-ye-ti, *-yeti, *-yé-ti, *-yéti, *-éh₁-ti, *-éh₁ti, *-éye-ti, *-éyeti

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/(i).jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Suffix

1. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Derives verbs from zero-grade roots
2. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Derives denominals from nouns
   - _dōmaz m (“judgement”) + *-janą → *dōmijaną (“to judge”)_
   - _namô n (“name”) + *-janą → *namnijaną (“to name”)_
3. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Derives factitives from adjectives.
   - _daudaz (“dead”) + *-janą → *daudijaną (“to kill”)_
   - _hailaz (“whole”) + *-janą → *hailijaną (“to heal”)_
4. _(morpheme, reconstruction)_ Derives causatives from basic strong verbs, with a sense of 'cause to do (the action of the verb)'. The stem vowel of the derived verb becomes that of the singular past tense, and the final consonant becomes the voiced Verner alternant.
   - _drinkaną (“to drink”) + *-janą → *drankijaną (“to give to drink”)_
   - _grētaną (“to weep”) + *-janą → *grōtijaną (“to make weep”)_

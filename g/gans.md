## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *gans

### Etymology

Inherited from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰh₂éns (“goose”). Compare Albanian gatë, Ancient Greek χήν (khḗn), Avestan 𐬰𐬁 (zā), Irish gé, Latin ānser, Latvian zùoss, Russian гусь (gusʹ), and Sanskrit हंस (haṃsá). Proto-Slavic *gǫ̑sь, however, does not exhibit the regular satem development as Baltic does and is therefore better explained as a Germanic loan, or at least influenced by the Germanic cognate.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ǵʰh₂éns

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ɣɑns/`

### Noun

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *grindaną

### Etymology

From either Proto-Indo-European *gʰrendʰ- (“to grind”) or Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰrendʰ- (“to crush; gnash”); in the latter case, an earlier and intermediate form would be *gwrendaną.
Cognate with Latin frendō (“to grind; gnash”), Lithuanian grę́sti (“to plane, scour”), and Albanian grind (“to fight”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰrendʰ-, *gʷʰrendʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣrin.dɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

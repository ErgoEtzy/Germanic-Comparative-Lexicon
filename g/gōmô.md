# *gōmô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/g%C5%8Dm%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *gōmô

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰeh₂- (“to yawn, gape”) via a -w- extension. Early Germanic -ōw- developed into -ō- only in open syllables, being shortened to -au- in closed syllables, resulting in the oblique form *gaumn-, whence the Old High German variant. Related to Lithuanian gomurỹs (“palate”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *ǵʰeh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣɔː.mɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ gum, palate

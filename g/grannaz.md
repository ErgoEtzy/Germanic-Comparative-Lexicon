## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *grannaz

### Etymology

From a Proto-Indo-European *gʰroH-n-uó-s, probably related to *granō (“awn, wispy hair”). An alternative theory links the word to Old Irish grind, grinn (“sharp”) (< *gʰrendʰ-i-), which would require the reconstruction of the Germanic as *gʰrondʰ-no-s instead.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰroH-n-uó-s

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ sharp
2. _(Germanic, North, reconstruction)_ thin

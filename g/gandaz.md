# *gandaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/gandaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *gandaz

### Etymology

Uncertain. The earliest attested use, in Proto-Norse ᚢᚾᚷᚨᚾᛞᛁᛉ (ungandiʀ, “unsorcered, not bewitched”), features the sense “black magic, witchcraft”; however, lots of descendants show a separate strong connection to wooden staffs, stems and branches etc. Due to middle-ground connotations like “magic wand” and “witch's familiar”, its hard to tell if there are two separate etymological roots (one for magic, one for staff), or one etymological root (staff → wand → witch's familiar → witchcraft / witchcraft → witch's familiar → wand → staff).
Various “magic” descendants revolve around “throwing” spells, thus if the original sense, then maybe from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰen- (“to beat, to slay”)?
If stemming from “staff”, then it may be derived or intermixed with a root word for wooden branch and stem etc, related to Welsh cang (“big branch”), Irish geagh (“tree-branch”), geagach (“branchy”), compared to descendants like Swedish gangel, gångel (“branch”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷʰen-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣɑn.dɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ unknown primary sense; based on descendants (if stemming from a singular root sense), one of:; black magic, witchcraft?
2. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ unknown primary sense; based on descendants (if stemming from a singular root sense), one of:; black magic, witchcraft?; a witch's familiar; evil, foul spirit?
3. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ unknown primary sense; based on descendants (if stemming from a singular root sense), one of:; staff, stem, branch?
4. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ unknown primary sense; based on descendants (if stemming from a singular root sense), one of:; staff, stem, branch?; a witch's magic wand?

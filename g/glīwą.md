# *glīwą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/gl%C4%ABw%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *glīwą

### Etymology

Of disputed origin:
* Beekes derives the word from Proto-Indo-European *gʰlew-o-s, from *gʰlew- (“to joke, make fun, enjoy”). In this case, cognate with Lithuanian glauda (“joke, jest”), Ancient Greek χλεύη (khleúē, “joke, jest, scorn”), Russian глуми́ться (glumítʹsja, “to mock, scoff at”), Serbo-Croatian глу́мити (“to act, feign”). See *glaumaz (“jubilation”) for more potential cognates.
* Kroonen derives the word from Proto-Indo-European *gʰley-wo-s, from a root *gʰley- (“to shine”), comparing Old Irish glé (“clear, bright”). Said root may be further related to Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰel- (“to shimmer, gleam”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰlew-o-s, *gʰley-wo-s, *ǵʰel-

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ joy, gladness, merriment, mirth

# *grīsaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/gr%C4%ABsan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *grīsaną

### Etymology

Of disputed origin. Watkins suggests Proto-Indo-European *gʰer- (“to grind, rub”), while Orel derives the term from Proto-Indo-European *gʰrey- (“to smear, paint”) and compares Ancient Greek χρῑ́ω (khrī́ō, “to smear, anoint”). However, compare Sanskrit हर्षति (harṣati, “to become on edge, nervous”), which is from *ǵʰers- (“surprised, stiff”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰer-, *gʰrey-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣriː.sɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to shudder, to shake
2. _(reconstruction)_ to be frightened, to be in awe

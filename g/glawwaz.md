## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *glawwaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *gʰlewH- (“clear, sharp”); cognate with Old Irish gluair (“clear, clean”) and perhaps *glūnēn- (“to peer”), whence dialectal Swedish gluna (“to stare, peer”) and Latvian glũnêt (“to lour”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰlewH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣlɑw.wɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ wise; prudent
2. _(reconstruction)_ wary; circumspect
3. _(reconstruction)_ perceptive
4. _(reconstruction)_ clever; smart
5. _(reconstruction)_ accurate; precise

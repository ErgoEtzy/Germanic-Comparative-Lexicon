# *grautaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/grautaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *grautaz

### Etymology

From pre-Germanic *gʰrowd-o-s, from Proto-Indo-European *gʰrewd-, *gʰer- (“to rub, grind, remove”). Akin to Proto-Slavic *gruda (“pile”), Proto-Slavic *grudьnъ (“crude, lousy”). Synchronically analyzable as a regular o-grade adjectival formation from *greutaną (“to grind, crush”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰer-, *gʰrewd-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣrɑu̯.tɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ coarse, crude
2. _(reconstruction)_ big, large

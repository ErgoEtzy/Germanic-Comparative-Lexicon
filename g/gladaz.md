# *gladaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/gladaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *gladaz

### Etymology

Uncertain.
* Perhaps from *gʰleh₂dʰ- (“to shine”). In which case, the term woudl be cognate to Lithuanian žlėjà (“dusk”), Latin glaber (“smooth, hairless”), and Old Church Slavonic гладъкъ (gladŭkŭ, “smooth, even, sweet”).
* Alternatively, from *glōaną (“to glow”), from *gʰleh₁- (“to shine”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

No Proto-Indo-European ancestor is listed.

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣlɑ.ðɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ shining, shiny, radiant
2. _(reconstruction)_ bright, cheery, cheerful
3. _(reconstruction)_ glossy, sleek, smooth

# *gudą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/gud%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *gudą

### Etymology

Unknown. Traditionally derived from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰutóm (neuter past participle), either meaning “(that which is) invoked”, from *ǵʰewH- (“to call, to invoke”), or “libated, poured as part of a liquid offering”, from *ǵʰew- (“to pour”), perhaps via the earlier meaning of “[libation made to an] idol” or “spirit immanent in a burial mound”. However, the derivation from *ǵʰewH- would normally result in long ū, and the root *ǵʰew- only occurs in Germanic in the extended form *ǵʰewd-.
Another hypothesis is a derivation from Proto-Indo-European *gʷʰutóm from the root *gʷʰew-., connected to Old Church Slavonic говѣти (gověti, “to revere”) (see Proto-Slavic *gověti). A non-Indo-European origin has also been proposed.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷʰutóm, *gʷʰutós, *ǵʰutóm, *ǵʰutós

### Pronunciation

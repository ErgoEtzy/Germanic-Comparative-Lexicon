# *grubaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/grubaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *grubaz

### Etymology

Origin uncertain. According to Kroonen, from Pre-Germanic *gʰrubʰos, from Proto-Indo-European *gʰrewbʰ- (“to grind”); see *greupaną (“to cut up, grind”) for more. Compare also Lithuanian grubus (“rough”), Serbo-Croatian grȗb (“rough, coarse”).
Alternatively from *ga- + *hrubaz, related to Proto-Germanic *hreubaz (“scabby, rough”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰrewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣru.βɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ coarse, rough

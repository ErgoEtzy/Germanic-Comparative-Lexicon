## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *granō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *gʰroH-néh₂-, with shortening due to Dybo's law, likely related to Proto-Slavic *grana (Serbo-Croatian grana (“branch”)). Possibly also related to Middle Irish grend (“beard, bristles”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰroH-néh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣrɑ.nɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ awn
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ hair resembling an awn, a bunch or bundle of wispy hair
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ bristles, beard hair, whiskers
4. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ moustache

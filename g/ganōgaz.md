# *ganōgaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/gan%C5%8Dgaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ganōgaz

### Etymology

From *ga- + *nōgaz, the latter a lengthened-grade adjective formed from *nuganą (whence *ganuganą (“to suffice”)), although *nōgaz has no attested descendants. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eh₂nóḱe (“he has reached, attained”), perfective of *h₂neḱ- (“to reach”). Cognate with Sanskrit अनाश (anā́śa).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *h₂eh₂nóḱe, *h₂neḱ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ɣɑˈnɔː.ɣɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ enough, sufficient

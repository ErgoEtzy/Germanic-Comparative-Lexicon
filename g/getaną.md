# *getaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/getan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *getaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *gʰe(n)d- (“to take, seize”). Cognate with Latin prehendō (“snatch, seize”), Ancient Greek χανδάνω (khandánō, “hold, contain”). The original meaning appears to have been "to seize an opportunity" or "to find a way".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰe(n)d-, *gʰed-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɣe.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to find or make a way
2. _(reconstruction)_ to attain, acquire, get, receive, hold

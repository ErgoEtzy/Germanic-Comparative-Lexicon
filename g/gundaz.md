## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *gundaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *gʰendʰ- (“sore, ulcer, abscess, boil”). Cognate with Ancient Greek κανθύλη (kanthúlē, “swelling”). See also Middle Persian gwnd (/⁠gund⁠/).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʰendʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ɣun.dɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ sore, boil; canker
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ purulent matter, atter, pus

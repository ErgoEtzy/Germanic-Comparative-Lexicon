# *makô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/mak%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *makô

### Etymology

An old n-stem as evidenced by the Kluge's law variant *makkô, perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *móg-ō, from *meg-, *mog- (“to knead, work”) (alternatively *mag-, *meh₂ǵ-, *meh₂ḱ-; various sets of cognates and meanings suggested). In any case from the same root as *makaz (“fitting, suitable, convenient, comfortable”); see there for the root reconstruction.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *móg-ō

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑ.kɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ an equal; comrade; mate; match

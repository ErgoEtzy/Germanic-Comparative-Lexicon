# *maltą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/malt%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *maltą

### Etymology

Uncertain, from *maltaz (“soft, gone bad”), or directly from *meltaną; ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *meld- (“to beat, crush, grind”), also the root of *mildijaz. Not few voices (Uhlenbeck, Bernecker, Brückner, Kiparsky, Skok and others) deem the word instead regularly formed in Proto-Slavic *molto, which you may see, from *melti (“to grind”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)meld-, *meld-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑl.tɑ̃/`

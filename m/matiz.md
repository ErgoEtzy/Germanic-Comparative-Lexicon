## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *matiz

### Etymology

An ablaut variant of the root *mēs-, mōs, usually derived from Proto-Indo-European *meh₂d- (“to drip, to ooze; grease, fat”). Contrary to Pokorny and followers, Kroonen separates the Germanic food words from this root, instead linking Ancient Greek μεστός (mestós, “full”) for a root *med- (“to satiate”). Some speculate a link between this term and Proto-Indo-Iranian *mátsyas (“fish”) exists.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *meh₂d-

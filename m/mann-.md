[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/mann-)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *mann-

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *mon- (“man”) or *men- (“to mind; to stay”). Alternatively, Kroonen, following Berneker (apud Kluge), favors the word splitting off from Proto-Indo-European *(dʰ)ǵʰmō, *(dʰ)ǵʰmon- (“human”) in the cases where the -m- wasn't syllabic (which otherwise gave *gum-, see *gumô); since the initial cluster would have been unpronounceable in Germanic, the result would be a reduced *(-)man-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(dʰ)ǵʰmō, *(dʰ)ǵʰmon-, *mon-, *ǵʰmṓ

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ human, human being, person, individual
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ man
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ name of the rune ᛗ (m)

# *mēkijaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/m%C4%93kijaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *mēkijaz

### Etymology

Of unclear origin.
"To cut" hypotheses reconstruct Proto-Indo-European *mēǵeyos (“cutter, sword”), from some root as *(s)meh₂- (“to beckon”) (> *meh₂y- > *meyh₂ > *mē(h₂)- > *mēǵ-), *(s)meh₁- (“to cut”), *(s)mēy(H)- (“to cut, hew”) and such. However, none of these are standard sound changes in the development of Proto-Germanic, and as such cannot be taken seriously as historical developments in the history of this word; contravention of the standard pillar of historical linguistics — the exceptionlessness of sound change — should only be done as a last resort.
Orel prefers taking the Germanic as a borrowing from some North Caucasian language, as represented by Proto-Northeast Caucasian *miqᶜV (“chisel, hoe, knife”), whence Avar *maqᶜala. See the potentially-related Proto-Slavic *mèčь (“sword”) for more theories.
Some reconstruct *mēǵ/g-yos and connect it with Proto-Germanic *mekaną (“to support”) as "secondary functional o-grade".[²]

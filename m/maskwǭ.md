# *maskwǭ

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/maskw%C7%AD)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *maskwǭ

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *mosgʷ- (“netting, mesh, knot, loop”), from *mesgʷ- (“to knit, tie, make a loop”), alternatively reconstructed as *mosg-(w-), *mesg-(w-). Cognate with Lithuanian mègzti (“to make a knot, knit”), mãzgas (“knot”), Latvian megzt, Tocharian A masäk (“joint”), as well as perhaps Proto-Slavic *mězgyrь (“tarantula”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *mesgʷ-, *mosgo-, *mosgʷ-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ loop, knot
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ mesh, netting
3. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ mesh used as a filter, facemask

# *mēlą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/m%C4%93l%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *mēlą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *meHlom; in regards to the further root, Kroonen etymologically sifts out the senses, which appear to have been conflated in descendants, as follows:
* For the "point in time, meal" sense, from the same origin as either the "measurement" or "spot" senses below.
* For the "spot, mark" sense, from the root *melh₂- (“dark”), whence also Ancient Greek μέλᾱς (mélās, “dark”), Lithuanian mė́las (“blue”).
* For the "measurement" sense, from *meh₁- (“to measure”).
Compare also Latin semel (“once”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *meHlom, *meh₁-, *melh₂-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɛː.lɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ time, occasion
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ point in time, period of time
3. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ time for eating, meal
4. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ spot, mark
5. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ measure, measurement

# *masuraz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/masuraz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *masuraz

### Etymology

Cognate with Proto-Slavic *mozolь (“bruise”). From *mas-, *mēs- (“spot, sore, scar”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mos- (“skin sore”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *mos-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑ.su.rɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a type of veined or spotted wood, such as maple or walnut
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ burnt wood
3. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ a scar or knot in wood; knarl

# *markō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/mark%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *markō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *mórǵs (“edge, boundary, border”), possibly from *merǵ- (“to divide”). Cognate with Persian مَرْز (marz, “boundary, frontier”), Latin margō (“border, edge”), Proto-Celtic *mrogis (“region, country”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *mórǵs

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑr.kɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ border, boundary
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ region, area

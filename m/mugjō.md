# *mugjō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/mugj%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *mugjō

### Etymology

From *mu- (from earlier *muwī, continuing Proto-Indo-European *muH-íh₂, *muH-iéh₂-s-, the short u a result of Dybo's law) + *-gjō (diminutive suffix). The western Germanic variants underwent a different velarization than *mują, though both are from the same ultimate source. Cognates include Latin musca (“fly (insect)”), Lithuanian mùsė (“fly”), Proto-Slavic *mùxa (“fly”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *muH-íh₂

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmuɣ.jɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, West, feminine, reconstruction)_ little fly, midge, mosquito

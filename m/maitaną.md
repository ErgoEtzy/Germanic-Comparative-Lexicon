# *maitaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/maitan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *maitaną

### Etymology

Traditinally derived from Proto-Indo-European *mey- (“small”) or *meh₂y- (“to cut”). Compare Proto-Celtic *mailos (“bald”).
However, in view of the likely causative *maidijaną (“to hurt”) (see there for further origin), Kroonen assumes the original form of this verb was *maiþaną, from Pre-Germanic *moitH-, with contamination from the related *mittōną (“to cut”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *meh₂y-, *mey-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑi̯.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to cut, hew
2. _(reconstruction)_ to cut off, sever

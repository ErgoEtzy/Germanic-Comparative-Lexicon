## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *malskaz

### Etymology

From earlier *malhskaz, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)mel- (“to weaken, soften, crush, grind”), equivalent to *malh- + *-skaz. Cognate with Ancient Greek μαλακός (malakós, “soft, weak”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)mel-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑl.skɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ soft, tender
2. _(reconstruction)_ touchy, sensitive
3. _(reconstruction)_ high-spirited; haughty, overconfident

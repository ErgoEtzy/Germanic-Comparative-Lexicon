## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *mainijaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *moyn-éye-ti (“to intend, give opinion”), cognate with Proto-Slavic *měniti (“to think”), a causative formation to a root *meyn-. Cognate with Old Irish mían (“wish; desire”). Ultimately possibly from Proto-Indo-European *mey- (“to change, exchange”). Equivalent to *mainō (“mind, opinion”) + *-janą.

Likely from *mainaz (“damaging, untrue, deceiving”) + *-janą or *mainą (“damage, unjustice”) + *-janą.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *mey-, *moyn-éye-ti

### Pronunciation

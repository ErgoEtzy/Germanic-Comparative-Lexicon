# *mōsą

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/m%C5%8Ds%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *mōsą

### Etymology

An ablaut variant of the Germanic root *mat- (“food”), usually derived from Proto-Indo-European *meh₂d- (“wet, liquid, fat, dripping”); compare *matjaną (“to lap up, eat”). Contrary to Pokorny and followers, Kroonen separates the Germanic food words from this root, instead linking Ancient Greek μεστός (mestós, “full”) for a root *med- (“to satiate”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *meh₂d-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɔː.sɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ wet food, mush, porridge
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ food, victuals

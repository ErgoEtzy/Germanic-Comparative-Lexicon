# *marzijaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/marzijan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *marzijaną

### Etymology

Causative form of an unattested strong verb *mersaną, from Proto-Indo-European *mers- (“to annoy, disturb, neglect, forget, ignore”). Related to Hittite [script needed] (maršant-, “deceitful, false, duplicitous”), Lithuanian miršti (“to forget, lose, become oblivious, die”), Armenian մոռանալ (moṙanal, “to forget, fail”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *mers-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑr.zi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to disturb, hinder, impede
2. _(reconstruction)_ to forget, neglect

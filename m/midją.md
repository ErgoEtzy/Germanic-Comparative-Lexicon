# *midją

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/midj%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *midją

### Etymology

Traditionally taken as a derivative of *midjaz (“middle”). In addition to this theory, Kroonen proposes an alternative derivation from a Proto-Indo-European *met-yo-, from a *met- (“to look, aim”) (which he considers separate from the "cut, measure" sense), comparing Lithuanian matýti (“to look, watch”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *met-yo-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmid.jɑ̃/`

### Noun

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *managaz

### Etymology

Likely from a substrate language of northwestern Europe. Proto-Celtic *menekkis (“frequent”), Proto-Slavic *mъnogъ (“many”), Proto-Finno-Permic *mone (“many, some”) are probably from the same source; according to Watkins, the Germanic, Slavic, and Celtic terms could all stem from a late (Northwestern) Proto-Indo-European *monogʰos., from *mengʰ-, *menegʰ- (“many, sufficient”). Older theories instead derive this term from Proto-Indo-European *méǵh₂- (“large”) with a nasal infix.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *monogʰos, *méǵh₂-, *méǵh₂s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈmɑ.nɑ.ɣɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ many
2. _(reconstruction)_ much (large amount of)

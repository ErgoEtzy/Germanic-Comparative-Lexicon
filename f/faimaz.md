## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *faimaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)poHy-m-os, from *(s)poH(y)- (“foam”). Cognate with Latin spūma (“foam”), Latin pūmex (“pumice”), Sanskrit फेन (phéna, “foam”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *(s)poH(y)-, *(s)poHy-m-os

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸɑi̯.mɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ foam

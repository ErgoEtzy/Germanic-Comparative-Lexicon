# *furhō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/furh%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *furhō

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *perk⁽ʷ⁾- (“oak”), probably a root noun *pérk⁽ʷ⁾s which was thematicized with the feminine suffix *-eh₂ (or later with the Proto-Germanic equivalent, *-ō).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *perkʷ-, *perk⁽ʷ⁾-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ fir, pine
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ forest (of fir or pine trees)

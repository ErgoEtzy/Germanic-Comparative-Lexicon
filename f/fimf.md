## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fimf

### Etymology

From Pre-Germanic *pémpe, with an irregular consonant change of the second *p from Proto-Indo-European *pénkʷe (“five”), perhaps influenced by the initial *p plus a resonant vowel preceding it; the expected form would have been **finhw. Possibly related to Proto-Germanic *funstiz (“fist”) and *fingraz (“finger”).

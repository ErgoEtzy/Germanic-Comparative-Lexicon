# *flōkaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/fl%C5%8Dkan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *flōkaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *pleh₂g- or *pleh₂k- (“to beat, strike”). Kroonen argues there is no need to reconstruct *pleh₂g- in Proto-Indo-European, in which case the 'k' was restored to *flōk- by analogy with the iterative derived from it. Cognate with Ancient Greek πλήσσω (plḗssō, “to strike”), Latin plangō (“to strike”), Old Church Slavonic плакати (plakati, “to beat one's breast; to lament”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *pleh₂g-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸlɔː.kɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to beat, strike
2. _(reconstruction)_ to lament

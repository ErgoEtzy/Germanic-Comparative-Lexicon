# *frustaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/frustaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *frustaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *prustós (“frost”), from the root *prews- (“to freeze>”) + *-tós. Synchronically analyzable as a nominal formation from *freusaną (“to freeze”). Cognate with Latin pruīna (“hoarfrost, frost”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-tós, *prews-, *prustós

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸrus.tɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ cold; frost; freezing weather
2. _(masculine, possibly, reconstruction)_ frost; rime (minute ice crystals)

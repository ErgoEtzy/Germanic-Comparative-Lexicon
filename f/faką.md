## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *faką

### Etymology

Via pre-Germanic *ph₂ǵóm, from Proto-Indo-European *peh₂ǵ- (“to fit, fasten, fix”). Cognate with Sanskrit पाजस् (pā́jas, “surface, side”), Latin pāgus (“division, district”) and pangō (“fasten, fix”, verb). The root is otherwise absent from Germanic, but see *fastuz.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *peh₂ǵ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸɑ.kɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, West, neuter, reconstruction)_ An enclosed or defined space; division; compartment; department
2. _(Germanic, West, neuter, reconstruction)_ Bounded space of time; period; interval

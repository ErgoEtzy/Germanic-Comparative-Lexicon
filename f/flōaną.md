## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *flōaną

### Etymology

Possibly from a pre-form *plōw-, the lengthened o-grade form of Proto-Indo-European *plew- (“to flow, swim”) or perhaps *pleh₃(w)-. Alternatively, from *pleh₃-,
Kroonen explains the alternation between -w- (in Anglo-Saxon) and -j- (in Low German) as a result of hiatus after the regular loss of *u after *ō.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *plew-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸlɔː.ɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to flow

# *fernaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/fernaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fernaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *per(H)- (“fore, first”) or *per- (“forth, over, across, through”), apparently with the same n-suffix or postfix found in the cognates Lithuanian per-nai (“last year”, adverb) and Latvian pēr-n (“last year, last time”) (see also Proto-Slavic *ol-ni (“last year”, adverb)). The identity of the suffix is uncertain, but possibly from *h₁én (“in”). See Proto-Germanic *-anē and *fanē for another possible connection.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *per(H)-, *per-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸer.nɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ foregoing, previous
2. _(reconstruction)_ recent, last-year

# *fimbulaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/fimbulaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fimbulaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *pamp-, *bamb- (“to inflate, swell”), a root of apparently sound-symbolic origin, referring to the inflated size of giants. Cognate with Lithuanian pamplỹs (“ball-shaped lump”), Latvian pàmpt, pempt, pumpt (“to swell”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *pamp-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸim.bu.lɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ based on descendants: distinguishing something large, considerable
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ based on descendants: giant; monster

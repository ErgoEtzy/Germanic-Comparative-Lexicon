## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *faimô

### Etymology

Uncertain; suggested to be from Proto-Indo-European *póyH-mn̥ or *péHi-mn̥ (“suckling”), from Proto-Indo-European *peyH- (“milk; fat”) (whence Avestan 𐬞𐬀𐬉𐬨𐬀𐬥 (paēman, “mother's milk”), Proto-Germanic *faitaz (“fat”)), compare semantics of Proto-Slavic *děva (“maiden, virgin”), Latin fēmina (“woman”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁(y)- (“to suckle milk”). Alternatively, cognate with Ancient Greek ποιμήν (poimḗn, “shepherd, herdsman”) (according to this view, the meaning was 'shepherd', whereas the derived term *faimnijǭ meant 'female shepherd' and eventually simply 'girl').

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰeh₁(y)-, *peyH-, *póyH-mn̥ or *péHi-mn̥

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ maiden, girl

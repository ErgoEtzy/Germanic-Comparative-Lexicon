# *fleuhaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/fleuhan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fleuhaną

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *plewk-, enlargement of *plew- (“flow”), assuming that the þ- in Gothic 𐌸𐌻𐌹𐌿𐌷𐌰𐌽 (þliuhan) was secondary (with sound change *fl- > þl-). Kroonen instead believes that the Gothic þ- was primary; if that was the case *fleuganą would have influenced its replacement by f- elsewhere in Germanic. He assigns no etymology.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *plewk-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸleu̯.xɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to flee
2. _(reconstruction)_ to escape

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fehtaną

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *peḱ- (“to pluck, ruffle, tousle, shear”), with semantic shift "pluck hair" > "struggle" > "fight". Cognate with Latin pectō (“to comb, thrash”).
Compare typologically драть (dratʹ), дра́ться (drátʹsja).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *peḱ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸex.tɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to comb, detangle
2. _(reconstruction)_ to struggle (with), fight
3. _(reconstruction)_ to shear

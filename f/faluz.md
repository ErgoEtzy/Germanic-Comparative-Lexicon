# *faluz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/faluz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *faluz

### Etymology

Unknown. From the same root as *fēlaz (“terrifying”) (which see for more detail), possibly Proto-Indo-European *pelh₁- (“to fear”), found also in Proto-Slavic *polšiti (“to scare”), *pȏlxъ (“afraid; fearful, timid”) and Old Norse fæla (“to frighten”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *pelh₁-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸɑ.luz/`

### Adjective

1. _(Germanic, West, reconstruction)_ terrible; cruel; bad; baleful

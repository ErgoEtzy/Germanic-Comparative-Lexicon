# *falisaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/falisaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *falisaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *pels-, *pelis- (“rock, cliff”), or perhaps an s-stem *pél-es-. Compare Old Irish ail (“boulder, rock”), Proto-Celtic *ɸallom (“cliff”), Sanskrit पाषाण (pāṣāṇa, “stone, rock”) and Pashto پرښه (parṣ̌a). Kroonen suggests reconstructing an acrostatic *pól-s ~ *pél-s-s, with o-grade based especially on the Old French borrowing falise.
Alternatively from a substrate language; more at πέλλα (pélla).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *pels-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸɑ.li.sɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, West, masculine, reconstruction)_ rock, cliff
2. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ mountain

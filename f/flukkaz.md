# *flukkaz

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/flukkaz)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *flukkaz

### Etymology

Probably originally used of birds, in which case a derivation from *flukkōną (“to fly, flutter”) is possible, with the original sense as "a group of flying animals" > "general group of mobile creatures".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *plewk-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸluk.kɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ crowd, troop, flock

# *fadurbanô

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/fadurban%C3%B4)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fadurbanô

### Etymology

From *fadēr + *banô, from Proto-Indo-European *ph₂tr̥-gʷʰon-ō, ultimately from *ph₂tḗr (“father”) + *gʷʰen- (“to strike, kill”). Cognate with Ancient Greek πατροφόνος (patrophónos), πατροφονεύς (patrophoneús), Sanskrit पितृहन् (pitṛhan).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *gʷʰen-, *ph₂tr̥-gʷʰon-ō, *ph₂tḗr

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸɑ.ður.ˌbɑ.nɔːː/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ father-slayer, patricide

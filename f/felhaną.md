# *felhaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/felhan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *felhaną

### Etymology

Unknown. Possibly:
* If the sense “to go, enter” is original, related to *fulgāną (“to follow”), for which Kroonen reconstructs a Germanic-only root *pelk- (cf. Kroonen (2013), p. 159, s.v. *fulgēn-).
* If “to conceal” is original, related to Old Prussian pelkis (“cloak”), from an extension *pel-k- of Proto-Indo-European *pel- (“to cover, wrap; skin, cloth”).
* A merger of two similar roots.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *pel-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸel.xɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to enter, to go inside
2. _(reconstruction)_ to put inside, to conceal, to hide, to bury

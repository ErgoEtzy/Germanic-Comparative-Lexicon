# *fērō

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/f%C4%93r%C5%8D)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fērō

### Etymology

From otherwise unattested *fēriz (“risky, dangerous”), vṛddhi gerundive of unattested *feraną (“to risk”), from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to go through”), + *-ō. Compare, in particular, Old Armenian փորձ (pʻorj, “attempt”), Ancient Greek πεῖρᾰ (peîră, “trial”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *per-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸɛː.rɔː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ danger

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *ferhwą

### Etymology

Probably derived from Proto-Germanic *ferhuz (“body; life; oak-tree”), itself from Proto-Indo-European *perkʷ- (“oak”), as oak trees symbolized life and vitality in Germanic mythology.
Old Norse fjǫrg (“gods”), from Proto-Germanic *fergō, could originally be the plural of this neuter (“vital forces”?), with analogical vocalism instead of the expected *furgō (with delabialization in this context).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *perkʷ-

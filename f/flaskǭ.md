## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *flaskǭ

### Etymology

Possibly from earlier *flahtskō, from Proto-Indo-European *pleḱ- (“to weave”). Orel alternatively compares the word to Lithuanian plókščias (“flat”), Proto-Slavic *ploskъ (“flat”), with these terms possibly also stemming from *pleḱ-.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *pleḱ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸlɑs.kɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ a plaiting-covered vessel
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ bottle, flask

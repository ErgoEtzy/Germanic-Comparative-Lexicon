## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fruskaz

### Etymology

From pre-Proto-Germanic *prugʰskos, from Proto-Indo-European *prugʰ-ḱó-s, from *prewgʰ- (“to leap”) + *-ḱós (animal suffix), possibly extended from *prew- (“to jump”). Cognate with Proto-West Germanic *froggō (“frog”), Old Norse frauðr (“frog”); see *froggō for more.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-ḱós, *prewgʰ-, *prugʰ-ḱó-s

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ frog

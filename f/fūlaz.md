## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fūlaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *púH-lo-s, from *puH- (“to be rotten; putrid”). Cognate with Lithuanian pū́ti (“to rot”), Latin pūs (“foul, corrupt matter; pus”), Sanskrit पूयति (pūyati, “rots, smells”), Persian پوسیدن (pusidan, “to rot”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *puH-, *púH-lo-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈɸuː.lɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ foul, dirty
2. _(reconstruction)_ rotten

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *fōdrą

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *peh₂- (“to protect, guard; to graze, feed”) + *-dʰrom or *-tróm, the suffix equivalent to *-drą; or directly from the root of *fōdijaną (“to feed”) and *fōdô (“food”) (including the *d), from an extended form *peh₂-dʰ- or *peh₂-t- of the same PIE root, + *-r-om. Cognate with Latin pābulum (“food, fodder”) and possibly pānis (“bread, food”).

From Proto-Indo-European *peh₂- (“to protect, guard, graze, feed”) + *-dʰrom or *-tróm, the suffix equivalent to *-drą; ultimately of the same origin as the "fodder" sense of Etymology 1, via a different semantic evolution from the basic sense of "to protect" rather than "to make protected" > "to feed".

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-dʰrom, *-r̥, *-trom, *peh₂-

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dwalaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *dʰwel- (“to dim, dull, cloud, make obscure, swirl, whirl”). Cognate with Latvian dulls (“crazy, mad”), Lithuanian dvylas (“dark, reddish brown”), and Proto-Celtic *dallos (“blind”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰwel-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdwɑ.lɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ confused, stunned
2. _(reconstruction)_ foolish, misled, mad

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dunstą

### Etymology

Per Kroonen, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰuns-to-, from a nasal-infix zero-grade of *dʰwes- (“dust, spirit”), and cognate with Tocharian B tänts- (“to scatter”), Sanskrit ध्वंसति (dhváṁsati, “he falls to dust”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰuns-to-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdun.stɑ̃/`

### Noun

1. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ mist, haze
2. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ evaporation
3. _(neuter, reconstruction)_ dust

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dulaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *dʰul-o-s, a zero-grade nominal formation from *dʰwel(H)- (“to err, go awry, make obscure”). Synchronically analyzed as from an earlier *dwulaz, zero-grade form of *dwalaz. Related to Old Irish dall (“blind”), Welsh dall (“idem”), Latvian dulls (“crazy, furious”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰul-o-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdu.lɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ confused, stunned
2. _(reconstruction)_ foolish, misled, mad

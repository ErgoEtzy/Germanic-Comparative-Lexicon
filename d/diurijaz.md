# *diurijaz

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *diurijaz

### Etymology

Unknown.
One suggested derivation is from Proto-Indo-European *dʰegʷʰ- (“to burn”), which assumes a semantic shift of “to heat up” > “warming” > “dear, cherished”. However, the semantic variation among descendants (see more at *diuriþō) may point to the original meaning having been “rare, scarce”. Alternatively, Guus Kroonen notes that this adjective is similar to Proto–West Germanic *dūrēn (“to grieve, regret, suffer”) (whence German dauern, Middle English douren), ultimately a loan from Latin dūrus (“tough, hard”), and may be a vṛddhi-gerundive thereof. In that case, the semantic development would have been 'mournable' > 'precious, expensive, dear'.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

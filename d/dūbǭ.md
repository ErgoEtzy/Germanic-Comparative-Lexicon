## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dūbǭ

### Etymology

Possibly from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewbʰ- (“to whisk; smoke; make obscure”). According to Kroonen, derived from Proto-Germanic *dūbaną (“to dive; to sink”), likely referring to the dove's characteristic divebombing flight.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈduː.βɔ̃ː/`

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ dove, pigeon

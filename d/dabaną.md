# *dabaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/daban%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dabaną

### Etymology

Kroonen derives the term from Proto-Indo-European *dʰóbʰ-e-, an o-grade present to a Balto-Germanic root *dʰebʰ- (“to fit”). The LIV, however, connects Gothic gadaban to a PIE root of the shape *dʰeHbʰ-, though it considers the Gothic term to be an innovative formation. Cognate with Lithuanian dabinti (“to beautify, decorate, adorn”), Russian до́брый (dóbryj, “good”). Kroonen explicitly rejects a comparison with Latin faber (“faber”) on account of the a-vocalism of the Latin term.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰóbʰ-e-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdɑ.βɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to come to pass, occur, happen
2. _(reconstruction)_ to fit, be fitting

# *drepaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/drepan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *drepaną

### Etymology

Due to the *p, which otherwise points to a dubious Proto-Indo-European *b, Kroonen assumes that ultimately the Germanic verb is a back-formation from the iterative *drappōną of *drabaną (“to hew”), which would be from a root *dʰrebʰ- (“to grind; crush; kill”), elsewhere attested only in Balto-Slavic. Cognate with Lithuanian drė̃bti (“to strike”), Latvian drêbt (“to beat”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *b

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdre.pɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to hit, strike

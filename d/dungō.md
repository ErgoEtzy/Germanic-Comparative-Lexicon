## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dungō

### Etymology

Unknown; perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *dʰḿ̥ǵʰ-, metathesized from *dʰǵʰḿ̥-, from *dʰéǵʰ-ōm ~ *dʰǵʰ-més (“earth”). Possibly cognate with Latvian dañga (“rut, corner, piece of land that is surrounded by water or marshlands on three sides, bay”); compare Proto-Germanic *dungijǭ (“pile, heap”). Often citied as being related to Proto-Germanic *dungz (“cellar”), from *dʰengʰ- (“to cover, hide, conceal”), however this seems semantically doubtful.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰḿ̥ǵʰ-

### Noun

1. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ earth, dirt
2. _(feminine, reconstruction)_ dung, manure

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *drunjaną

### Etymology

From *drunjuz (“sound”) + *-janą.
Per Vasmer, the above root *drunjuz is perhaps ultimately from an imitative Proto-Indo-European *dʰreh₁w- (“to drone”); see also Sanskrit ध्रणति (dhráṇati, “to sound”), Old Irish drésacht (“crackling, noise”), German trensen (“to make heavy sounds from the vocal cords”), Dutch drenzen (“to moan”), Ancient Greek θρῆνος (thrênos, “lamentation for the deceased”), English drone, Old Prussian droanse (“corncrake”), Gothic 𐌳𐍂𐌿𐌽𐌾𐌿𐍃 (drunjus, “sound”). This theory is implicitly accepted by Orel.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰreh₁w-

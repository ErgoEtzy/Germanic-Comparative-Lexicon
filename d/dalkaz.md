## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dalkaz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *dʰelg-, *dʰelk- (“to stick, prick, stab”). Cognate with Lithuanian dilgus (“prickly”), Latin falx (“hook, sickle”), Old Irish delg (“spine, needle”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰelg-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdɑl.kɑz/`

### Noun

1. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ pin, needle
2. _(masculine, reconstruction)_ the tongue of a buckle; clasp
3. _(Germanic, North, masculine, reconstruction)_ knife

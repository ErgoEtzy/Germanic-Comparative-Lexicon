## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dīsiz

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *dʰéh₁s (“god”). Also possible relation to Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁(y)- (“to suckle, nurse”) related to Russian деви́ца (devíca, “maiden, girl”).
Kroonen states the following:
“An etymologically problematic word. Some have compared OCS děva f.
'lady' < *dʰh₁-oi-ueh₂-, apparently derived from the stem *dʰh₁-oi- 'to suckle' (see *dīan-). This suggestion is perhaps supported by Elfd. disa w.v. 'to suckle' (whence dise f. 'nipple'?), although the origin of the s-suffix remains unclear. Within Germanic, *dīsi- can alternatively be linked to *edis- (q.v.), but this would imply that the word is of non-Indo-European origin.” He does not mention the Proto-Indo-European *dʰéh₁s (“god”) etymology. The term has also been connected with Proto-West Germanic *idisi.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰeh₁(y)-, *dʰéh₁s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdiː.siz/`

### Noun

1. _(Germanic, North, feminine, reconstruction)_ goddess
2. _(Germanic, North, feminine, reconstruction)_ demi-goddess
3. _(Germanic, North, feminine, reconstruction)_ noble or virtuous woman, virgin

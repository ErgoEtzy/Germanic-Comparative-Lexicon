## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dūbijaną

### Etymology

From *dūbǭ (“dove, pigeon”) + *-janą (“denominative suffix”).
According to Orel, derived from *dūbaną (“dive, sink”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-ye-ti, *-yeti, *-yé-ti, *-yéti, *-éh₁-ti, *-éh₁ti, *-éye-ti, *-éyeti, *dʰewbʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈduː.βi.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to submerse, dive

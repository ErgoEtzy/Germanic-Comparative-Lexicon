# *dwaljaną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/dwaljan%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dwaljaną

### Etymology

From *dwalaz (“confused, stunned, foolish, misled, mad”) + *-janą (factitive suffix), additionally conflated with *dwelaną (“to err”) + *-janą (causative suffix), both ultimately from the same origin.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**PIE form(s) resolved through Proto-Germanic components:** *-ye-ti, *-yeti, *-yé-ti, *-yéti, *-éh₁-ti, *-éh₁ti, *-éye-ti, *-éyeti, *dʰwel-, *dʰwelH-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdwɑl.jɑ.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to be confused, perplex
2. _(reconstruction)_ to hold up, delay

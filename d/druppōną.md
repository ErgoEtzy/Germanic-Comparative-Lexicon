# *druppōną

[Alphabetical index](../_Index.md) · [View on Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/drupp%C5%8Dn%C4%85)

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *druppōną

### Etymology

From earlier paradigm *druppōþi ~ *drubunanþi, from pre-Proto-Germanic *dʰrubʰ-nā́-ti ~ *dʰrubʰ-un-ánti, iterative from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrebʰ- (“to drop”). Possibly conflated with a denominative construction *drupô (“drop of liquid”) + *-ōną. Cognate with Lithuanian dri̇̀bti (“to drop”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰrebʰ-

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdrup.pɔː.nɑ̃/`

### Verb

1. _(reconstruction)_ to drop, to fall
2. _(reconstruction)_ to drip, to seep

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dumbaz

### Etymology

Probably from Proto-Indo-European *dʰm̥bʰ-o-s, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰembʰ- (“to be stunned”).
Alternatively, Kroonen takes the word as an adjectival derivative of *dimbaną (“to fog, smoke”), with semantic shift "foggy, hazy" > "dumb, mute", ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰemH- (“to smoke, blow”). However, Forte thinks this semantic development is implausible.

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *dʰemH-, *dʰembʰ-, *dʰm̥bʰ-o-s

### Pronunciation

- IPA: `/ˈdum.bɑz/`

### Adjective

1. _(reconstruction)_ dumb, mute, unable to speak

## Proto-Germanic

**Reconstruction:** *dilī

### Etymology

From Proto-Indo-European *dʰelh₁-íh₂s ~ *dʰl̥h₁-ih₂és, from *dʰelh₁- (“to flourish, grow”) + *-ih₂s. Cognate with Latin folium (“leaf”), Middle Irish duille (“leaf”), Welsh dalen (“leaf”), Ancient Greek θαλλός (thallós, “spout”), θάλλω (thállō, “to flourish, grow”), Old Armenian դալար (dalar, “verdant”), Albanian dal (“to sprout”).

### Proto-Indo-European ancestry

**Directly mentioned PIE form(s):** *-ih₂, *dʰelh₁-, *dʰelh₁-íh₂s ~ *dʰl̥h₁-ih₂és
